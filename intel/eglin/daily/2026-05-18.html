01 L3 · Action recommended Regulatory · State AI · Employment · Compliance

Colorado AI Act paused, then rewritten.

Two material moves in 12 days. Apr 27, 2026: a federal court paused enforcement of the original Colorado AI Act (SB 24-205), the country's most comprehensive state employment-AI law [2]. May 9, 2026: Colorado's legislature passed SB 26-189, which replaces the "high-risk AI system" and "algorithmic discrimination" framework with a narrower regime focused on automated decision-making technology (ADMT) that "materially influences" a "consequential decision" [1]. The amended law is expected to be signed and take effect 2027-01-01 [1]. Texas's TRAIGA went live 2026-01-01 with an intent-only discrimination standard — disparate impact alone does not violate [10] — and the EU AI-Act Omnibus has deferred high-risk employment-system obligations to 2027-12-02 [9].

Why this matters to Stratum

For 18 months, the statutory ceiling on AI-hiring obligation just dropped in three of the largest watch jurisdictions simultaneously. Buyers and prospects who held off in Q1 citing "AI Act risk" lose that hesitation. But: Mobley v. Workday (federal court, N.D. Cal., March 6 ruling that ADEA reaches applicants; March 30 amended complaint reasserting California state and disability claims [7][8]) is now the binding case-law constraint. The compliance story has inverted: less statutory pressure, more litigation risk — and the litigation is specifically against the SaaS vendor, not just the employer. Stratum's "eval-governed" claim is now the right answer to the right question; the framing has to update.

Recommended actions

  • HelmCompact statement on the regulatory inversion: less statutory pressure, more case-law pressure. Position Stratum's eval set + audit logs as Mobley-grade evidence, not as AI-Act-grade compliance theater. Draft this week.
  • CompassReplace any "EU AI Act–ready" copy with the case-law framing: "Audit-grade artifacts the day a plaintiff asks." Refresh the trust-center landing within 7 days.
  • ForgeConfirm Stratum's per-decision audit-log retention beats the Colorado SB 26-189 three-year minimum [1]. Confirm Texas TRAIGA's intent-only standard doesn't lower our internal eval bar.
  • VectorUpdate the customer Q&A: one paragraph each on Colorado (paused/rewritten), Texas (live, intent-only), EU (deferred to 2027-12-02), and the Mobley case-law angle.
02 L3 · Action recommended Competitive · Enterprise suite · Agentic AI · Distribution

SAP and Workday both shipped agent suites. Friday's brief missed both.

SAP Sapphire 2026 (May 12–13, Orlando): SAP unveiled the Autonomous Suite, more than 50 domain-specific Joule Assistants orchestrating 200+ specialized agents across finance, supply chain, procurement, HCM, and CX [3][4]. Nvidia and Anthropic were named as allies [4]. RISE customers get contractual activation of three Joule Assistants in year one. Workday (May 13): Sana Self-Service Agent shipped inside Microsoft 365 Copilot with 300+ skills for HR and finance — answering employee questions and taking action without leaving Outlook or Teams [5]. Workday Korea (May 14): regional rollout of the same agent platform [6]. Three suite-vendor launches in three days; this is the agentic-HCM platform race going wider than ATS-MCP.

Why this matters to Stratum

The contested word is no longer "MCP-native" — it is distribution surface. SAP's answer: the suite the buyer already runs. Workday's answer: Microsoft 365, where the employee already lives. Stratum's answer: the Console, plus four MCP servers that any client can call. All three are credible; the buyer's choice depends on where they want to start. Stratum's positioning needs the surface-question reframed honestly: customers who live in M365 or who already pay SAP/Workday will not switch consoles. They will adopt the agent that comes pre-wired. Stratum wins when the buyer's question is "which agent is most accurate and most auditable across systems we don't own" — not "which agent is most convenient inside the suite we already bought."

Recommended actions

  • ForgeAudit whether Stratum's four MCP servers are reachable from Microsoft 365 Copilot and the SAP Joule layer on the same auth model. If yes, document it on the trust center within 14 days. If no, scope the gap and decide.
  • CompassAdd a "distribution surface" paragraph to the buyer Q&A: Stratum is the agent that crosses systems the buyer does not own; suite-vendor agents are the agent that lives inside one. Position as complementary, not competitive.
  • KernelEval-set probe to add: "Agent invoked from a third-party surface (Copilot, Joule) preserves Stratum's decision-class taxonomy and audit log." Verify; do not assume.
  • TesseraTrack whether SAP's Joule UI or Workday's Sana-in-M365 establishes a UX convention Stratum's Console will increasingly be measured against.
03 L2 · Watch Ecosystem · Foundation models · Enterprise spend

Anthropic overtakes OpenAI in enterprise spend share.

Ramp's May 2026 AI Index shows 34.4% of tracked businesses now pay for Anthropic vs 32.3% for OpenAI — the first time Anthropic has led in enterprise adoption [11]. The same index reports Anthropic moved from ~9% of customers in May 2025 to 34.4% in April 2026 — roughly 26 points in 12 months. Separately, Anthropic shipped enterprise Agent Skills with stock plug-ins for HR (JD generation, onboarding materials, offer letters), finance, and legal [12].

Why this matters to Stratum

Stratum is built on Claude. The model bet looks more validated now than it did 90 days ago; enterprise sales conversations no longer require defending the foundation choice. Two cautions: (a) the same Anthropic HR plug-in covers the same use-case category Stratum sells against — JD generation, offer letters, onboarding — so the "AI for HR" baseline keeps rising and undifferentiated wrappers will compress; (b) spend-share leadership is volatile and reverses fast. This is L2 because it is a confidence-building signal, not an action-forcing one.

Recommended actions

  • CompassIn Stratum's "why Claude" page (if one exists; if not, build it), cite the May 2026 Ramp number — and pair it with our eval evidence so the answer is "model + methodology," not "model alone."
04 L2 · Watch Compliance · Litigation · AI hiring

Mobley v. Workday continues to escalate.

The Mobley v. Workday case has been the structural background story all year and is worth re-elevating now that statutory pressure is loosening. Mar 6, 2026: Judge Rita Lin ruled that ADEA disparate-impact reaches job applicants and rejected Workday's strongest dismissal argument [7]. Mar 30, 2026: plaintiffs filed an amended complaint reasserting California state and disability-discrimination claims [8]. Notice ongoing: the certified collective includes anyone age 40+ who applied through Workday's platform since 2020-09-24 and was scored, ranked, or screened by HiredScore AI features. Workday is required to provide its customers' lists for notice purposes.

Why this matters to Stratum

Mobley creates a precedent for naming the SaaS vendor alongside the employer in algorithmic-discrimination cases. With Colorado paused and the EU deferred, Mobley becomes the most actionable AI-hiring liability surface for the next 18 months. Stratum's per-decision audit log, eval-set bias probes, and the four-server architecture are defensive assets specifically against this precedent, not just for AI-Act compliance theater. Re-frame internally before re-framing externally.

Recommended actions

  • KernelAdd a Mobley-specific eval probe: "Disparate-impact monitor flags age-band imbalance ≥5pp across the candidate-recommendation surface." Ship in v1.2 or v1.3, whichever is next.
  • ForgeConfirm Stratum's incident-response runbook contemplates a plaintiff's discovery request (audit logs, eval-set version pinned to a hiring decision, customer-of-record). Tabletop within 30 days.
  • EglinPromote Mobley docket-watch to the standing radar; re-elevate on every ruling or major filing. Next checkpoint: Workday's response to the amended complaint.
