01 L3 · Action recommended AI · Recruiting · MCP · Competitive

Workable becomes the third ATS with MCP in six days.

Workable announced general availability of the Workable MCP Server on 2026-05-13 [1]. It ships with 38 MCP tools spanning jobs, candidates, pipeline stages, offers, requisitions, employees, time-tracking and time-off, and calendar events; supports Claude and ChatGPT clients out of the box; respects each authenticated user's role and job-assignment permissions; and is included at no additional cost across all subscription plans [1]. Workable cites a customer base of 6,200+ companies across 100+ countries.

Why this matters to Stratum

Three credible ATS vendors have now shipped MCP in six calendar days (Greenhouse and Ashby on May 7; Workable on May 13). That is a category move. Workable's free-bundling decision is the strongest signal — it implies the vendors believe MCP is a baseline, not a premium add-on. Stratum's "MCP-native" positioning still holds as architecture (four servers, agent-owned), but as a single-word claim it is now generic. The differentiation has to migrate to tool surface depth, governance evidence, and eval-verified behavior.

Recommended actions

  • ForgePublish Stratum's MCP tool count and scope inventory by Friday. Target a side-by-side: Stratum vs Greenhouse vs Ashby vs Workable, by tool count and capability domain. If we trail on count, scope the gap; if we lead on governance, document it.
  • CompassReplace any standalone "MCP-native" language in customer-facing copy with the three-part claim: "MCP-native, eval-governed, four-server-architecture." The single word is no longer differentiating.
  • KernelPull Workable's permission-scoping documentation. If their model is per-user role inheritance, our differentiation is the published eval set and AEDT-aligned bias probes — not the access model itself.
  • VectorRefresh the customer talking-point card from yesterday: add Workable to the "yes-they-have-MCP-too" list with the specific distinction we offer.
02 L2 · Watch Competitor · UX · Performance · AI governance

Lattice's AI Agent joins your 1:1s, drafts reviews from evidence.

Lattice's Spring/Summer 2026 release moves the Lattice AI Agent to a right-hand sidebar across the platform [2]. Two new capabilities are worth flagging: (a) the agent can join 1:1 meetings to capture notes and surface coaching insights; (b) Evidence-based AI Reviews generate first drafts grounded in 1:1 history, past reviews, feedback, and growth areas. Lattice explicitly states "managers remain accountable; Lattice AI will not submit reviews on their behalf" [2] — a governance posture, not just marketing.

Why this matters to Stratum

Two signals here. UX: in-product right-rail agents are converging across the category — Ashby Assistant, Lattice AI Agent, and our recruiter console will all be compared on the same interaction pattern. Tessera should treat this as the emerging buyer expectation. Governance: Lattice's "AI will not submit on the manager's behalf" line is the kind of plain-language, drawable-on-screen commitment that maps cleanly to our human-in-the-loop bar. We should be making the equivalent commitment, in the equivalent words, on the same surfaces.

03 L2 · Watch Regulatory · EU · AI · Calendar

EU AI Omnibus: formal vote calendar firms up — June/July 2026.

Following the 2026-05-07 political agreement on the Digital Omnibus, both the European Parliament and the Council are now expected to formally vote between June and July 2026 to adopt the text [3][4]. The proposed move of high-risk AI employment-system obligations from 2026-08-02 to 2027-12-02 remains in the package, but is not yet law. MEPs adopted their joint position on the Omnibus in March 2026; the May 7 political deal aligned Council and Parliament positions [4].

Why this matters to Stratum

This is a calendar update, not a posture change. Until the June/July vote, the legal status of the deferral is conditional. EU enterprise buyers will continue asking the AI Act question through the summer, and "what changes for us when the Omnibus passes" will become a recurring inbox item for Vector. The posture we set yesterday — ship to the high-risk bar regardless of the deferral — remains the right answer; this brief just adds dates to it.

04 L2 · Watch Compliance · Pay transparency · California

California pay-data report deadline just passed; penalties now mandatory.

California's annual pay-data report was due 2026-05-12 [5]. Two enforcement shifts matter: (a) penalties for failure to file are now mandatory upon CRD request, no longer subject to court discretion — $100 per employee for first-time violations, $200 for subsequent [5]; (b) the "pay range" definition has tightened to require actual expected compensation, not broad placeholder bands. Massachusetts and New Jersey are running active audits in parallel [5].

Why this matters to Stratum

Job-posting generation and offer-letter surfaces are within scope when our customers operate in California, Massachusetts, or New Jersey. The mandatory-penalty shift turns "did our system produce a compliant posting" from a customer-curiosity question into a vendor-selection criterion that buyers will weight in 2026 RFPs. Not a roadmap intervention yet; a backlog confirmation and a buyer-conversation talking point.

Recommended actions

  • ForgeConfirm posting-generation and offer-letter surfaces enforce per-state range-format rules. If gaps exist, add to the backlog with mandatory-penalty context noted.
  • CompassAdd to the buyer Q&A: "What does Stratum do when a generated posting would violate state pay-transparency law?" — answer should be concrete.
