02 L3 · Action recommended Regulatory · EU · AI · Employment

EU AI Act: political agreement on the AI Omnibus deferral.

A political agreement was reached on 2026-05-07 on the AI omnibus legislative proposal. High-risk AI obligations for employment systems — originally enforceable from 2 August 2026 — are now proposed to apply from 2 December 2027, contingent on the Omnibus being formally adopted [3][4]. Finland already conferred enforcement powers on its market surveillance authority under Article 99 in January 2026; other member states are following [4].

Why this matters to Stratum

EU buyers will continue to ask the AI Act question — the deferral does not eliminate the obligation, it shifts the enforcement date by 16 months. Stratum's compliance posture remains a differentiator, especially against US-headquartered competitors who may interpret the deferral as a reason to slow their compliance work. We should not.

Fact vs claim

Verified facts

  • Political agreement reached 2026-05-07 on AI Omnibus deferral [3]
  • New target enforcement date for high-risk employment: 2 December 2027 [3][4]
  • Finland confers Article 99 powers January 2026 [4]
  • Enforcement is national, not EU-central

Open / unverified

03 L3 · Action recommended Regulatory · NYC · AEDT · Bias audit

NYC AEDT: enforcement is about to get serious.

A NY State Comptroller audit published 2025-12-02 found DCWP enforcement of Local Law 144 from July 2023 to June 2025 was "ineffective" — 75% of 311 complaint calls were misrouted; DCWP identified 1 violation across 32 surveyed companies where Comptroller auditors identified 17 [5][6]. DCWP has committed to a substantially more rigorous 2026 enforcement approach: better complaint handling, cross-trained staff, and proactive (not complaint-driven) investigations. Major employment law firms (DLA Piper among them) advise employers to expect a stricter enforcement phase in 2026 [6].

Why this matters to Stratum

Stratum's recruiter product touches AEDT scope. Even though we are pre-revenue in NYC, our public commitments around bias audits, eval-set transparency, and human-in-the-loop hiring decisions are about to become a marketable differentiator as NYC starts visibly enforcing. Greenhouse and Ashby just shipped MCP; neither has publicly visible bias-audit infrastructure. Closing that gap is a free message for us.

Recommended actions

  • ForgeDocument Stratum's current AEDT posture: which features touch scope, what audits we run, what we publish. Internal first, public-ready in 30 days.
  • KernelEnsure the eval set has explicit AEDT-aligned bias probes. Document them.
  • CompassAdd a compliance-posture line to the customer-facing comparison: "Our bias audits are published. Ask competitors for theirs."
04 L2 · Watch Compliance · Pay transparency · State law

Pay-transparency: enforcement, not new jurisdictions.

The 2026 pay-transparency story is no longer about new states — 16 states plus DC are now covered, with ~60M workers under disclosure rules — it is about aggressive enforcement. Massachusetts and New Jersey moved from policy to active audits and penalties in 2026. New York's 2024–2025 investigations identified thousands of non-compliant postings. California tightened the definition of "pay range" to require actual expected compensation, not broad placeholders. Oregon now requires detailed payroll explanations at hire (effective 2026-01-01) [7][8].

Why this matters to Stratum

Our compensation-management surfaces (compa-ratio, pay-band configuration, market benchmarking) are within scope when they generate job postings or offer letters. The enforcement shift makes "did our system produce a non-compliant posting" a recurring customer support question and a defensible vendor-selection criterion. Recommend monitoring; not a roadmap intervention yet.

Recommended actions

  • ForgeConfirm whether posting-generation and offer-letter surfaces enforce range-format rules per state. If not, add to backlog.
  • CompassStock a "what does your system do when a posting violates pay-transparency law" answer for buyer conversations.
