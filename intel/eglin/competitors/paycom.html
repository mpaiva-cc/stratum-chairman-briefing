00 · Headline read

Where Stratum can credibly win.

Paycom's distinguishing product is Beti (Better Employee Transaction Interface) — employees verify and approve their own paychecks before payday, reducing payroll corrections by 80%+ on average [1][2]. This is a genuine product innovation; Paylocity has no equivalent self-service payroll feature [1]. IWant is the command-driven AI engine that pulls data from Paycom's single database for real-time results [1][2]. Single-database architecture is the platform-level commitment.

Paycom's challenge is the buyer-experience tax: bundled pricing with mandatory 15-30% implementation fees [1], rigid configurability ("doesn't bend if you want to do things your own way"), and mixed post-launch support [2]. Per Yahoo Finance coverage Beti is creating "AI jitters" investor attention — buyers are increasingly asking whether self-service payroll is a moat or a feature [2].

Partnership-shaped for Stratum, not displacement. Stratum Recruiter + Paycom payroll-of-record via integration is the realistic motion.

01 · Company overview

Self-service-first HCM, single-database architecture.

Paycom was founded in 1998 in Oklahoma City by Chad Richison (still CEO). Publicly traded (NYSE: PAYC). The distinguishing product commitment is single-database architecture — all HCM modules sit on one underlying database, supporting the self-service-first product philosophy (Beti and IWant being the headline examples).

02 · Positioning

"Employees own their data" — self-service-first.

Paycom positions employee self-service as the operational model: employees verify their own paychecks (Beti), update their own information, query their own data via IWant. This reduces administrative load on HR teams and reduces payroll errors. It also reduces HR-team agency in cases where the employee model doesn't fit.

03 · Target customers

Mid-market US, technology-comfortable workforce.

  • Core ICP: Mid-market (100-2,500 employees), US-only, professional services, tech, healthcare-adjacent.
  • Geography: US-only payroll.
  • Not their fit: Workforces less comfortable with employee self-service; organizations wanting customization-heavy workflows.
04 · Product strengths

What Paycom actually does well.

  • Beti. Genuine product innovation; 80%+ payroll-correction reduction is a real operational outcome [1][2].
  • Single-database architecture. All modules on one database; data consistency advantage.
  • IWant AI engine. Command-driven data access including voice [1].
  • Compliance tools. Statutory updates and regulatory automation [2].
