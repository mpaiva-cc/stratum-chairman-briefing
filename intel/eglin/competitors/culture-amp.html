00 · Headline read

Where Stratum can credibly win.

Culture Amp is the engagement-survey category leader. The product is built around continuously-collected employee feedback, survey design, sentiment analysis, and benchmarking against a corpus of 1B+ survey responses [1][2]. AI at Culture Amp is survey-analytics focused — surfacing themes in open-ended responses, flagging sentiment patterns, offering a coaching chatbot — not agentic. The conservative AI posture fits the buyer who values benchmarking integrity over experimental features.

This is not a direct Stratum competitor. Culture Amp owns engagement and survey analytics; Stratum's recruiting + people-graph posture is complementary. Build the integration: Stratum Recruiter brings new hires into Culture Amp engagement-tracking through the people-graph. The realistic 2026 motion is partnership, not displacement.

01 · Company overview

The engagement-survey category.

Culture Amp was founded in 2011 in Melbourne by Didier Elzinga, Doug English, and others; it now has dual HQs in Melbourne and San Francisco. The company has raised more than $250M, with notable investments from Index Ventures, Sequoia, Felicis. ~6,500 customers globally; strong international footprint relative to US-only competitors. The core product surface is engagement surveys, employee experience analytics, performance management (lighter than Lattice's), and the benchmarking database that spans 1B+ survey responses across customers [1][2].

02 · Positioning

"The employee experience platform" — benchmarking-led.

Culture Amp positions on the strength of its benchmarking corpus: customers can compare their engagement scores to similar organizations because Culture Amp has the comparable data. This is a real moat — survey-data network effects compound across years.

03 · Target customers

Mid-market to enterprise, engagement-led buyer.

  • Core ICP: Mid-market to enterprise (200-10,000+ employees), People Ops or Chief People Officer buyer, organizations that treat engagement as a board-level metric.
  • Geography: Global; particularly strong in AU/NZ, US, UK, EMEA.
  • Not their fit: SMB price-sensitive, performance-management-first buyers (Lattice/15Five own that), recruiting-first buyers.
04 · Product strengths

What Culture Amp actually does well.

  • Benchmarking corpus. 1B+ survey responses across customer base — the real network-effect moat. No competitor matches the comparison set [1][2].
  • Survey design and analytics. Customizable surveys, real-time feedback, anonymous response handling, sentiment analysis, theme detection [2].
  • International footprint. One of the stronger non-US-anchored HR-tech vendors; APAC/EMEA presence is real.
  • Performance management (lighter). Calibration, reviews, feedback workflows — competent though not as deep as Lattice.
  • Methodology rigor. Org-psychology-informed survey design; brand carries weight with sophisticated People Ops buyers.
05 · AI claims — verified vs marketing

What is real, what is copy.

Verified AI capabilities

  • Survey theme detection in open-ended responses [2]
  • Sentiment flagging and trend analysis [2]
  • General coaching chatbot [2]
  • Benchmarking-as-comparison surface — not strictly AI but core moat

Marketing claims (unverified or weak)

  • AI capabilities described as "primarily focused on engagement analytics" — accurate but limited [2]
  • No agent-routed workflows; coaching chatbot is general-purpose
  • No public MCP, no agent specification
  • No public AEDT bias-audit (lower regulatory exposure since survey AI is not screening AI, but absent)
  • No third-party audit relationship for the underlying AI
06 · Compliance & accessibility

Posture: standard certifications, employee-data-handling strong.

  • Security: SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR. Strong baseline given EMEA footprint.
  • Privacy: Anonymous survey response handling is a core product feature, not just a posture — operationally credible.
  • Bias auditing: No public AEDT page; survey AI has different fairness exposure than hiring AI.
  • EU AI Act: Survey-analytics AI is plausibly not Annex-III high-risk, but Culture Amp's EMEA exposure makes Article-2 minimal-risk classification reasonable.
07 · Recent launches

What Culture Amp shipped.

  • 2025–2026 · Survey-AI theme detection and sentiment enhancements. Incremental, methodology-driven [2].
  • 2025 · General coaching chatbot. Modest, scope-limited [2].
  • Through 2025 · Benchmarking-corpus expansion. Continuous data-network growth.
08 · Customer complaints

Recurring patterns.

Pattern 1 · Performance management depth
"Performance isn't their primary strength; better suited for engagement than reviews."

Honest tradeoff. Lattice and 15Five win performance-led deals. [1][3]

Pattern 2 · Pricing
"Enterprise pricing scales fast; small teams find it expensive vs alternatives."

Standard segment-mismatch complaint.

Pattern 3 · Survey-cadence rigidity
"Built around scheduled surveys; less suited for always-on pulse listening."

Cultural-methodology tradeoff.

Pattern 4 · Action-step follow-through
"Generating insights is strong; translating into action steps requires manual follow-through."

Where 15Five's coaching/action-focus wins comparatively.

Pattern 5 · AI scope limited
"AI is engagement-analytics-focused; doesn't extend to broader people-data workflows."

Appropriate for Culture Amp's scope; comparison point against agent-platform competitors.

Pattern 6 · Integration scope
"Integrates with major HRIS but the integration depth varies by partner."

Standard.
