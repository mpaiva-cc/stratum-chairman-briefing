00 · Headline read

Where Stratum can credibly win.

The 2025 Lattice HRIS exit is one of the more remarkable strategic moves in HCM in the last several years. Lattice launched an HRIS and payroll product, attempted to compete with HiBob/BambooHR/Rippling on suite breadth, found the unit economics and engineering complexity unworkable, and publicly announced sunsetting both products with customers having until July 2026 to migrate [1][6]. Lattice is now refocused on performance management, engagement, growth, and the AI Agent. This is honest strategic discipline — and the kind of admission most public companies cannot make.

The product on the focused surface is strong. Lattice AI helps speed up review writing, generates packets, summaries, evaluations; the AI Agent analyzes data from Engagement, Grow, Performance, 1:1s, Updates, Feedback to surface trends [1][2]. Reviewers consistently praise the AI features themselves; the persistent complaints are about navigation complexity, learning curve, and "process-heavy" workflows — not AI quality [1][2][7].

Stratum's defensible ground against Lattice: this is the complementary case. Lattice owns performance and engagement; Stratum's recruiting and people-graph posture pairs naturally. The right Stratum motion is integration — Stratum Recruiter + Lattice Performance, with the people-graph as the connecting primitive. Not displacement. Compass and Yield should build the partnership, not the competitive comparison.

01 · Company overview

Performance & engagement leader, focused.

Lattice was founded in 2015 in San Francisco by Jack Altman and Eric Koslow. Sarah Franklin (ex-Salesforce) became CEO in 2024. The company has raised more than $328M, with notable rounds from Andreessen Horowitz, Founders Fund, and others. In 2024-2025 Lattice launched HRIS and payroll modules; in mid-2025 the company announced both products would sunset by July 2026, with customers migrating off [1][6]. The Lattice focus going forward: performance management, engagement, growth, 1:1s, feedback, the AI Agent.

This is the unusual case of a public-private SaaS company making a focused-rather-than-broad bet. The HRIS-suite-thesis didn't work for Lattice; the focused-engagement-and-performance-with-AI thesis is the reset. Eglin's read: the reset is credible, the product is strong on the narrowed surface, and the partnership opportunity for Stratum is real.

02 · Positioning

"For companies that take people seriously" — performance, engagement, growth.

Post-HRIS-exit positioning is tight: performance management, engagement surveys, 1:1s, feedback, growth/career, the AI Agent layered across them. The implicit message: best-in-class on the people-development side, integration-first on everything else.

03 · Target customers

Mid-market and above; modern people-team buyer.

  • Core ICP: Mid-market and growth-stage tech (200-5,000 employees), with strong people teams who treat performance and engagement as strategic. Vertical concentration in tech, professional services, design, creative.
  • Geography: US-anchored, growing EMEA presence.
  • Not their fit: Buyers who want one-system HRIS+payroll+performance (after July 2026 this is the new reality); SMB price-sensitive; enterprise (Workday Talent owns that segment).
04 · Product strengths

What Lattice actually does well.

  • Performance reviews and feedback workflows. Best-in-class on the modern people-team market. Calibration, peer reviews, scorecards, multi-step approval.
  • 1:1 and feedback rhythm. The product encourages and rewards continuous feedback rather than annual reviews — a category-shaping commitment.
  • Lattice AI for review writing. Praised for speeding up review packets, summaries, evaluations [1].
  • AI Agent across Engagement, Grow, Performance, 1:1s, Updates, Feedback. Trend and insight surfacing across the data Lattice already owns [1][2].
  • Honest strategic discipline. The HRIS exit is unusual; demonstrates leadership willingness to refocus on competitive strengths.
05 · AI claims — verified vs marketing

What is real, what is copy.

Verified AI capabilities

  • Lattice AI for review writing — packets, summaries, evaluations [1]
  • AI Agent surfacing trends and insights across engagement, performance, growth [1][2]
  • Search bar question-answering [1]
  • AI-assisted feedback summarization

Marketing claims (unverified)

  • No public MCP, no agent specification, no tool list
  • No public AEDT bias-audit page (performance-management AI raises distinct fairness concerns)
  • No third-party audit relationship named
  • No public Responsible-AI statement at Ashby/Eightfold depth
06 · Compliance & accessibility

Posture: standard certifications, light AI-fairness evidence.

  • Security: SOC 2, GDPR, ISO 27001. Standard.
  • Bias auditing: No public AEDT-aligned bias-audit page; performance-AI fairness has fewer NYC-AEDT requirements than recruiting, but the underlying concern (performance reviews driving compensation and promotion) is real and worth published evidence.
  • EU AI Act: Performance management AI is plausibly Annex-III high-risk; no public Annex-III conformance materials.
  • Accessibility: No public VPAT located.

08 · Customer complaints

Recurring patterns.

Pattern 1 · Friction, not fundamentals
"Most common complaints are about friction, not fundamentals — learning curve, clunky navigation, features that feel buried."

UX-throughput tax. Doesn't undermine the core product but slows adoption. [1][2]

Pattern 2 · Process-heavy
"Parts of the platform feel rigid or process-heavy."

Tradeoff of structured performance management; relevant for teams that want lightweight. [1]

Pattern 3 · Goals and objectives setup
"Filling up goals and objectives is confusing; tedious update processes."

OKR-style workflow tax. [1]

Pattern 4 · Feedback delivery friction
"Managers have difficulty sending feedback, having to do it one by one."

Bulk-action UX gap. [1]

Pattern 5 · HRIS migration burden
"Customers who adopted Lattice HRIS now face migration burden by July 2026."

Self-inflicted; honest of Lattice to announce early but real for affected customers. [1][6]

Pattern 6 · Buried features
"Features feel buried; navigation requires learning."

Same shape as Workday and iCIMS complaints, smaller in degree. [2][7]

09 · Differentiation opportunities for Stratum

Where Stratum can credibly own ground.

Dimension
Stratum's defensible ground
01 · Partnership, not replacement
Stratum Recruiter + Lattice Performance via people-graph

Lattice owns performance; Stratum owns recruiting. The people-graph is the natural connector. Build the integration; don't compete.

02 · HRIS-exiting customer transition
Be the partner Lattice HRIS-customers turn to

Customers on Lattice HRIS+payroll must migrate by July 2026. Stratum's people-graph + connector posture is the recruiting-side answer; partner with HiBob / BambooHR / Rippling on the HRIS side for joint transition stories.

03 · MCP openness
Open platform connecting performance + recruiting

Lattice + Stratum + HRIS via MCP is the architectural picture worth painting publicly.

04 · Governance as artifact
Eval set + ledger + Compact refusals (including for performance AI)

Performance AI fairness is under-discussed in the category. Stratum can publish an eval-set probe specifically for compensation/promotion bias scenarios — a meaningful artifact category Lattice does not currently match.

05 · Modern recruiter UX
Tessera console clarity

Lattice's process-heavy UX is the same shape as the broader category pattern, even though smaller in degree. Tessera's console is the recruiter-side answer.

06 · Strategic discipline as proof
"Do one thing well" applies to both companies

Lattice's HRIS exit is honest strategic discipline. Stratum's recruiting + people-graph focus is the same discipline applied to recruiting. The partnership story writes itself.

10 · Eglin's recommendation

The play.

Against Lattice, the play is partnership. Build the integration, publish the joint architectural picture (Stratum Recruiter + Lattice Performance + customer-chosen HRIS via the people-graph), and be visible at the July 2026 Lattice HRIS migration moment. Compass + Yield + Helm own this.

Single most important insight. The Lattice HRIS exit demonstrates a category-wide truth: the "do everything" suite is harder than vendors usually admit, and customers benefit when vendors specialize. Stratum's recruiting + agent + people-graph specialization is the same kind of bet. Publish the joint story.
