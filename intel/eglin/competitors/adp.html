00 · Headline read

Where Stratum can credibly win.

ADP is the payroll incumbent — ~$20B revenue, decades of statutory-tax-and-compliance moat, and processes payroll for roughly one in six US workers. The HCM-modernization story is ADP Lyric HCM, framed as a full-suite cloud HCM for enterprise; ADP reports recent large enterprise wins on Lyric [3]. The AI strategy has two parts: (a) ADP Assist agents (January 2026) — persona-based AI agents for HR, payroll, talent, workforce [1][7]; (b) ADP Marketplace AI Agents (March 2, 2026) — a curated destination of third-party AI agents from partners including Absorb, Aquera, G-P, Employ, Praisidio, Salary.com, Tapcheck, Quantum Workplace [4][5]. The Marketplace launch is the more strategically interesting move: ADP positioning itself as the platform where customers consume third-party AI agents, not just as a vendor of its own.

This is partnership territory, not displacement. ADP's payroll-of-record position is durable and not what Stratum is competing for. The realistic Stratum motion is to be one of those third-party AI agents on ADP Marketplace — Stratum Recruiter as a curated agent partner, integrating with ADP's payroll-of-record customer base via the Marketplace. Compass + Yield should pursue ADP Marketplace listing actively.

01 · Company overview

The payroll category.

ADP was founded in 1949 in Paterson, NJ. The company is the largest HCM/payroll vendor by revenue (~$20B annual revenue) and serves more than one million clients across the SMB, mid-market, and enterprise tiers. Product lineup: ADP RUN (SMB payroll), ADP Workforce Now (mid-market HCM), ADP Lyric HCM (enterprise, modern cloud), plus specialized industry products and a global payroll network [3][6].

2026 strategic moves: ADP Assist agents (January) for in-product AI in HR/payroll/talent/workforce, the March 2 Marketplace AI Agents launch with named third-party partners, an investor presentation in May 2026 outlining AI agent rollout as a key growth lever [1].

02 · Positioning

"AI at the scale of work" — payroll-of-record plus curated agent ecosystem.

ADP's 2026 positioning combines payroll-incumbent durability with an AI ecosystem play: ADP Assist as native agents, ADP Marketplace as curated third-party agents, ADP OneData as the underlying data platform [1][3][6]. The framing is mature: payroll is the constant, AI is the layer that makes workforce intelligence emerge from the payroll-of-record data.

03 · Target customers

All tiers — SMB to enterprise, US-centric with global capability.

  • SMB: ADP RUN.
  • Mid-market: ADP Workforce Now (largest customer base by count).
  • Enterprise: ADP Lyric HCM (the modern cloud platform; competing more directly with Workday at the top tier).
  • Geography: US-anchored with global payroll network; particularly strong North America footprint.
04 · Product strengths

What ADP actually does well.

  • Payroll-of-record durability. The category moat: statutory tax compliance, banking integration, decades of regulatory experience. Effectively impossible to replicate without buying ADP.
  • ADP Marketplace. The curated agent-partner ecosystem [4][5] is the most enterprise-credible third-party AI distribution channel in HCM.
  • ADP Lyric HCM. Modern enterprise platform; recent large-customer wins [3].
  • ADP OneData. Unified data platform underlying the agent strategy.
  • Scale and reliability. Payroll error tolerance is zero; ADP's operational track record is the trust asset.
05 · AI claims — verified vs marketing

What is real, what is copy.

Verified AI capabilities

  • ADP Assist agents — January 2026 launch, persona-based [1][7]
  • ADP Marketplace AI Agents — March 2, 2026 curated launch with named partners (Absorb, Aquera, G-P, Employ, Praisidio, Salary.com, Tapcheck, Quantum Workplace, others) [4][5]
  • ADP Lyric HCM with embedded AI [6]
  • ADP OneData unified data platform [3]

06 · Compliance & accessibility

Posture: payroll-incumbent strong, AI-fairness evidence developing.

  • Security: SOC 1/SOC 2, ISO 27001, payroll-grade compliance — among the strongest baselines in the category.
  • Bias auditing: No public AEDT bias-audit page for ADP-native AI specifically; the Marketplace third-party partners may have their own audits but ADP-curated audit posture is not yet public.
  • EU AI Act: ADP corporate AI ethics framework; product-specific Annex-III mapping not yet public.
  • Accessibility: ADP publishes VPATs at the suite level.
