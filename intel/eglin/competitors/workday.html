00 · Headline read

Where Stratum can credibly win.

Workday is the enterprise incumbent. They run payroll for a meaningful fraction of the Fortune 500, their AI cadence has accelerated materially (Illuminate, ASOR, Flex Credits), and they have shipped MCP support inside the Agent System of Record as part of the GA release [1][7]. The "Workday doesn't have AI" framing the market sometimes reaches for is wrong as of late 2025. Reading them as feature-light in 2026 will get any Stratum AE corrected by the first Workday SE in the room.

What Workday does not have, and is currently exposed on: (1) a clean AI-fairness story. Mobley v. Workday was conditionally certified as a nationwide ADEA collective action on 2025-05-16; Workday's own filings disclosed that 1.1 billion applications were rejected using its software tools during the relevant period; the collective is "potentially hundreds of millions" of members [3][4][5]. An amended complaint reuping California state-law and physical-disability claims was filed 2026-03-30 [9]. Whatever Workday says publicly about Responsible AI, every Compact-class buyer's AI-governance committee is reading this case. (2) Recruiter UX. The complaint pattern on G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, and Reddit r/recruiting is consistent and old: too many clicks, rigid workflows, candidates abandon long applications [2][8]. (3) Mid-market and growth fit. Workday wins the 5,000-plus seat enterprise; it does not win the 500-5,000 segment on either price or speed-to-value.

Stratum's defensible ground against Workday at the 100k-employee scale-plan is narrow but real: MCP-native architecture published in the open (Workday's MCP support exists inside ASOR but the customer-visible tool list, permission model, and agent-routing documentation are not as inspectable as Stratum's four-server public architecture); continuous governance as artifact (Stratum's eval set, ledger, refusals — published; Workday's response to the Mobley exposure is process-and-policy heavy and audit-light on public surfaces); and throughput-respecting recruiter console (Tessera's clicks-per-task wedge is the most under-priced axis against Workday Recruiting specifically). Workday wins on installed base, finance integration, and global payroll. Stratum cannot win those grounds in 2026 and should not pretend to.

01 · Company overview

The 100k-seat enterprise HCM default.

Workday was founded in 2005 by Dave Duffield (PeopleSoft founder) and Aneel Bhusri after Oracle's PeopleSoft acquisition. Headquartered in Pleasanton, CA. Carl Eschenbach became sole CEO in early 2024 after a co-CEO transition with Bhusri (who remains executive chair). The company reports more than 11,000 customers worldwide, including more than 60% of the Fortune 500, with FY25 revenue around $8.4B and ~20,000 employees [1][6]. The core product is a unified HCM + financial-management cloud — payroll, HR, talent, planning, spend, financial close — served as a single object-model SaaS suite. Workday Recruiting is the talent-acquisition module that competes with Greenhouse, Ashby, iCIMS, and SmartRecruiters at the enterprise tier.

The relevant AI posture shift is recent and substantial. 2025-05-19: Workday announced the next generation of Illuminate agents at Workday Rising in San Francisco [6]. 2025-09-16: Workday expanded Illuminate with new HR, Finance, and industry agents (Business Process Copilot, Case Agent, Performance Agent, Cost & Profitability Agent, Financial Close Agent, Academic Requirements Agent, Student Administration Agent), all with general availability targeted across 2026, and introduced Flex Credits — a subscription-bundled consumption model that lets customers apply credits across agents and platform innovations [1][7]. 2026: the Workday Agent System of Record (ASOR) went GA, with Agent Gateway support for MCP and A2A (agent-to-agent) protocols and OpenTelemetry alignment [10]. ASOR is the strategic move — it positions Workday as the system that governs all AI agents (Workday's and third-party) acting on Workday data and processes.

Eglin's read: The narrative inside Workday is that ASOR + Flex Credits is the strategic answer to the agent era — own the control plane, sell credit-based consumption, route everything through your governance layer. It is a credible enterprise play. The narrative outside Workday is shaped just as much by Mobley as by Illuminate. Both narratives are operating in parallel right now, and the buying conversation is increasingly about which one the customer's AI-governance committee believes.

02 · Positioning

"Trusted, compliant AI for enterprise work."

Workday's public positioning, especially in the May 2026 "While the EU AI Act Stalls, Workday Leads on Trusted, Compliant AI" blog post and the ASOR GA materials, is built around three nouns: governance, transparency, and control [10][11]. The narrative: Workday has strengthened logging and traceability across AI systems, supports MCP and A2A through the Agent Gateway, aligns with OpenTelemetry, and through ASOR provides "visibility and control over all of your AI agents" — Workday-built and third-party [10].

This positioning is well-aimed. It addresses the three things enterprise CIOs are asking about agent-era HCM (who is doing what on my data, can I prove it after the fact, can I stop it). It also serves a defensive purpose: ASOR is the architectural answer that lets Workday claim governance leadership at exactly the moment Mobley is making "Workday + AI fairness" a board-level conversation. The positioning works if buyers do not look closely at the gap between posture and artifact — what Workday describes in blogs about ASOR vs what is inspectable by a customer pre-purchase.

Eglin's read The "Workday Leads on Trusted, Compliant AI" framing is, in Eglin's view, a positioning move dressed as a technical claim. The substance of ASOR is real and defensible — agent registry, logging, MCP support. The framing — "leads" — is unverifiable; there is no independent audit, no published bias-audit summary for Workday Recruiting comparable to Ashby's FairNow scope, no AEDT-conformant bias-audit landing page that Eglin could find at the time of writing. Stratum should let the framing go uncontested in the trade press and instead focus on what it can ship that Workday cannot quickly match: operational governance evidence — eval sets, decisions ledgers, public refusals — that exists at the artifact layer, not just the architecture layer.
03 · Target customers

Enterprise. Public sector. Not the mid-market growth segment.

  • Core ICP: Enterprise (5,000+ employees), with material strength at the 25,000+ tier and Fortune 500 / Global 2000. Healthcare, financial services, technology, public sector, higher education.
  • Logos visible publicly: Walmart, Bank of America, Target, Salesforce, Netflix, Airbnb, plus a deep public-sector and higher-ed footprint (Workday Student is a non-trivial part of the higher-ed administrative-systems market).
  • Geography: Global. EMEA and APAC enterprise presence is real, though US remains the strongest concentration. Global payroll is a competitive advantage at scale.
  • Not their fit (today): Mid-market growth (500-5,000 employees) where speed-to-value and price-per-seat matter more than breadth — that is HiBob, Rippling, BambooHR, Lattice territory. AI-native startups (Ashby's ICP). Recruiting-only buyers who do not want a full HCM suite.
  • Where they are pushing in 2026: Workday for Industries (specialized higher-ed agents) and the lower end of enterprise via accelerated implementation programs.
  • The 100k tell: Stratum's scale-plan is anchored at 100k employees. At that scale, the realistic Workday-substitute conversation is not "rip and replace HCM" — it is "best-of-breed talent acquisition on top of Workday Core HCM, integrated via the people-graph." That framing is what makes the differentiation defensible at the top of market.
04 · Product strengths

What Workday actually does well.

  • Object-model unification. A single object model for worker, position, organization, and financial dimensions. The reason Workday wins finance + HR consolidation deals is that the data model is genuinely shared, not federated. This is the architectural moat — 20 years old, hard to replicate.
  • Global payroll and compliance breadth. Native and partner-routed payroll across ~100 countries. Compliance update cadence (statutory tax changes, country-specific reporting) is industry-leading at enterprise scale.
  • Agent System of Record (ASOR) as GA platform. The Agent Gateway with MCP and A2A support, plus OpenTelemetry alignment, is a genuine architectural step — Workday is the first major HCM suite to publish a vendor-neutral governance plane for agents [10]. Whether the implementation quality matches the framing is the next 12-month test.
  • Workday Extend and Workday Studio. Customer and partner extensibility is real and used in production by large customers. Combined with the Workday Marketplace, this is a meaningful integration moat.
  • Industry depth. Workday Student (higher-ed), Workday for Public Sector, and the specialized Healthcare/Financial Services flavors are differentiated assets. Few HCM vendors operate at this vertical depth.
  • Financial discipline and balance sheet. ~$8.4B revenue, profitable, well-capitalized — buyers' procurement teams see Workday as low vendor-risk. This is a meaningful intangible at the 100k-seat scale.
05 · AI claims — verified vs marketing

What is real, what is copy.

Verified AI capabilities

  • Illuminate agent set, expanded September 2025: Business Process Copilot, Case Agent, Performance Agent (HR); Cost & Profitability, Financial Close (Finance); Academic Requirements, Student Administration (Industry) [1][7]
  • Agent System of Record (ASOR) — GA, with MCP and A2A support via Agent Gateway, OpenTelemetry alignment [10]
  • Flex Credits — subscription-bundled consumption model for agents, GA [1][7]
  • Workday Data Cloud — unveiled at Workday Rising 2025 [6][12]
  • Job-description assist, candidate matching, performance-review summarization — shipped, validated in customer reviews [2][8]
  • Strengthened logging and traceability across AI systems for audit and post-market monitoring, framed against EU AI Act expectations [11]

Marketing claims (unverified, directional, or framing)

  • "Workday Leads on Trusted, Compliant AI" — positioning, not independently verified [11]
  • "Visibility and control over all of your AI agents" via ASOR — true at the architecture layer; customer-inspectable artifacts (tool lists, permission models, agent-routing docs) less public than the architecture diagram
  • "Industry-standard security model" — generic claim, not a specification
  • "Purpose-built for work" — Illuminate marketing language [6]
  • "Measurable outcomes" from agents — directional; no published benchmark for time-to-fill, time-to-resolution, or bias-by-segment outcomes
  • No public AEDT bias-audit summary for Workday Recruiting that Eglin could locate, despite NYC-exposed customer base — significant gap given Mobley exposure
  • No public model card or LLM-provider disclosure for Illuminate agents
Eglin's read · the Mobley lens Every AI claim Workday makes in 2026 is now read against the Mobley v. Workday case. The 1.1 billion-rejected-applications figure came from Workday's own filings [4][5]. The court's finding that Workday is "sufficiently involved in the hiring process to be held potentially liable as an agent of the employers" reshapes the vendor-liability landscape for the whole category [4]. ASOR — the agent registry, logging, traceability — is the right architectural response, but it is announced after the conditional certification, which will read in cross-examination as remediation, not differentiation. Stratum's position should not be "we are unlike Workday on AI fairness." It should be: "we built our governance layer before any customer asked us to prove we needed one, and the artifacts are inspectable now." That is a different, sharper claim and it does not depend on Workday being bad.
06 · Compliance & accessibility

Posture: strong certifications, active AI-fairness litigation.

  • Security: SOC 1 / SOC 2 / ISO 27001 / ISO 27017 / ISO 27018 / FedRAMP / IRAP / C5 — among the broadest certification footprints in HCM. Trust posture is enterprise-grade and not in question.
  • EU AI Act: Strengthened logging and traceability across AI systems, framed against AI Act expectations for technical documentation, record-keeping, and post-market monitoring [11]. Workday Recruiting is plausibly a "high-risk system" under Annex III; Workday's public Annex-III mapping is not as detailed as a high-risk-system-conformance document a Notified Body would expect.
  • AEDT (NYC Local Law 144): No publicly visible bias-audit summary landing page for Workday Recruiting that Eglin could locate as of 2026-05-18. Implied compliance via customer use; not vendor-published in the format AEDT requires the deploying employer to publish.
  • Mobley v. Workday (ADEA collective action): Conditionally certified 2025-05-16 [3][4]. Amended complaint adding California state-law and physical-disability claims filed 2026-03-30 [9]. This is the single most consequential AI-fairness case in HCM and the outcome will shape vendor liability across the category. Workday has continued operations and product cadence through the litigation; settlement vs verdict path is uncertain.
  • Bias auditing: Workday publishes Responsible AI principles and has an internal Ethics & Compliance program. No third-party bias-audit relationship (FairNow, Warden AI, BABL, ORCAA) for Workday Recruiting is named publicly at the level Ashby's FairNow scope is named. Compare: Ashby's audit is third-party, periodic, and named.
  • Accessibility: Workday publishes a VPAT and states WCAG 2.1 AA conformance for core products. Documentation is more public than most competitors in this set.
  • Data residency: Multi-region (US, EMEA, APAC, Canada). Enterprise-grade.
Eglin's read Workday's compliance posture on security and accessibility is strong. Its compliance posture on AI fairness is, candidly, contested. The Mobley case is not a "claim" — it is a litigated proceeding with a court order conditionally certifying the collective. The way to talk about this externally is not as a slogan ("Workday discriminates"); it is as an observation about what the category needs to evolve to — vendor accountability for hiring outcomes, not just vendor neutrality on hiring tools. Stratum's posture should treat Mobley as a category event, not a competitor cudgel. That is both more honest and more durable.
07 · Recent launches (last 18 months)

What Workday shipped.

  • 2026 (recent) · Workday Agent System of Record GA. ASOR is now generally available, with Agent Gateway supporting MCP and A2A, OpenTelemetry alignment [10].
  • 2025-09-16 · Illuminate expansion. Seven new agents added to Illuminate across HR, Finance, and Industry; Flex Credits consumption model unveiled; broader GA targeted across 2026 [1][7].
  • 2025-05-19 · Workday Rising 2025. Next generation of Illuminate agents announced, Workday Data Cloud unveiled, Flex Credits previewed [6][12].
  • 2025 · AI Recruiter capability expansions. Job-description assist, candidate matching, scorecard suggestions, automated screening — incremental and consistently shipped through the year [2][8].
  • 2024–2025 · Mobley collective certified. Not a product launch but the most consequential category event — conditional certification 2025-05-16; amended complaint 2026-03-30 [3][4][9].
08 · Customer complaints (G2 / Capterra / Trustpilot / Gartner)

Recurring patterns.

Workday HCM averages ~4.0/5 on G2 with ~1,200+ reviews; Workday Recruiting averages slightly lower; Trustpilot scores are materially worse for candidate-side experience [2][8][13]. Complaint patterns recur across G2, Capterra, Gartner Peer Insights, and Trustpilot, and they are old — recruiter and candidate UX has been the consistent gap for years:

Pattern 1 · Recruiter UX friction
"Workflows feel rigid and unintuitive, requiring more clicks and navigation than expected for routine recruiting tasks — slows execution in fast-moving hiring."

Cited consistently on G2 Workday Recruiting and Gartner Peer Insights [2][13]. Tessera's throughput-respecting console is the direct counter — same wedge that worked against Greenhouse and Ashby, with much stronger evidence at Workday.

Pattern 2 · Candidate application abandonment
"Hiring Managers hate it, recruiters hate it, candidates hate it. Long forms, repeated information entry, broken back-button on multi-page applications."

Recruiter of 26 years on Trustpilot, with explicit comparison to Greenhouse, iCIMS, SuccessFactors [8]. Candidate-experience complaint is the most consistent single criticism of Workday in the public domain.

Pattern 3 · Implementation cost and timeline
"Implementations consistently overrun on cost and time. Consulting hours scale with the customer-side configuration ambition."

Familiar enterprise-suite critique. Becomes more material at the 5,000–15,000 employee tier where the ROI calculation gets tighter than at the Fortune-500 tier.

Pattern 4 · Customization rigidity / change management
"Changing configured business processes is non-trivial — you often need a partner SI or Workday-certified internal team to make routine changes."

Configurability is real but expensive to operate. The Business Process Copilot Illuminate agent is announced partly as a response to this complaint [1].

Pattern 5 · Reporting and analytics latency
"Reporting is powerful but slow; complex reports take minutes; ad-hoc analysis often requires data exports to BI tools."

Workday Data Cloud (2025-05) is the response. Customer evidence of the response actually solving this is still thin in public reviews.

Pattern 6 · Pricing and module bundling
"Total cost is opaque; modules priced by employee count plus implementation; AI consumption now layered on top via Flex Credits."

Flex Credits clarifies AI consumption pricing but adds a new dimension to the total-cost conversation. Procurement teams are tracking this carefully in 2026 RFPs.

09 · Differentiation opportunities for Stratum

Where Stratum can credibly own ground.

Dimension
Stratum's defensible ground
01 · MCP openness
Four servers, named tools, customer-inspectable

Workday supports MCP via the Agent Gateway inside ASOR [10]. Stratum publishes the four-server architecture (graph, console, recruiter, connectors) with engineering documentation and named tools. The Workday answer is "we govern your agents in our control plane." The Stratum answer is "we expose ours publicly so you can build your own control plane." These are different goods — Stratum's framing should be "your governance, our openness," not "we have MCP and they don't."

02 · Governance as artifact
Eval set + decisions ledger + Compact refusals

Workday's response to Mobley is process- and policy-heavy on public surfaces; the inspectable artifacts (third-party bias-audit summary for Workday Recruiting, model card for Illuminate agents, AEDT bias-audit landing page) are thinner than the architectural narrative implies. Stratum's eval-set runs, decisions ledger, and six published refusals are operational evidence. This is the single most under-priced differentiation lane against Workday in 2026.

03 · Recruiter throughput
Modern console, clicks-per-task as the metric

Workday Recruiting's UX-friction complaint is the most consistent single criticism in the public domain, decades old, replicated across G2, Capterra, Gartner Peer Insights, Trustpilot, and Reddit r/recruiting [2][8][13]. Tessera's console — calm on top, powerful underneath — wins recruiter trials when the comparison is "clicks to disposition" not "feature count."

04 · Candidate experience
Apply once, don't repeat yourself, mobile-first

Application abandonment on Workday-hosted careers sites is a candidate-experience complaint Workday cannot fix without redesigning the apply flow. Stratum's careers-site shipping rhythm should make this an explicit comparison point: a recorded "apply in N minutes" demo against a public Workday careers site, side-by-side, is a sales asset Compass should commission.

05 · Speed to value
Weeks, not quarters

Workday implementations are quarter-scale projects. Stratum's connector-factory cadence + the autonomous-operating model means deployment is weeks, not quarters, at the recruiting layer. This is mid-market territory by definition; the right way to position is "Stratum Recruiter on top of Workday Core HCM at the 100k tier, integrated via the people-graph" — not as a Workday replacement, which is not a 2026 sale.

06 · Autonomous operation as proof
Eleven-agent swarm running the company

Workday's narrative is "agents will transform work." Stratum's narrative is "agents are running our work — eleven of them, in production, with a public decisions ledger." The autonomous-operating model is a credibility asset against the largest incumbent in the category specifically because Workday cannot replicate it — their organization is not built that way and could not become so within the 2026 buying cycle.

10 · Eglin's recommendation

The play.

Against Workday at the 100k-seat scale, Stratum does not "win the HCM suite." Workday wins that conversation through finance integration, global payroll, and 20 years of object-model engineering. The right framing is best-of-breed Recruiting + people-graph on top of Workday Core HCM, with three sharp wedges: (1) MCP openness (we publish, they govern), (2) governance as artifact (eval set, ledger, refusals — operational, inspectable, predates any litigation we are exposed to), and (3) recruiter and candidate throughput (clicks-per-task, apply-flow brevity — Tessera's craft as competitive weapon).

The Mobley posture matters. Stratum should not use Mobley as a marketing weapon. The case is real, ongoing, and serious; treating it as a slogan is both ethically wrong and tactically fragile (Workday will settle or win on appeal, and the slogan will date badly). The right posture is category-level: Mobley is the event that proved vendor liability is a category risk, and Stratum was built with that risk in mind from day one. That framing is more durable, more honest, and harder for Workday to refute.

Owners. Helm for the overall positioning paragraph and the Mobley posture. Compass + Forge for the MCP openness side-by-side. Kernel for the governance-as-artifact narrative (especially the eval-set framing). Tessera for the recruiter and candidate UX comparison demos. Yield for the people-graph-on-top-of-Workday-Core integration story. Compass should commission the "apply in N minutes" comparison demo as a Q3 2026 sales asset.

Timeline. ASOR is GA now; Workday's MCP-supporting narrative will harden through Workday Rising 2026 (likely late summer / fall). Stratum should publish a public side-by-side and the "best-of-breed on top of Workday" positioning page before Rising 2026 to anchor the comparison frame ahead of Workday's next major announcement cadence. Pair this battlecard with the Greenhouse and Ashby battlecards for the Compact's enterprise-positioning review.

Single most important insight. The Workday narrative in 2026 is not "they don't have AI." It is "their AI has a control plane and a court case." Stratum's positioning needs to respect both halves of that, and to operate at the artifact layer (eval set, ledger, refusals, MCP openness) where Workday is structurally slower to respond. The architectural narrative is contested. The artifact layer is where Stratum can credibly win the next 12 months.
