00 · Headline read

Where Stratum can credibly win.

Paylocity is the mid-market HCM/payroll vendor that has built durable customer satisfaction through a strong customer-engagement motion and per-module pricing transparency relative to Paycom. G2 ratings sit at 4.5/5 across ~5,000 reviews [1][2][3]. The platform covers payroll, HR, benefits administration, time tracking, performance, and a basic recruiting module. AI is "embedded" rather than agent-platform; the cadence trails the headline-AI competitors.

This is partnership territory for Stratum, not displacement. Paylocity customers who want best-in-class recruiting are the realistic complement-sale; build the connector and the integration story.

01 · Company overview

Mid-market HCM with durable CE motion.

Paylocity was founded in 1997 in Schaumburg, IL. Publicly traded (NASDAQ: PCTY), ~$1.5B+ revenue, ~40,000 customers concentrated in the mid-market segment. The product is a full HCM/payroll suite; pricing is per-module at $22-32 per employee per month with no implementation fee — a meaningful differentiator vs Paycom's bundled-with-implementation pricing [1]. The customer-engagement motion is among the strongest in mid-market HCM.

02 · Positioning

"Modern HR and payroll" — CE-led mid-market.

Paylocity positions on customer experience and ease of use for the mid-market segment. AI is embedded across workflows but not the headline; the brand strength is dependable mid-market payroll and HR with a strong account-management motion.

03 · Target customers

Mid-market US-centric, 50–1,500 employees.

  • Core ICP: US mid-market (50-1,500 employees) across professional services, manufacturing, retail, healthcare.
  • Geography: US-only payroll natively.
04 · Product strengths

What Paylocity actually does well.

  • Customer-engagement motion. Strong account management; 4.5/5 G2 with high review count [1][2].
  • Per-module pricing. No mandatory bundle; no implementation fee — transparency advantage vs Paycom [1].
  • US payroll reliability. Mid-market payroll-of-record durability.
  • Mobile experience. Generally well-rated mobile app vs many competitors.
  • Benefits administration depth. Strong open-enrollment and benefits workflows.
05 · AI claims — verified vs marketing

What is real, what is copy.

Verified AI capabilities

  • AI embedded across workflows (content generation, insights surfacing)
  • Recruiting module with basic AI assists
  • Standard HR workflow automation

Marketing claims (unverified or thin)

  • No headline AI agent product comparable to ADP Assist or Workday Illuminate
  • No public MCP, no agent specification
  • No public AEDT bias-audit page
  • No third-party audit relationship named
06 · Compliance & accessibility

Posture: mid-market-standard.

  • Security: SOC 2 Type II, GDPR. Standard.
  • Bias auditing: No public AEDT page.
  • Accessibility: Light documentation.
07 · Recent launches

What Paylocity shipped.

  • 2025–2026 · AI embedded enhancements. Incremental rather than headline; cadence trails ADP/Workday.
  • Through 2025 · Benefits and payroll workflow refinements.
