00 · Headline read

Where Stratum can credibly win.

Deel is the global-employment infrastructure leader — EOR, contractor management, and global payroll across 150+ countries, with ~$12B valuation. The August 2025 launch of Deel AI Workforce introduced specialized named agents (Hiring Guru, PTO Fairy, Border Buddy, Schedule Sheriff, IT Guy, Goodbye Genie, Payroll Detective) — disciplined named-product AI similar to 15Five's approach [1][2]. Trustpilot rating is 4.8/5 across 7,000+ reviews, though the review distribution is heavily contractor-tilted [3].

Deel is in an unusual public position as the defendant in Rippling's corporate-espionage litigation. As of late 2025, bank records appeared to indicate Deel paid the confessed spy via the Deel COO's wife's account; the DOJ is reportedly investigating; the case is active and Deel has continued operations [Rippling battlecard]. Stratum's policy is not to weaponize this litigation against Deel. The case is ongoing, the outcome is not settled, and using it as a marketing weapon is ethically weak and tactically fragile. The category-level lesson — internal data-access controls matter — applies to Stratum regardless.

The realistic Stratum motion is partnership-shaped on cross-border recruiting: Stratum Recruiter handles candidate sourcing and selection globally; Deel handles the employer-of-record conversion when the candidate accepts. Build the connector; publish the joint architectural picture.

01 · Company overview

Global-employment infrastructure category.

Deel was founded in 2019 in San Francisco by Alex Bouaziz and Shuo Wang. The company has scaled aggressively — ~$12B valuation, customers across 150+ countries, broad EOR + contractor + global payroll product set. Product pricing: EOR at $599/employee/month, Global Payroll at $29/employee/month, Contractor Management at $49/contractor/month, US Payroll from $19/employee/month, US PEO at $95/employee/month all-inclusive [1][3][5]. Deel AI Workforce launched August 2025 with named specialized agents [1][2].

The Rippling espionage litigation is active. Rippling alleges Deel orchestrated a multi-month spy operation; banking records produced in November 2025 indicate fund transfers via the Deel COO's wife's account to the confessed spy; DOJ investigation reportedly active; jurisdictional rulings ongoing in Irish High Court as of March 2026 [Rippling battlecard sources]. Outcome unsettled. Eglin's view: cover the case factually; do not weaponize.

02 · Positioning

"Hire anyone, anywhere" — global employment infrastructure.

Deel positions as the platform that makes hiring across borders simple: EOR for compliance, contractor management for flexible engagements, global payroll for cross-jurisdiction salary distribution. The AI Workforce launch positions Deel as also being an AI-agent platform for HR tasks, with the named specialized agents reinforcing the discipline that 15Five also demonstrates.

03 · Target customers

Cross-border employment.

  • Core ICP: Companies hiring across borders — startups going global, scale-ups expanding international teams, contractor-heavy organizations.
  • Geography: Global by design; 150+ countries supported.
  • Not their fit: US-domestic-only payroll buyers (Gusto, Paylocity, ADP own that); enterprises with established cross-border payroll already through Workday or ADP global.
04 · Product strengths

What Deel actually does well.

  • EOR coverage breadth. 150+ countries — the moat in global employment infrastructure.
  • Contractor management. Easy payment, compliance, agreement generation for contractor relationships.
  • Deel AI Workforce. August 2025 launch with named specialized agents (Hiring Guru, PTO Fairy, Border Buddy, Schedule Sheriff, IT Guy, Goodbye Genie, Payroll Detective) — disciplined naming [1][2].
  • Speed to value. Onboarding international employees in days vs months at enterprise alternatives.
  • Self-generation AI employment letters. EOR employees can generate compliant verification letters from their dashboard — reduces HR document workload [1].

06 · Compliance & accessibility

Posture: cross-border-compliance strong, AI-fairness evidence thin.

  • Security: SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR. Strong baseline given global scope.
  • Employment compliance: Native to the product — Deel's whole brand is cross-jurisdiction employment compliance. Strong here.
