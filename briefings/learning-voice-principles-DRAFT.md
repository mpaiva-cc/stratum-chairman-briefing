# Voice Principles — The Learning Product (DRAFT)

**Author:** Echo (PR / voice) · **Date:** 2026-05-19 · **Status:** DRAFT

The problem: learning product voice defaults to one of two failure modes. The brochure voice ("unlock your team's potential") sounds like a vendor. The tutor voice — slightly elevated, slightly patient — is condescending. Neither fits Stratum.

Recruiter's voice is efficiency-led. Tenure's voice is warmth-led. The learning product's center of gravity is **apprenticeship**: the voice of someone handing a specific thing across a workbench. Not performing expertise. Transmitting it.

---

## Seven principles

**1. Teach by showing, not by naming.**
Name the mechanism. Not the category. Say what the agent looks at and what it does.

*Use:* "It reads the gap before it proposes anything."
*Refuse:* "Intelligent, adaptive, personalized learning pathways."

**2. The learner is a professional with limited time.**
Every sentence should assume forty minutes this week, not four hours. Never imply the product works better if the learner spends more time in it.

*Use:* "Fifteen minutes. One gap closed. Back to work."
*Refuse:* "Immersive learning experiences that fit your schedule."

**3. Specificity is the only form of credibility here.**
The LMS category has made vague claims about engagement and retention for twenty years. Name what we measure. If we can't measure it, we don't claim it.

*Use:* "The agent tracks whether the skill shows up in the work record. Not whether you clicked 'complete.'"
*Refuse:* "Drive meaningful behavior change across your organization."

**4. Never flatter. Never motivate. Never coach in the second person.**
"You've got this" is a fitness app. We inform and move on.

*Use:* "Completed. Next gap: data storytelling. Estimated time: 22 minutes."
*Refuse:* "Great work! You're on a roll. Keep the momentum going."

**5. On the listen widget, read as if handing notes to a peer.**
Widget defaults: OpenAI HD "echo" voice, speed 0.95. Calm, unhurried — not broadcast, not classroom. A colleague reading you the important part of a memo while you're doing something else.

**6. "Learning" is fine. "Journey" is not.**
"Learning journey," "learning experience," "learning destination." Clichés. Say what the product does. It surfaces skill gaps. It closes them.

**7. Provenance is part of the voice.**
When the product cites a source, the voice says so — not as a disclaimer, as a feature. "That answer is from your company's March 2026 compliance manual." Grounding is what differentiates us from an LMS that generates its own content.
