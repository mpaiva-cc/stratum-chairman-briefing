## The four options

**Quiet ship.** Publish the product page. No press, no embargo, no podcast. Let the page speak. This is the pattern Stratum used for Recruiter. Advantage: the product does not have to be narratively complete on day one. The page exists; journalists who find it cover it on their schedule. Disadvantage: agentic learning is a contested category in 2026. Without a single authoritative framing, the first journalist to cover it names it for us — and names it wrong.

**On-record, one tier-1 reporter.** Embargo with Casey Newton (Platformer), Maxwell Strachan (Fast Company), or Karen Hao (independent). One briefing call, one story, one chance to set the frame before the category noise closes in. Advantage: controlled narrative, credible outlet, no panel to prepare for. Disadvantage: a reporter with access will ask the three hard questions listed below. We need complete answers, not holding language.

**Founder podcast appearance.** Acquired, This Week in Startups, 20VC. Sixty to ninety minutes of unscripted founder voice. Advantage: long form lets the methodology land. Disadvantage: Stratum has not yet appeared on any of these shows. A first appearance on a major podcast at product launch asks the audience to trust both the founder and the product simultaneously. That is too much work for one session. This option is right for the second cycle — after the product has a name, a customer, and a story.

**Conference keynote.** HR Transform, Unleash, HR Tech. Advantage: the category audience is in the room. Disadvantage: Eglin's research is still in placeholder state. We do not yet have the credible differentiation story tight enough for a keynote Q&A. Keynote is a Phase 2 move.

## Recommendation: quiet ship, with one pre-briefing option held in reserve

**The product is not named. Eglin's research is not complete. We do not have a customer reference for learning.** In that condition, the right move is quiet ship — the page publishes, the listen widget reads it, and the press strategy clock starts only after Helm has written the dispatch and the product has a name and a claim.

The one exception: if Helm decides the dispatch is complete and the claim is falsifiable, we should offer a single background briefing to one reporter — Karen Hao, because she covers AI-in-the-enterprise without the LMS industry beat's incumbency bias — with a 48-hour embargo before the page goes live. Not a full press release. A twenty-minute call and the page URL. She covers it or she doesn't.

We do not do the podcast. We do not do the conference. We earn those after the first customer is on record.

## The three hard questions

### "Is this just an LMS with a chatbot on top?"

**Helm's answer.** "That framing treats the content library as the product. We don't have a content library. What we have is a people-graph that already knows what each person's role requires, what they've done, what their manager has said about their gaps. The learning agent reads that graph and closes the gap. The distinction matters: we're not adding a chat window to a course catalog. We're building from the work record outward."

### "Are you replacing instructional designers?"

**Helm's answer.** "Instructional designers inside large enterprises spend most of their time on compliance mandates — safety certification renewals, regulatory recertification. That work is largely undifferentiated and manual. We automate that part so that the L&D team's actual expertise goes to the problems a graph can't solve: leadership development, change management, culture work. That's not replacement. That's where the budget should have been going anyway."

### "What about hallucinations in training content — what if the agent teaches something wrong?"

**Helm's answer.** "It's the right question and we treat it as an infrastructure question, not a disclaimer. Every piece of content the agent surfaces carries a provenance trace — what source, what version, what SME approved it. We do not generate claims from the model and present them as policy. The model routes; verified sources speak. If a source is not in the verified set, the agent says so. Learners see the difference."
