§ 01 · Product

What it is, what it isn't, and how it touches your stack.

The ten questions a CHRO and her CIO ask in the first thirty minutes. If you only read one category, read this one.

10 answers Each links to the deeper page when there is one.
Q · 01

What does Stratum actually do?

Stratum builds a unified people graph from your HRIS, ATS, performance, payroll, and equity systems, then sits a CHRO-grade copilot — the Console — on top of it. You ask questions like "who left in Q1 and why," the Console answers with citations to the underlying records, and over time it begins to take supported actions you approve.

Read more on the product page →
Q · 02

How is it different from Workday, Greenhouse, or Rippling?

Workday, Greenhouse, and Rippling are systems of record — they store the truth of who is hired, paid, and reviewed. Stratum is a system of truth that sits above them, connects them, and answers the questions the records can't. We don't replace them; we make them legible to a CHRO.

Read the full comparison →
Q · 03

Do you replace our HRIS or ATS?

No. If a vendor tells you they will replace your HRIS in twelve months, they are either selling a five-year migration or a fiction. Stratum reads from your existing systems and — in Plan B — writes carefully back into them through approved actions.

Q · 04

What systems can you connect to today?

Out of the box: Workday, Greenhouse, Lever, Lattice, 15Five, ADP-WFN, BambooHR, Carta, Okta, Rippling, plus Snowflake / BigQuery / Databricks for warehouse mirrors. One custom HRIS connector is included in standard implementations.

See the integration directory →
Q · 05

How does the Console work without us giving you our data?

Two options. Either you spin up an isolated tenant in our environment and we pull through read-only connectors, or — for the more conservative — we deploy the Console inside your VPC and the data never leaves your perimeter. Both setups are described in the security packet.

See the deployment options →
Q · 06

Can we try it without commitment?

Yes. The Console at stratum.ai/console runs on a synthetic but realistic 4,200-employee company. You can ask it real CHRO questions and watch the citations come back. No login, no email gate, no demo gauntlet.

Try the live console →
Q · 07

What does "system of truth above the system of record" mean?

Records say what happened. Truth says what it meant and what should happen next. Your ATS knows Helm was hired in March; only a graph that joins her offer, her org, her manager's tenure, and her first review can tell you why she resigned in October. That join is what we sell.

Q · 08

Do you use our data to train AI?

No. Customer data is never used to train any model — ours or anyone else's. The contract says so, the architecture enforces it, and the SOC 2 audit confirms it. Aggregated, fully anonymised benchmarks are an opt-in only.

Read the data-use stance →
Q · 09

Can we use our own LLM — GPT-5, Gemini, our private model — instead of Claude?

Not yet. Today the Console runs on Anthropic Claude with a small set of internal evaluators. Bring-your-own-key for GPT-5 and Gemini is on the roadmap for the second half of 2027. Private-model routing follows once we are sure we can hold the eval bar.

See the roadmap →
Q · 10

What's on the product roadmap?

Through 2026: more connectors, the Decisions surface (Plan B write-back), and the second eval set. Through 2027: BYOK, the manager copilot, and a structured agents API. We publish the roadmap as a single page rather than a deck so you can read it in five minutes.

Read the roadmap →
§ 02 · Pricing

A platform fee, plus the decisions we actually supported.

No per-seat math. No surprise renewals. We bill for what the Console did, not who logged in.

8 answers Two plans, annual prepaid, usage in arrears.
Q · 01

How much does Stratum cost?

Plan A (Platform) starts at $12,000 / month, annual prepaid, for companies in the 1,000–8,000 employee band. Plan B adds a quarterly usage tier on decisions supported. The full math, including the band table, is published.

See pricing in full →
Q · 02

Why don't you publish per-seat pricing?

Because the value of Stratum doesn't scale with headcount — it scales with how many real decisions the Console supports. A 1,200-person company with an active CHRO often gets more out of it than a 6,000-person one with a quiet one. Per-seat pricing punishes the wrong thing.

Q · 03

What's a "decision supported"?

A material people decision the Console either answered with cited evidence or executed via an approved write-back — a hire, a promotion, a comp adjustment, an org change, an offboarding. We count them quarterly, you see the log, and the tier is set against that count.

Q · 04

Is there a minimum contract length?

Twelve months. We don't sell month-to-month — implementation alone is sixty days, and a six-month contract isn't long enough to be a good citizen for either side.

Q · 05

Can we do a paid pilot before signing a full contract?

For the right design partner, yes. The pilot is ninety days, scoped to two connectors and the Console, and the fee credits against the first-year contract if you proceed. Two design-partner seats remain for 2026.

Q · 06

Are there volume discounts?

There are band discounts published in the band table. There are no off-table discretionary discounts — the published price is the price, and your procurement team won't be punished for not negotiating hard.

Q · 07

Can we negotiate the usage tier?

Within reason. The tier brackets are fixed; what we will discuss is which decision types count, how quarterly true-ups behave, and whether unused usage rolls. None of that is a markup hidden in the SOW.

Q · 08

What happens at renewal?

Renewal is at the published price for your band, with a ninety-day notice period either way. If your band changed materially mid-term, we true up rather than ambush you at the end of year one.

Q · 01

Are you SOC 2 compliant?

SOC 2 Type I is complete (March 2026). Type II observation window closes August 2026; the report is expected in October. ISO 27001 follows in Q1 2027.

Q · 02

Do you have a SIG / CAIQ questionnaire available?

Yes — both SIG Lite and the CAIQ v4.0.3 are pre-filled and available within twenty-four hours of an MNDA. We do not gate them behind a sales call.

Request the packet →
Q · 03

What's your data residency story?

Two regions live today: US (us-east-1) and EU (eu-west-1, Dublin). Your tenant pins to a single region for storage, compute, and inference. APAC (ap-northeast-1, Tokyo) opens Q3 2026.

See residency & sub-processors →
Q · 04

How do you handle our PII?

Encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256 with customer-isolated KMS keys). Access is role-scoped, logged, and reviewed quarterly. PII is never used for model training, never shared with sub-processors except for the explicit list in the DPA, and is purged on contract termination per the schedule.

Q · 05

Can we use customer-managed keys (BYOK)?

Yes. AWS KMS BYOK is generally available; GCP and Azure CMEK are in private preview and expected GA in Q3 2026. Key rotation is yours to schedule and revoke is honoured immediately.

Q · 06

What's your incident response SLA?

P1 confirmed within four hours, customer notification within twenty-four hours, written post-mortem within fourteen days. We will not bury an incident in a status page footnote.

Q · 07

Have you ever had a customer data incident?

No. As of T+7d, Stratum has had zero customer-data incidents and zero confirmed breaches. The status page is the canonical record and will remain so when that changes.

See the status page →
Q · 08

How are you preparing for the EU AI Act?

Stratum is classified limited risk under our self-assessment because the Console supports a human decision-maker rather than making the decision. We publish model cards, eval sets, and the human-in-the-loop record for every Decisions write-back, in line with Articles 13 and 14.

In simpler terms

The EU AI Act puts AI tools in different risk buckets. Stratum is in the limited risk bucket because a human — your CHRO or HR team — makes every final call. The Console gives evidence and options; it does not act alone. We document this at every step and publish the records.

Q · 09

What about NYC AEDT compliance?

We are not an Automated Employment Decision Tool under Local Law 144 because the Console does not score, rank, or filter candidates. Our hiring-side product surfaces evidence; the recruiter and hiring manager make the call. We publish the annotation matrix on request.

In simpler terms

New York City has a law (Local Law 144) about AI tools used in hiring. That law targets tools that automatically score or rank job candidates. Stratum shows your recruiter information and context — the recruiter decides. That is why the law does not apply to us.

Q · 10

Can we audit you?

Yes. Annual customer audit rights are in the MSA — penetration testing of the customer tenant, log access, and a one-day on-site at our Palo Alto office. We will share the standing pen-test report and the audit calendar before you sign.

§ 04 · Implementation

From signed MSA to first cited answer, in sixty days.

Ninety days if the integration footprint is unusual. Each milestone has an SLA, and we credit usage if we miss it.

8 answers Five implementations completed to date. Median: 58 days.
Q · 01

How long does implementation take?

Standard is sixty days from MSA signature to the Console answering its first cited question. Deep — a custom HRIS, four-plus connectors, or a private deployment — is ninety. Both are written into the SOW and tied to milestone SLAs.

See the implementation timeline →
Q · 02

What do we (the customer) have to do?

Three things, sized small. Name an executive sponsor (usually the CHRO), name a technical lead (usually a senior People Ops manager), and clear about eight hours per week from each for the first thirty days. After that, the load drops to two hours weekly.

Q · 03

What if our HRIS is on a custom version of Workday?

Most Workday tenants are custom — that's normal. Our Workday connector handles common variations automatically; the genuinely odd objects (custom prompts, custom business processes that affect worker data) are mapped during implementation and reviewed with your Workday partner.

Q · 04

Will you connect to our custom internal HRIS?

Yes. Standard implementation includes one custom HRIS connector, built against your API by our Customer Engineering team and documented in your tenant. Additional custom connectors are scoped in the SOW.

Q · 05

Do you have implementation partners we can use?

Not yet, and we are honest about it. The first ten customers are implemented exclusively by Stratum Customer Engineers because we are still learning what the playbook should look like. We expect to name two or three certified partners in Q4 2026.

See the partner directory →
Q · 06

Who is our day-to-day contact after implementation?

A named Customer Engineer who knows your tenant, plus a Customer Success Manager for the QBR cadence. Both stay through year two — we do not hand you off to a generalist after launch.

Q · 07

What's the SLA on missed implementation milestones?

Each milestone has a hard date in the SOW. A miss caused by us credits two percent of annual platform fee per week of delay, capped at twelve percent. A miss caused by you (data not delivered, sponsor unavailable) extends but does not credit.

Q · 08

What if implementation takes longer than sixty days?

If it's our fault, the credit clause above applies and your Customer Engineer's bonus is impacted — incentive alignment we publish to customers on request. If it's the shape of the integration, we agree a revised plan with you in writing within five business days of the slip.

§ 06 · Hiring & culture

What it's like to work here, and why you might.

Honest answers about equity, sponsorship, and the trade we are asking you to make against a steadier company.

8 answers Four open roles · interview loop runs in seven business days.
Q · 01

Are you hiring?

Yes. Four roles open today, growing to about twelve through 2026. We hire deliberately — most months we close one role, not three.

See open roles →
Q · 02

What's it like to work at Stratum?

Small, written, and quiet. We default to a one-page memo over a fifteen-person meeting, we ship every Friday, and we close Slack at 7pm Pacific. The job is intense without being chaotic — that distinction matters to us.

Q · 03

How do you make decisions?

A written proposal with a recommendation, a forty-eight-hour comment window, then a named decider. We use the DACI model and publish the trail to the team. No decisions by Slack thread.

Q · 04

Do you support remote work?

Hybrid, anchored. Two anchor days in Palo Alto each week (Tuesday and Thursday), three flexible. For exceptional candidates outside the Bay Area we will discuss fully remote with a monthly visit.

Q · 05

How much equity do you offer?

Generously, for a company this small. Senior engineers in the first twenty hires receive between 0.3% and 1.5% on a four-year vest with a one-year cliff. We publish the band per level internally; we will share yours in writing before you sign.

Q · 06

What's your interview process?

Four stages over seven business days: a written take-home (paid), a technical conversation, a working session with the team you would join, and a values conversation with a founder. We close the loop within forty-eight hours of the last stage, yes or no.

See the loop in detail →
Q · 07

What's your visa / sponsorship policy?

We sponsor H-1B transfers, O-1s, and TN visas for senior engineering hires. We do not, today, sponsor first-time H-1B lottery candidates — the operational load is too high for a seven-person team. We will revisit by Q1 2027.

Q · 08

Why should I join Stratum versus a larger company?

Because you want your fingerprints on the product, on the culture, and on the eval set. A larger company will pay you more cash today and offer fewer surprises. We will pay you well, give you real equity, and ask you to help build a company you would want to recommend to your friends. That trade isn't for everyone.
