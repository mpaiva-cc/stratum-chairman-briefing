The 60-day implementation

From contract to first defensible board answer in sixty days.

Implementation is the part everyone in HCM lies about. Here is what actually happens, week by week, when you sign with Stratum — the people we put on it, the hours we ask of your team, and the four milestones we pay you back for if we miss.

Three principles

How a Stratum implementation is different from the deployments you have lived through.

No. 01

You ship in weeks, not quarters.

Median time to first connector live: 14 days. Median time to first CHRO Console session: 38 days. Median time to first board-answer-from-Stratum: 51 days. Those are not aspirational numbers; they are the arithmetic mean of our first three design partners — Mercator, Northshore, and Northwind — between February and April of this year. The remaining two of our five design partners are still inside the 60-day window as of this writing; we will revise these medians upward only when we have evidence to.

No. 02

We co-author your eval set, not just deploy a product.

Implementation is partially a research project. Your Customer Engineer will sit with your People Analytics team and turn the questions your board has actually asked over the last four quarters into a 100-question eval set that becomes the ground truth for our model on your data. We run that eval set nightly against your graph for the life of the contract. When your Chair asks a new question that the eval set didn't anticipate, we add it — and our nightly accuracy number is the one we report in the QBR, not a vendor-friendly proxy.

No. 03

We pay if we slip.

Every implementation has a four-milestone SLA with a clear remediation if any milestone misses by more than seven calendar days. The remediations are listed in plain numbers further down this page — 25%, 50%, a full month, and a joint review with our CEO. We do not hide them in a schedule of exhibits. We have not yet had to pay one out. If we miss, we want it to hurt us before it hurts you.

What we ask of your team

Four named roles. Real hours.

We will not pretend that a Stratum implementation is zero-effort for the customer. It is less effort than a Workday rollout — considerably less — but it is not nothing. Here is exactly what each of the four named roles on your side will own.

Role 01 — Sponsor

CHRO / Project sponsor

60 – 90 min / week, weeks 0 – 5 · then 30 min / week

The CHRO is the only sponsor a Stratum implementation needs. She owns the eval-set questions, ratifies the first graph snapshot, signs off the Implementation Acceptance Document, and delivers the first board answer alongside Vector in Week 8. Most CHROs find this is the highest-leverage hour of their week through the first six weeks.

Role 02 — Driver

People Analytics lead

4 – 6 hrs / week, weeks 1 – 6

The People Analytics lead is the technical counterpart to your Customer Engineer. She resolves identity conflicts in Week 3, ratifies the first graph snapshot, owns the eval-set expansion through Week 5, and becomes the first-line reviewer for any board-ready answer thereafter. This role is the hero of implementation. We design it that way.

Role 03 — Reviewer

IT Security liaison

8 – 12 hrs total · weeks 1 – 2 + weeks 6 – 7

The IT Security liaison joins the architecture session in Week 2, signs off on field-mapping and row-level access control, then re-engages at production cut-over in Week 6 to review the pen-test rerun. We hand her the entire Trust packet — SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR, EU AI Act stance — in Week 0; most enterprise CISOs need only one follow-up call.

Role 04 — Specialist

Performance Mgmt owner

2 – 3 hrs total · concentrated in week 4

The Performance Management owner reviews our flight-risk and comp-band methodologies in Week 4. This is mostly reading our methodology document and one 60-minute Q&A with our team. We surface our model assumptions in writing — including the parts where reasonable HR leaders disagree with them — and we record her dissent in the methodology log we hand back.

Frequently asked, plainly answered.

What happens if our HRIS data is messier than you expect?

We expect mess. Every customer's data is messier than they think — typically 8 – 15% of records require some human review at the identity-resolution step in Week 3. We do not treat this as a defect; we treat it as the work. Your People Analytics lead will spend four to six hours on it. If your data is meaningfully worse than that band — say, more than 25% conflicts — we will tell you in Week 1, in writing, and propose a revised schedule before you have spent the cost of the implementation.

Do you require us to migrate off Workday?

No. Stratum is the system of truth on top of your record systems, not a replacement for them. We expect you to keep running Workday (or SAP, or Oracle, or whatever you already own) as your HRIS of record. We read from it; we do not ask you to leave it. Several of our design partners have actively renewed their Workday contracts during their Stratum implementation — this is normal and we encourage it.

Can we test the eval set during evaluation?

Yes. A thirty-question evaluation eval set is part of the pre-contract evaluation phase. We run it against an anonymised slice of your data, in our environment, and we publish the accuracy score to your team before you sign. We do not pick the thirty questions; you do — drawn from your real board questions of the last two quarters. This is the test we want you to run on us, not the test we want to run on ourselves.

What if our CISO has questions you've never heard before?

Then we have learned something. Our security packet — SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, GDPR + DPA, EU AI Act stance, full sub-processor list — answers most of what enterprise CISOs ask. For the rest, your CISO gets a direct line to Forge, our Head of Security, who will join one hour of her office hours if she wants. We have not yet had a CISO question we could not answer in writing inside five business days.

Does the Customer Engineer stay with us?

Through Day 90, yes. Vector (or whoever your CE is) will sit with your team daily for the first two weeks, twice weekly through Week 8, and weekly through Day 90. On Day 90 the relationship transitions to a named Customer Success Manager, with Vector available on escalation for the remainder of the first year. We do not move your CE off the account inside the first 12 months unless you ask.

What is the most painful part of implementation, honestly?

Identity conflicts in Week 3. The first time your People Analytics lead opens a list of 180 cases — same person under three email addresses, same role spelled four ways, two managers claiming the same direct report — it is an uncomfortable mirror to hold up to your own data. We do not try to hide it. We make your People Analytics lead the hero of resolving them: she gets a clean review queue, a triage protocol, and a writeup of every decision she makes so that the next time her CHRO asks "why is this number what it is?", the answer is already in front of her.

We will spend more time on your implementation than any other vendor — because the eval set we build with you is the moat.
