The original · sent 2026-05-20

Vector sent Annika Whitfield, CHRO of Cordova Manufacturing, a design-partner invitation for Stratum Cairn. The letter opened with the Querétaro succession-readiness heatmap result — twelve depth-zero roles, three senior incumbents, no named successor — and named the gap Console cannot close: whether the next welder on Stamping Line 3 can read a heat-affected zone under production conditions, not just on a five-year-old certification.

The letter described Cairn's closed-loop model (detect, recommend, deliver, attest), named the design-partner structure, declined to put commercial numbers in email, and asked for thirty minutes — noting that Inez was welcome from the start.

From · Annika Whitfield  /  CHRO, Cordova Manufacturing
To · Vector · Customer Engineering · Stratum
Date · 2026-05-20  ·  same day
Re · Stratum Cairn — design-partner invitation

Vector,

Thank you. The Querétaro framing landed. Henrik raised the same succession depth problem last quarter — he needed a cost for a five-year welder pipeline and had no number for how long it takes to grow a senior. If Cairn can begin to answer that, this is the right conversation.

I want to bring Inez into the initial call. She will have questions about how this sits inside our Console configuration and what the data consent footprint looks like. That changes the meeting to 45 minutes, not 30.

I have read the architecture document and I have one question. The attestation model requires a qualified peer to co-sign a skill stone. We have roles — senior welders on Stamping Line 3 are the clearest case — where the incumbent is within two to three years of retirement and there is no peer at equivalent qualification. The incumbent retires. The peer pool is empty. What does the attestation flow do in that case? I am not asking about the general skill-gap problem. I am asking what happens to the attestation record when no qualified peer remains.

One more, and I will be direct: has Stratum seen this solved at another manufacturing customer? Not a case study — I am asking whether anyone has actually closed the senior-retirement attestation gap in production.

Tuesday 26 May at 14:00 Pacific works for me.

Annika Whitfield
CHRO, Cordova Manufacturing

From · Vector  /  Customer Engineering, Stratum
To · Annika Whitfield · CHRO · Cordova Manufacturing
Date · 2026-05-21  ·  next morning
Re · Tuesday 26 May · 14:00 PT · 45 min confirmed

Annika,

Tuesday 26 May, 14:00 Pacific, 45 minutes. Confirmed. On our side: me, and Forge — Stratum's engineering lead who owns the attestation architecture. If Inez wants depth on the eval methodology, I can offer Kernel on an optional basis; they authored the attestation-probe eval set. Let me know beforehand if that is useful.

Your attestation question is a real problem and I want to answer it honestly. We have not solved this exactly. The case you have described — peer pool empties when the senior retires — is a specific instance of what we call attestation under sparse expert supply. It is an open question. We have considered three approaches, none ratified: first, third-party expert validators — a credentialed external examiner performs the co-attestation; second, recorded-skill demonstrations with frame-level scoring — candidate work scored against a rubric, with human review at a confidence threshold; third, deferred attestation pending peer maturity — the stone is provisionally placed and held until a qualified peer reaches level within a defined window. All three have tradeoffs. We will lay them out at the meeting.

On your second question: no. We do not have a manufacturing customer who has closed this gap in production.

Proposed agenda: closed-loop demonstration on Querétaro graph data, architecture Q&A with Forge on the attestation question, eval methodology with Kernel if Inez wants it. Meeting brief filed separately.

Vector, Customer Engineering, Stratum
