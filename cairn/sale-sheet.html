§ 01 · The Headline

Most learning platforms tell you who finished. Cairn tells you who changed.

Cairn is Stratum's learning product — an agentic layer that detects skill gaps from the work people are already doing, closes them in under fifteen minutes, and verifies the close by watching the next thing they ship. No catalog. No "journey." A waymarker.

§ 02 · The Five-Line Pitch

Cairn is the learning product for HCM that treats a closed skill gap as the unit of work — not a completed course.

It reads the gap from real work signals on the people graph, then proposes a 90-second remediation in the tool the person is already in.

It verifies closure by observing the next artifact, not by scoring a quiz.

It does not produce content. It rides on top of the best libraries — Pluralsight, Coursera, Credly — and adds the closed loop.

And it carries the full Stratum audit posture: every recommendation defensible against the role graph, the work graph, and a deadline.

§ 03 · What It Does · What It Does Not

Three things, not thirty. And a list of refusals.

What Cairn does

  1. Detects skill gaps from work. Reads commits, doc edits, ticket resolutions, meeting summaries — under consent — and infers the delta between the role's claimed skills and the person's demonstrated skills. Sources every inference to the artifact it came from.
  2. Prescribes a 90-second remediation in-context. Surfaces a single nudge in Slack, the IDE, or a doc comment. Pulls the actual lesson content from a licensed partner library; cites the source by name and the company manual when grounded.
  3. Attests the close by watching the next artifact. Marks a gap "closed" only when the behaviour shows up in subsequent work — not when "complete" is clicked. Writes the attestation to the people graph; renders it as a verifiable credential.

What Cairn does not do

  1. No catalog of courses. We license content; we do not write it. Coursera, Pluralsight, O'Reilly, and Credly are partners, not competitors. If you are buying a catalog, buy theirs.
  2. No generative tutoring on regulated content. HIPAA, OSHA, SOX, anti-bribery, EEO — the agent surfaces the manual passage and the partner's certified module. It does not paraphrase. Not in 2026; possibly not ever.
  3. No learning data sold, brokered, or used to train third-party models. Learner records are protected employment-record class by default. The agent attests; it does not export.
§ 04 · The Closed Loop

The shape of the work.

Four steps. The catalog vendors do step three. Stratum is the only HCM that closes step four against the same graph that powers Recruiter and Tenure.

Detect · Prescribe · Deliver · Attest. Tessera will redraw this in Inkscape; the shape stays.
§ 05 · Pricing

Three skeletons. One recommended.

L&D buyers want a per-EE line because it forecasts cleanly. CFOs want outcome-based pricing because it disciplines the vendor. Cairn's hybrid model gives each one the half they need — and protects Stratum from getting paid for activity instead of value.

Option A · Safe
Per-EE
$18–28 / EE / yr
Platform floor $80K

The boring HCM convention. Forecasts cleanly through procurement; aligns to Recruiter and Tenure lines on the same invoice. Loses the outcome story — pays us whether a gap closes or not.

Option B · Outcome
Per-gap-closed
$60–120 / attested skill
No platform floor

The pure outcome line. Defensible to a CFO; impossible to forecast through a CHRO's annual budget cycle. Variable cost the buyer cannot model. Procurement bounces it.

Recommended
Option C · Hybrid
Base + activation fee
Platform $80K + $14 / EE / yr
+ $35 / attested skill

A per-EE base that procurement can model, plus a per-attested-skill activation fee that ties price to value. Customers know what they are buying — and what they are paying when the loop closes.

Why Option C — the eighty-word rationale

The hybrid is the only model that lets a CHRO forecast a budget line and a CFO defend the spend in the same breath. The per-EE base ($14, deliberately undercutting Option A) underwrites platform cost and keeps the line item legible to procurement. The per-attested-skill fee ($35) means we get paid when Cairn does what it claims — close a gap, attest it, render the credential. We hold pricing discipline: no discount through partners, no match on platform fee, value match only on the activation tier.

§ 07 · Ninety-Day ROI · Falsifiable Claims

Three things a customer can measure. Three things we will let you falsify.

If any of these fail in the first 90 days of a design-partner engagement, the activation tier is paused and Compass writes the customer note explaining why. We publish before the outcome is certain.

Claim 01 · Detection

≥ 70% of detected skill gaps trace to a specific work artifact within 30 days.

Falsifiable. If Cairn cannot show you the commit, ticket, or document that triggered a recommendation, the recommendation does not ship to the learner. Measured weekly; published to the customer's audit log.

Claim 02 · Closure

≥ 40% of opened gaps close within 14 days of first remediation.

Falsifiable. Closure requires an attested behaviour change in a subsequent artifact — not a "complete" click. Measured per cohort; reported alongside open-but-not-closed rate.

Claim 03 · Time

Median learner time per remediation under 4 minutes across the first 90 days.

Falsifiable. If the median crosses 4 minutes, the product is failing Echo's voice principle on respect-for-time and we say so. Reported with p50 / p90 visible.
