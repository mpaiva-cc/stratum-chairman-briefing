Carmen, this brief is the working document for your August 12 visit. You read the stones ledger before you accepted. You asked to see the methodology disclosure before agreeing to validate. You retained the explicit right to refuse to sign if either the stone or the methodology fails your standard. All three framings are intact.

I am writing eight days out so you have time to push back on anything that does not match what we discussed by phone on 2026-07-30, and to arrive without surprises. If anything here overstates your scope or misrepresents your role, tell me before you board the plane and we will correct it on the public record and resend.

§ 01 What you are being asked to do

Three things. Each is a discrete act with its own standard. You can complete any subset and decline the rest.

  1. Validate Stone 002 against your professional standard

    Examine the four weld plates Mateo produced between 2026-07-10 and 2026-07-22 (detailed in § 03) against AWS D1.1. Apply your own acceptance criteria, your own tooling, your own sequence. The standard is yours; we are not asking you to soften it or to treat the visual classifier’s judgment as primary.

  2. Observe Mateo weld a fifth plate

    Lap joint, 3/8″ plate — same configuration as plate 3, which he initially failed for under-penetration and re-welded on his own initiative one week later. He will weld plate 5 in your presence at plant 3 on the morning of 2026-08-12. Material specs and the WPS will be on the bench. Full detail in § 04.

  3. Review the Hearth visual classifier’s plate analyses

    Hearth produced a one-page analysis for each of the four plates. We hand you those outputs at the plant. Tell us where it agreed with you, where it disagreed, and where it missed something subtle. This is the part of the visit that most directly improves the system; see also § 08.

§ 02 What you are not being asked to do

If anyone in the room leans on you for any of the following, redirect them to me by phone (§ 06).

  1. Validate the eval methodology more broadly

    That is Counsel’s and Kernel’s territory. Their 014-T predicate review is on the ledger and is not yours to ratify or contest. Your scope is one stone, four plates, one new plate, and a classifier output set.

  2. Validate the people-graph architecture

    That is Forge’s. Questions about how Cairn constructs provenance chains or enforces the provisional flag go to Forge through me. You do not need a position on any of that for your attestation to stand.

  3. Endorse Stratum or Cairn as a product

    You are not a Stratum partisan. Nothing about this engagement is conditioned on a favorable view of the company. If the methodology or the stone fail your standard you have the explicit right to say so on the record (§ 07, outcome three).

  4. Sign anything outside AWS D1.1

    All five plates are in D1.1 scope: butt joint (plate 1), T-joint fillet (plate 2), lap joint ×2 (plates 3 and 5), corner joint (plate 4). If anything at the plant falls outside that code, flag it and we pull it from scope immediately.

§ 04 The fifth plate — your observation

We are asking Mateo to weld a fifth plate in your presence because plate 3 carries the interesting history: a failure he caught, documented, and corrected without prompting. The re-weld passed. What a static record cannot tell us is whether that corrective behavior was a one-time event or a repeatable habit. The fifth plate asks that question to his hands rather than to a file.

Plate 05
Lap joint · 3/8″ plate · same WPS as plate 03 Morning of 2026-08-12 · plant 3 inspection bay · Querétaro

Material. A36 carbon steel, 3/8″, cut and surface-prepped by the bay leadhand the day prior. Wire-brushed, no mill scale, no oil. Tack-up to your satisfaction; if a tack does not satisfy you, Mateo re-tacks.

Procedure. WPS-CDA-LJ-038 — same as both the plate 3 initial weld and the re-weld. GMAW, ER70S-6 wire, 0.045″, 75/25 Ar/CO₂. Travel speed, voltage, and wire feed logged. WPS document on the bench.

Your role. Observer. You set the pace. Pause at any point to inspect before the next pass. Ask Mateo to narrate or have him work in silence. One plate is in scope; a second plate under altered conditions is a separate engagement and a separate invoice.

Photography. Hearth photographs plate 05 under controlled lighting after Mateo finishes. Classifier output available in the afternoon. Whether you review it before or after your own inspection is your call; see § 05.

You asked on the phone whether we would brief Mateo on what outcome we were hoping for. We did not. Annika and Tomás Herrera have been told that your judgment — including a judgment that he is not ready — ends the visit on its own terms.

§ 05 What the visual classifier said

Hearth’s analysis for each plate: a pass/fail verdict, a confidence score, a heat-map of the regions the model weighted, and a three-to-five sentence text rationale. For plate 3 you receive two outputs — the initial fail and the re-weld pass. Hearth was trained on approximately 14,000 AWS D1.1 plates from public inspection datasets and a Cordova-specific augmentation set; provenance for both training sets is documented and available on request.

The order of review is your call. Some inspectors prefer to form a judgment before reading the model output, to avoid anchoring. Others prefer to read the model first and treat it as a checklist. Both are defensible. We hand you the outputs in a sealed folder; open them when you choose. We note the order on the ledger but do not interpret it as a quality signal.

§ 07 What we will do with your findings

Three outcomes are possible. All three are written down in advance so that no part of Stratum’s response is improvised. You asked for this on our July 30 call; this is it.

  • If you sign

    Stone 002 moves from PROVISIONAL to VERIFIED on the public ledger within 48 hours. Your name, certification body (Lincoln Electric), and inspection date appear in the attestation chain per engagement letter clause 4. No downstream record for Mateo changes without a separate, individually documented ledger entry.

  • If you don’t sign but find it defensible with conditions

    We publish your conditions verbatim on the ledger. Stone 002 remains PROVISIONAL with those conditions as a permanent public part of its record. The wording belongs to you. We do not rewrite it, summarize it, or conceal it.

  • If you refuse to sign

    We delete Stone 002 from the graph. Your statement — in your words, any length — is published on the ledger as the closing record for that stone, and we report to Counsel. We do not retaliate, we do not argue on the ledger, we do not ask you to revise. The entry reads under your name with the version-stamp of the methodology you found wanting. You charge for your time at the same rate as any other outcome.

§ 08 The classifier-improvement clause

Entirely voluntary. If while examining the plates you notice one thing Hearth missed — acceptable to D1.1 but a leading indicator a senior inspector would flag: a subtle ripple pattern, a toe angle that signals travel speed variation, a tack geometry that will stress under thermal cycling, a porosity cluster below the acceptance threshold but worth monitoring — please name it specifically and on the record.

We will use the finding to retrain Hearth. Your name does not attach to the model version release. We are not asking you to grade the classifier; we are asking you to teach it one thing.
