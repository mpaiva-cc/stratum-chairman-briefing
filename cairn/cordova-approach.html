Cairn — Design-partner approach to Cordova.

The first-approach letter from Vector to Annika Whitfield, CHRO, inviting Cordova Manufacturing to be Stratum Cairn's first design partner. Includes the methodology disclosure, follow-up plan, and meeting-prep checklist.

From Vector·Customer Engineering·2026-05-20·Design-partner outreach record
Date · 2026-05-20
To · Annika Whitfield, CHRO · Cordova Manufacturing
From · Vector · Customer Engineering · Stratum
Re · Stratum Cairn — design-partner invitation

Annika,

I keep thinking about the Querétaro result. When you typed that succession-readiness query in August — the same question you had been carrying for sixty hours over six months — the Console returned twelve depth-zero roles, three senior incumbents over sixty, and no named successor at any readiness level. You read the heatmap three times, pushed your chair back, and said Marcelo needed it before Friday. That was the right answer to the question you already knew how to ask. What it could not do was tell you what happens to those three roles in the thirty-six months it takes to grow a successor. Specifically: how do you know, before the incumbent walks out the door, that the next welder on Stamping Line 3 can actually read a heat-affected zone under production conditions — and not just on a certification exam he passed five years ago?

That gap is where skill retention in a manufacturing company actually lives. It is not in a training catalog. It is not in a performance rating. It lives in the artifacts of real work — the weldment sign-off, the dimensional inspection that caught the deviation, the shift handoff note that named the problem before the next crew found it at cost. The challenge for Cordova is not that this evidence does not exist. It is that nothing in your current stack reads it, maps it to the role requirement, and writes it back to the succession record.

The reason I am writing is that the Compact ratified Stratum Cairn this morning. Cairn closes the loop that Console opens. It reads consented work signals, detects where a demonstrated skill lags a role requirement, proposes a bounded remediation grounded in a partner content library, and then watches the next artifact to confirm whether the gap actually closed. When it does, it signs the verification and writes a portable credential to the people graph. One stone per attested skill. We have a record that the next welder can read a heat-affected zone because he read one, last Tuesday, on a real weldment, and the stone is in the graph.

The Compact named Cordova as the first design partner for Cairn. That is a deliberate choice — it reflects what you have already shown us you need. The design-partner structure is the same one we used for Console and Tenure: bounded scope, weekly reviews, the right to terminate at any time without cause, and the right to review everything before it publishes. Commercial terms are in the sale sheet and I will not put a number in an email before Henrik has seen the architecture.

The ask is thirty minutes next week. I would walk you through what we are proposing, show you where Cairn would sit inside Cordova's existing Console and Tenure data, and hear whether the manufacturing-floor context changes the scope. I am available any afternoon. Bring Inez from the start if that is useful — I will come prepared.

Looking forward to it. Vector, Customer Engineering, Stratum.
Methodology disclosure  ·  commitments before send
  • Voice reviewed by Echo This letter was reviewed by Echo against Vector's voice protocol before transmission. Warmth, specificity, and the absence of oversell confirmed. No claims were added in the review pass.
  • Factual claims verified against the people-graph data Every specific claim about Cordova's workforce situation — the twelve depth-zero roles, the three incumbents over sixty, the Querétaro context — is sourced from Cordova's own Console data produced at T+118d. No data from any other Stratum customer is used in this letter.
  • Pricing aligned with Briefing 017 §IV The letter references the design-partner activation-fee waiver as ratified in Briefing 017 §IV ($80K platform floor + $14/EE/yr + $35/attested-skill). No commercial terms appear in the letter body; the letter directs Annika to the sale sheet and names Henrik as the right audience for the price conversation.
  • No data-training claims The letter makes no representation that Cordova's data will train any Stratum model. Cairn trains on the synthetic graph and public taxonomies (O*NET, ESCO) per Briefing 017 §VIII, non-negotiable 1.
  • No engagement-score promises The letter makes no claim about engagement scores, retention lift, or any metric that Stratum has not measured in a production Cairn deployment. The value claim is structural — attested skill visible in the people graph — not numeric.
Follow-up plan  ·  if no response to initial letter
+3d  ·  2026-05-25
One short note offering to send the Cairn architecture link and sale sheet in advance of any conversation. No second ask for a meeting. A door left open, not a knock.
+7d  ·  2026-05-27
If still no response: a single line acknowledging the timing may be difficult, naming the week of June 2 as an alternative window. This is the last outbound touch in this window. We do not escalate; we do not copy Henrik or Inez without Annika's direction. She sets the cadence.
+14d  ·  2026-06-03
If no response by this date, Vector moves the Cordova Cairn thread to a standing item on the quarterly review agenda and waits for Annika's signal. No further outbound contact until she opens the conversation or until the next scheduled QBR. Cordova's leadership has earned the right to set the cadence.
What we are bringing to the meeting
  • Product tour Cairn product tour — the guided walkthrough of detect, recommend, deliver, and attest. Screen-by-screen. No registration required for Annika's session.
  • Sale sheet Cairn sale sheet — the pricing summary, design-partner terms, and the hybrid model rationale (Briefing 017 §IV). Henrik's copy of the data-flow diagram is attached as the second page.
  • Technical architecture Cairn architecture document — the four-step loop, the schema additions (cairn_stone, skill_gap, trajectory, attestation), the 014-T autonomy predicate, and the consent-renewal axis. Inez will want this before the second call.
  • Partner-content shortlist Cairn partnership thesis — the three named content partners (Pluralsight, Coursera, Credly), integration depth for each, and the portable-credential rail that Credly supplies for manufacturing-trade attestations.
