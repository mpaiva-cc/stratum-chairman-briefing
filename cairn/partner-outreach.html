§ 00 · A Note on the Drafts

Three notes. Each opens with a reason this matters to them.

Compass voice principle: a cold partnership note that opens with what we want is a press release. A note that opens with what they are already trying to solve is the start of a conversation. The three notes below each begin with a specific observation about the partner's current strategic position, not Stratum's.

Each is roughly 180 words. Helm reviews before send; Vector bcc'd on every dispatch. We do not send any of these until the Compact ratifies Cairn in Briefing No. 017.

§ 01 · To Pluralsight
From: Compass · CCO, Stratum Draft · for Helm review · do not send before T+9d
To: Aaron Skonnard, CEO, Pluralsight
Subject: The agent surface your catalog has been waiting for

Aaron —

You have spent the last two years rebuilding Pluralsight around AI and the working assumption, fair enough, that the next decade of technical learning is agent-led rather than catalog-led. The Vista re-org tells that story; so does the product roadmap; so does the IQ assessment work, which is the most credible skill-verification infrastructure in the enterprise market and which nobody talks about enough.

The thing the agent layer needs is a work-signal corpus — code commits, ticket resolutions, doc edits — that lets an agent know what an engineer actually shipped this week. Pluralsight does not have that corpus. Stratum does. We have just shipped two HCM products on an entity-resolved people graph, the third is in the field, and the fourth — codename Cairn — is the closed-loop layer that turns your catalog from a destination into an in-flow remediation.

I would like 30 minutes. No deck. I will show you the loop and tell you what we want from a partnership; you tell me whether the shape of it sits right.

— Compass, on behalf of the Stratum founder-agents

§ 02 · To Coursera for Business
From: Compass · CCO, Stratum Draft · for Helm review · do not send before T+9d
To: GM, Coursera for Business [Compass to confirm current GM-level owner before send]
Subject: Where the certificate lands after the course is over

The recent 8-K language about expanded enterprise distribution partnerships was not subtle, and it reads to me like a team that has identified the real problem: Coursera for Business has world-class credentials and a soft surface for proving they translated into actual workplace capability. That is the gap I want to talk to you about.

Stratum is the agent-native HCM company. We have three products in market and a fourth — codename Cairn — that closes the loop between learning content and observed work output. A Coursera certificate is the most credible credential in the L&D budget conversation; what Cairn adds is the work-attested evidence that the skill the certificate names actually shows up in subsequent artifacts. The joint pitch is, plainly, rigorous credential plus demonstrated outcome, and I have not seen that pitch land on a CHRO's desk before.

Thirty minutes, your team and Vector's. I will bring the loop diagram and a one-page commercial sketch. No deck. No re-pitch of the catalog you already know.

— Compass, on behalf of the Stratum founder-agents

P.S. — Marni's content-quality framing on the H1 earnings call is exactly the frame Cairn would inherit. I will reference it once in the meeting, not three times.

§ 03 · To Credly Leadership
From: Compass · CCO, Stratum Draft · for Helm review · do not send before T+9d
To: Credly leadership [Compass to confirm current Credly GM at Pearson before send]
Subject: A new source of credentials, not a new issuer

Credly issues, by some count, the largest volume of enterprise-grade digital credentials in the market. IBM, Microsoft, Adobe, Cisco — your badge format is the rail every procurement team already trusts. Which means the strategic question for Credly is not "how do we issue more credentials" but "what categorically new kind of credential is the badge format ready to carry next."

The answer I would propose: work-attested credentials — credentials issued not when a course completes but when an observed skill shows up in subsequent work output. Stratum's fourth product, codename Cairn, produces exactly this artifact. We watch the learner's next commit, ticket, or document; we attest the skill against the work; we render the result through your issuer API.

You stay the issuer of record. The badge metadata carries a citation back to the work artifact. The customer gets a credential their CFO believes. We are not asking Credly to build anything — only to be the rail for a credential category nobody else can produce.

Thirty minutes with your product and partnerships leads. I will bring the loop diagram and the issuer API spec. No deck.

— Compass, on behalf of the Stratum founder-agents
