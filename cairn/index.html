§ 01 · The wedge

The incumbents sell catalogs.
We sell closed gaps.

"Every recommendation our learning agent makes is traceable to a role requirement, a work artifact, and a defensible deadline. Not a course completion rate."
§ 01 · The signal others can't see

Gap detection from work output, not surveys.

Cornerstone, Degreed, Workday Learning — they all start from a self-assessment or a manager's nomination. Cairn starts from what your people actually ship. Code commits. Pull request patterns. Document edits. Support case resolutions. The agent reads the work and infers the gap. No survey required.

§ 02 · The loop others can't close

Closed-loop verification by observing subsequent work.

After an intervention, Cairn watches what the learner builds next. If the behavior changed, the stone is placed. If it didn't, the intervention was wrong — and the agent says so. This is the ROI proof that L&D leaders have been asking for since Josh Bersin wrote the first Definitive Guide.

§ 03 · The graph others don't own

Grounded in the same people graph as Recruiter and Tenure.

Skill claims at hire (Recruiter) → demonstrated skills at 90 days (Tenure) → ongoing skill maintenance (Cairn). One entity-resolved person across the full lifecycle. Workday is bolting Sana onto a monolith; we shipped this graph from the start.

§ 02 · The closed loop

Four steps. One closed loop.

Every gap Cairn closes passes through the same four stages. Nothing moves to the next stage automatically — each transition is disclosed to the employee and the manager.

Step 01
Detect
Signal in, gap identified.

Talus reads work signals — commits, PRs, docs, tickets, peer review patterns — and cross-references against the role expectation in the people graph. Gap identified when observed behavior diverges from role requirement.

Example: Marisol's last 3 PRs used StatefulSets where Kubernetes Operator patterns would have been more idiomatic. Talus flags: gap in k8s-operator-authoring vs Staff SRE requirement.
Step 02
Recommend
Intervention proposed with provenance.

Hearth proposes a specific, time-bounded intervention. Not a catalog link — a matched module, a peer-pair, an in-context nudge. Every recommendation arrives with its provenance chain: why this, why now, why this colleague.

Example: Hearth proposes: (1) Pluralsight "Kubernetes Operators Deep Dive" — 4h, (2) peer-pair with Dev Kapoor who shipped an Operator controller in the last sprint. Deadline: before next architecture review.
Step 03
Deliver
In context, not in an inbox.

The intervention surfaces in the tool the person is already using — a Slack DM, an IDE overlay comment, a doc thread — not as an email to a calendar block. Ninety seconds, not ninety minutes.

Example: Module link surfaced in Marisol's IDE when she opens a file with StatefulSet imports. Peer-pair calendar invite sent through existing scheduling integration. No separate LMS login required.
Step 04
Attest
Observed, verified, stone placed.

Hearth watches Marisol's next PR. If the Operator pattern appears — in context, correctly — the stone is placed. Auto-attestation triggers. Manager sign-off is requested for the skill addition to her profile. The stone is hers to keep.

Example: PR #4891 implements a custom Operator controller. Hearth verifies the pattern, triggers attestation, requests manager sign-off from Dev Kapoor. Gap closed; trajectory updated.
§ 03 · The agent roster

Two agents. One closed loop.

Cairn adds two agents to the Stratum roster. Each has a single job, a defined failure budget, and a hard stop at the edge of its autonomy predicate.

Talus
Detection agent · read-only by design

Talus reads the accumulated work-artifact and attestation nodes for a person against their role-requirement node. It infers which skills are present, which are missing, and which are stale. When the delta clears its confidence threshold, it proposes a gap and a matched remediation — then stops. It never delivers an intervention itself; that boundary is structural, not a policy. Every detection report is visible to the employee before Hearth acts on it.

  • Reads work artifacts, attestations, and role requirements from the graph
  • Proposes gaps and matched remediations — never delivers them
  • Surfaces detection methodology and confidence score alongside every gap
  • No learner-facing writes — Hearth owns delivery and verification
  • Failure budget: grounding-failure rate, reviewed monthly
Hearth
Recommendation + attestation agent · human-in-loop

Hearth proposes interventions, coordinates delivery, and observes subsequent work to verify gap closure. It matches the gap to the right content partner (Pluralsight, Coursera, Credly), the right peer in the org, and the right moment. After the intervention, it watches for behavioral change in work output. Attestation is proposed; manager sign-off is always required to write to the employee's skill profile.

  • Proposes matched interventions with full provenance chain
  • Coordinates content delivery with partner integrations
  • Surfaces peer-match recommendations from people graph
  • Observes subsequent work for behavioral verification
  • Proposes attestation — never writes unilaterally to profile
§ 04 · What Cairn is not

Three things we explicitly refuse to be.

The category is crowded with systems that promise transformation and deliver catalogs. Cairn is designed against three specific failure modes.

Not a content library

We do not compete on catalog depth.

Coursera has years of university content. Pluralsight has the best technical skill library in the market. LinkedIn Learning is bundled into every enterprise seat. We license from them. We do not try to out-catalog them. Cairn's value is the closed loop, not the content.

Not a chat tutor

We do not put an LLM in a chat window and call it learning.

"Agentic learning" in 2026 is mostly an AI that answers questions. That is table stakes — Sana, Khanmigo, and Microsoft 365 Copilot all ship it. Cairn does what a chat tutor cannot: it closes a specific gap against a specific role on a specific deadline, with evidence.

Not a compliance checkbox

We do not confuse completion records with skill evidence.

HIPAA completion is a compliance event, not a learning outcome. Cairn handles regulated training as a special case — the agent surfaces, delivers, and logs it — but completion data lives in a separate, auditable record class. We will not let compliance theater crowd out the actual work of skill development.

§ 05 · The architecture

Cairn-stones aggregate into Trajectories in the people graph.

Each verified skill event is a stone. Stones cluster into Trajectories. Trajectories are first-class nodes on the same graph as hire records, onboarding events, and team memberships. The graph is how promotion decisions become defensible — not because the manager says so, but because the work says so.

What a cairn-stone is.

A stone is a verified skill event. Not a course completion. Not a manager endorsement. A verified demonstration of a skill in real work output, attested by the agent and confirmed by a human reviewer. Stones are durable — they move with the person across roles, departments, and even companies (with appropriate consent).

What a Trajectory is.

A Trajectory is the collection of stones between a person's current role and their target role in the people graph. The agent calculates what stones are missing, in what sequence they're most efficiently acquired, and what the current velocity implies for the promotion timeline. No Trajectory is ever invisible to the employee — they see the same data the manager sees.

Why this matters for decisions.

When a manager proposes a promotion or a lateral move, the Trajectory is evidence, not opinion. The CHRO can see why one person was ready for Staff and another wasn't — not because someone said so, but because the work graph says so.

§ 06 · Compliance posture
EU AI Act · Aug 2 2026 effective date. AI systems that evaluate learning outcomes are classified as high-risk under Annex III. Cairn's architecture accounts for this from day one: documented risk-management system, bias detection on assessment outputs, six-month minimum logging for any attested skill event, and a disclosed human-oversight point at every write to a skill profile. No attestation is written without manager sign-off — this is not a policy choice, it is a hard system constraint.

NYC AEDT / Local Law 144. Cairn outputs that feed into promotion or compensation decisions trigger AEDT obligations. We have architected a clean boundary between "learning evidence" (Cairn) and "talent decision" (Console). The learning evidence informs; the human decides. Pillar owns the compliance tier table; Kernel owns the autonomy predicate. Both reviewed before B017 ratification.

Accessibility · WCAG 2.2 AAA. All dynamically-generated learning content — including AI-authored nudges and module summaries — must pass WCAG 2.2 AA on generation. The Cairn UI targets AAA. This is not aspirational; it is a standing O-1 objective with a public gap log.
