Section 2 · Stratum's objectives

What we want from the meeting.

Two outcomes, ranked. If the meeting closes only one, it must be the first.

  • Primary · MOU signed within 5 business days post-meeting Cordova commits to the design-partner program. Terms: zero activation-tier cost for the first 100 attested skill stones; full $80K platform base begins year-2; per-EE charge at $14 applied to Cordova's manufacturing-floor scope from go-live; per-stone charge at $35 on stones beyond the first-100 tier. Vector walks through these numbers in the pricing slot (minutes 30–40). Annika has the authority to countersign under $200K/year without board approval; the existing customer note confirms this. The preferred outcome is Annika signs in the room. If she takes it to her board, five business days is the outer bound.
  • Secondary · Architecture-input answer on the attestation question Which of the three approaches to attestation under sparse expert supply does Annika find most workable for the senior-retirement case at Stamping Line 3? This is not a contract decision and must not be framed as one. It is an architecture-input decision: Forge needs Annika's directional preference before committing engineering weeks to any one approach. Vector takes notes; no conclusion is committed at the meeting.
Section 3 · Cordova's objectives

What Cordova wants from the meeting.

Based on the email thread and the prior Tenure beta relationship, Cordova comes into this call with three questions. All three are answerable. The third is conditional on Henrik joining on time.

  • First · Confidence that Cairn is real, not a deck Annika replied within hours of the approach letter. She read the architecture document before writing back. She is not evaluating a pitch; she is deciding whether Cairn is at a stage where Cordova's time and data are worth committing. The Querétaro demonstration (minutes 05–20) answers this directly: a live closed-loop run on real Cordova data, with methodology-disclosure panels visible. No synthetic employees. No Marisol Ortega.
  • Second · Architecture answer on the senior-retirement question Annika asked a specific question in her reply: what happens to the attestation record when no qualified peer remains? She is not asking about the general skill-gap problem. She is asking about a specific failure mode. Forge presents the three approaches in minutes 20–30. No ranking. Annika and Inez react. The session ends with Cordova's directional preference documented.
  • Third · Clarity on pricing Henrik joins for the pricing slot only. His question, documented in the customer note (original annotation: "If commercial conversion is proposed, please bring proposed pricing to me with the data-flow diagram attached."), is whether he can hold the Cairn commercial model in his head in ten minutes. Vector walks through the hybrid model. Henrik will want the cyclicality question answered.
Section 4 · 45-minute agenda

The 45-minute agenda.

Times are targets, not walls. The only hard constraint: Henrik joins at 30 and leaves at 40. Do not let the attestation discussion overrun into his slot.

00–05
Welcome + scope

Vector opens. The methodology framing: what Cairn does and, critically, what it does not do. Cairn is a closed-loop learning agent — detect, recommend, deliver, attest — not a workforce-planning dashboard. Inez and Annika have both read the architecture; this is orientation, not introduction.

05–20
Closed-loop demo on Querétaro data

Forge runs the demonstration. The data set is Cordova's own Querétaro graph — the same twelve depth-zero roles, three senior incumbents, no named successor that the approach letter named. The employee used in the detect–recommend–deliver–attest run is a real Cordova employee whose data Annika has cleared for use. Vector to confirm identity with Annika 24 hours before the call. Marisol Ortega is not used; she was synthetic and is retired from this engagement.

Methodology-disclosure panels must be visible throughout. If Forge is screensharing, pin the disclosure panel before starting the run.
20–30
The senior-retirement attestation question

Forge presents the three approaches named in the email acknowledgment. No ranking; Stratum has not ratified any of them.

  • Option A. Third-party expert validators — a credentialed external examiner performs the co-attestation in the senior's absence.
  • Option B. Recorded-skill demonstrations with frame-level scoring — candidate work scored against a rubric, with human review at a confidence threshold.
  • Option C. Deferred attestation pending peer maturity — the stone is provisionally placed and held until a qualified peer reaches level within a defined window.

Annika and Inez react. Vector takes notes. No approach is committed at the meeting. If Kernel is present, they speak only when Inez asks about eval methodology depth.

30–40
Pricing + design-partner terms · Henrik joins

Vector walks the hybrid model: $80K base + $14/EE (manufacturing-floor scope) + $35/stone. Design-partner discount: $0 activation tier for the first 100 attested stones; full $80K base begins year-2. If Annika decides to sign the MOU at the meeting rather than take it to her board: there is no formal board approval requirement under $200K/year per the customer note. Henrik should understand the cyclicality exposure: a 20% headcount reduction in the manufacturing-floor scope (~4,160 EE today) reduces the per-EE component by approximately $11.6K in a full year, not catastrophic against the $80K base. The per-stone component scales with activity, which also contracts in a downcycle.

Henrik leaves at :40. Do not let the pricing discussion drift into the attestation segment or vice versa.
40–45
Forward

Next steps. If MOU is signed: connector kickoff (Yield) within 7 days. First synthetic-data smoke test under Cordova's real graph within 14 days. If MOU is deferred: Vector sends clean terms by end of day 2026-05-26, with a five-business-day response window. Vector confirms the employee-data clearance for future demo use. Annika's attestation-approach preference documented and filed with Forge for architecture planning.

Section 5 · Talking points by attendee

Talking points by attendee.

These are not scripts. They are pressure maps. Know where each person's weight sits before the call starts.

Annika Whitfield
CHRO · Cordova Manufacturing

Annika is an industrial-engineer-turned-HR-leader. Fourteen years at GE Aviation. She respects specificity and methodology disclosure. She distrusts SaaS warmth. She came to Cordova to give her board data, not expressive pauses. Lead with the data. If the Querétaro demo shows the heatmap running in under 90 seconds on live data, that is the moment her posture opens. Do not explain what she can read.

Her attestation question is not academic. She has three senior welders on Stamping Line 3 inside two to three years of retirement with no qualified peer ready. She is asking whether Cairn can close that gap before the first retirement or whether she will be holding an empty attestation record. Be honest about where the architecture stands. She will respect the honesty more than a promise.

She will likely decide on the MOU in the room if the demo lands and the pricing math is straightforward. Do not oversell the MOU. Let her decide.

Inez Quintero
IT Director · Cordova Manufacturing · 15-year company veteran

Inez built the Oracle HCM integration with Cordova's plant systems herself in 2019. She has already reviewed the Stratum data-flow diagram (the one Forge drew with her at T+47d). She is not evaluating Stratum from scratch. She is checking whether Cairn adds a new attack surface or a new write path that was not in the original MSA.

Her questions will be security posture (SOC 2, data residency, AEDT compliance line), the consent footprint for the employee in the demo, and whether Cairn writes back to Oracle HCM Cloud. The answers: Stratum is SOC 2 Type II; data residency is configurable (US-West by default for Cordova); the AEDT disclosure is in the methodology block on every decision output; Cairn does not write to Oracle without explicit human approval and audit log. Reference the trust center if she wants the full sub-processor list.

Brief but substantive. She does not need a sales pitch. She needs a clean answer per question.

Henrik Adler
CFO · Cordova Manufacturing · 22-year company veteran

Henrik's framework: if he cannot hold the data flow from HRIS to output in ten minutes, the answer is no. He signed off on the original MSA because Forge drew that diagram with Inez on a whiteboard and did not use the word insights. Cairn's commercial model must be equally legible.

He will ask what happens to Cordova's Cairn spend if headcount contracts. The honest answer, in his terms: the per-EE component applies to the manufacturing-floor scope (~4,160 EE today at 80% of workforce). A 20% reduction in that scope reduces the annual per-EE charge by approximately $11.6K. The $80K platform base is fixed until year-3 renewal. The per-stone charge scales with attestation activity, which also contracts in a downcycle. Worst case for Cordova in a 20% headcount-cut year: Cairn spend drops by roughly $12–14K against a base of ~$138K. That is a 9–10% reduction, not a cliff.

Do not discount the $80K platform base. That is a hard floor per Compass's pricing discipline. If Henrik pushes: the discount is already in the structure (the first-100-stones activation tier at $0 is a $3,500 discount on the per-stone rate). Name it as such.

Section 6 · Refusal lines — what we will not say or do
  • No promise on unbuilt attestation methodology We will not commit to an attestation approach we have not built. The three options (A, B, C) are presented as open architecture questions. If Annika asks which one Stratum recommends, the honest answer is: we do not yet know; that is why we are asking her.
  • No feature outside the architecture document We will not commit to a feature, connector, or capability outside the current architecture document. If a request falls outside scope, Forge says: "That is worth exploring; I will bring it back with a sizing estimate within a week."
  • No other customer's data We will not show any other customer's data in this session. The demo runs on Cordova's own Querétaro graph. If Annika asks how Halcyon or any other customer handled a comparable problem, the answer is a verbal description only, with no data and with that customer's consent obtained in advance.
  • No discount below $80K platform base The $80K base is a hard floor per Compass's pricing discipline. We do not negotiate this number. If Henrik pushes for a lower base, the response is: the design-partner structure already reflects a discount in the activation tier, not in the base.
  • No delivery commitment before M1 mitigation is verified We will not commit to a delivery timeline for the attestation methodology before Kernel's M1 mitigation re-run is complete. If Annika asks for a timeline: "We will have a recommendation within two weeks of the re-run completing; I will send it in writing."
Section 7 · Risks
  • Forge gets a question he cannot answer. The right answer is: "I don't know; I'll get you an answer within 24 hours." That answer is better than a wrong answer. Annika has already seen Forge use it on the Oracle connector question at T+47d; she respects it.
  • Henrik raises a procurement concern requiring legal review. Defer to a second call within five business days. Do not improvise a legal position. If he raises a DPA concern beyond the three Cordova annotations already in the MSA, Inez should be invited into that follow-up alongside counsel.
  • The senior-retirement attestation question becomes a deal-block. If Annika says she cannot commit to the MOU until the attestation architecture is resolved, we defer signing rather than overcommit. The correct framing: "That is the right call. Let us resolve the architecture question in the next two weeks and reconvene. The design-partner terms are not time-limited."
