§ 01 · The premise Why this page exists, and why it doesn't read like a marketing page.

Most "Why Us vs Them" pages on the open internet are a marketing artifact — a feature checklist where the vendor marks every column green and the competitors get a tasteful red X. We won't insult you with one. This page is built on the actual product trials of all five subjects, eleven CHRO interviews conducted under personal NDA between February and April 2026, and four conversations with people who used to work for the incumbents we cover. It summarises and de-confidentialises material that lives in Stratum's internal Competitive Intelligence Dossier No. 001. Where it would help, we have linked to that dossier; the public version of this page leaves the names of the customers anonymous.

We will lose to a Workday, an Oracle, or a SAP if your need is system-of-record completeness, deep ERP integration, or seventeen years of audit-ready payroll history across forty jurisdictions. We are not the right answer there, and we will tell you so on the first call. We will win when your need is asking your people data a question and getting an answer you can put in front of your board on Tuesday afternoon. That is the boundary. Everything else on this page is detail.

Numbers and rankings on this page defer to the briefings and the intel dossier. If a number on this page disagrees with one in those documents, the briefings are right. Methodology in the footnote below the table.

§ 03 · Head to head

Four vendors, four honest reads.

For each of the primary competitors, three things in monospace: where they win, where we win, and the customer profile for which they are the right answer. We are happy to put any of this in writing for your procurement memo.

Head to head · 01

Stratum vs Workday

WDAY: $61B mkt cap Customers: 10,500+ Implementation: 14–22 months

The architectural difference matters more than any feature comparison. Workday is the system of record. It is the place where the employee object lives — hire date, comp band, manager, badge ID, leave balance — and the warehouse it was designed to be in 2008. Stratum is the system of truth. We sit on top of Workday (and ADP, and Greenhouse, and your CSV backups) and resolve those records into a graph that a CHRO can ask a question of in English.

Workday is genuinely excellent at what it was built for. The Illuminate Copilot is a credible orchestration layer, but it was welded onto an object model designed before "agent" meant anything technical. We are not trying to replace Workday; we are trying to be the surface a CHRO actually uses — and we read Workday's data on the way in.

"We use Workday like a filing cabinet. We'd like to use it like a brain." — CHRO, regional bank, 4,200 EE
Where Workday wins
17 years of audit-ready payroll history across 40+ jurisdictions. Deep ERP integration with Oracle Financials and SAP S/4HANA. Reference-customer breadth (10,500+, including most of the Fortune 500). Six-figure spend approves without a procurement fight. If you are buying a system of record, Workday is the safe answer.
Where we win
Speed to first answer: 8 weeks in our design partners vs 14–22 months for a typical Workday rollout. Per-decision pricing, not per-seat — your CHRO is not punished for hiring. AI-native architecture: a people graph and an agentic surface were the chassis, not a 2024 retrofit. NPS 47 (Mercator) vs Workday's median 18.
When to pick them
If you are a 5,000-EE company replacing a custom HRIS or an aging PeopleSoft install and you need a full system-of-record replacement with payroll across 12+ countries, buy Workday. Put Stratum on top of it in year two when you are ready to actually use the data you've paid to migrate.
Head to head · 02

Stratum vs SAP SuccessFactors

SAP: part of $250B parent HCM rev: ~$2.9B Joule for HR: beta · Apr '26

SuccessFactors is the slowest mover in the incumbent set and the one whose AI story is most clearly behind. Joule for HR went into beta in April 2026 and the public reception has been middling. Of the four CHRO interviews where SuccessFactors came up, three were candidly looking at alternatives — and the alternative they mentioned most often was Workday, not Stratum. We hear that and take the lesson: SAP's vulnerability is not automatic upside for a challenger.

That said: SAP wins decisively on global payroll, on talent suite breadth (LMS, comp, succession, recruiting), and on EU-government tenders where being a German vendor is worth points. Stratum wins on AI maturity, on velocity, and on the pricing model. The customer that should pick SAP is the one already deep on S/4HANA — the integration math wins.

Where SAP wins
Global payroll in 99 countries with country-team coverage. Talent suite breadth (LMS · comp · succession · recruiting in one suite). EU-government tender preference. Long ERP relationships through S/4HANA. Compliance posture is genuinely strong (BAFIN, BaFin-adjacent, ICO).
Where we win
AI maturity by a wide margin — Joule for HR is early; our agentic surface is in production with five design partners. Velocity: SAP ships on a 12–18 month cycle; we ship a release every six weeks. Pricing transparency: SuccessFactors quotes assume an ERP cross-sell. We don't.
When to pick them
If you are already running SAP S/4HANA and your CIO has a standing order to buy SAP for everything ERP-adjacent, the integration math favours SuccessFactors. Use Stratum on top for the CHRO surface.
Head to head · 03

Stratum vs Oracle HCM

ORCL: $384B mkt cap OCI HR Assistant: beta · 2026 Cloud HCM customers: ~6,000

Oracle HCM is the quiet incumbent. It does not market like Workday and does not have SAP's branding budget, but in healthcare, federal-adjacent government, and Oracle Cloud Financials shops, it is genuinely dominant. The OCI HR Assistant beta is a real product, not vapor — but, like SAP's Joule, it is early. And Oracle's installed base is most concentrated in industries (healthcare, public sector) where the buyer is legitimately not in a hurry to adopt AI.

We will not compete with Oracle in an Oracle Cloud ERP shop on Oracle Cloud Financials. The integration cost favours staying. We will compete — and win — when an Oracle Fusion customer is looking for a CHRO surface that can read out of Fusion without moving to it.

Where Oracle wins
Cloud ERP integration if you are on Oracle Cloud Financials. Long-tenure customer references in healthcare (Cleveland Clinic-class), public sector, and US federal-adjacent contractors. Database-grade audit trails. Compliance posture for FedRAMP-adjacent buyers.
Where we win
AI maturity (OCI HR Assistant is beta; we are GA). Pricing transparency — Oracle list prices are negotiated three times and the final number is opaque to your board. Time-to-value: our design partners hit first defensible answer at week 8 vs 12–18 months for Fusion HCM.
When to pick them
If you are an Oracle Fusion ERP shop, you intend to stay there, and your CFO already trusts Oracle's database posture, Oracle HCM is the right answer. Layer Stratum on top in year two if your CHRO wants a surface that her board will actually read.
Head to head · 04

Stratum vs Rippling

Rippling: $13.5B last round Customers: ~20,000 Sweet spot: 200–2,000 EE

Rippling is a different shape of competitor. It is the most-product-velocity company in this dossier and the one whose compression strategy on pricing has reset what an HR-buyer expects to pay. It is not, today, a CHRO platform — it is an HR + IT + Finance bundle for the mid-market. That distinction matters: a Rippling competitor at 600 EE is a different conversation than at 6,000 EE.

Where Rippling wins is the under-2,000-EE company that wants one bill for HR, IT provisioning, finance, and devices. Where we win is the 1,000–8,000 EE enterprise where the buyer is a CHRO, not a COO, and the decision surface matters more than the consolidated invoice. Different ideal customer; honest positioning.

Where Rippling wins
Mid-market HR + IT + Finance bundle (the only credible single-vendor play for sub-2,000-EE companies). Speed to deploy: 4–6 weeks for a 600-EE rollout. Pricing simplicity in that segment — a published per-employee number that does land at $8/head in some segments.
Where we win
AI maturity: Rippling's AI investment is real but newer than ours. Enterprise customer profile: we serve 1,000–8,000 EE credibly; Rippling's sweet spot is 200–2,000. Depth of decision-support for CHROs — Rippling has not built that surface yet.
When to pick them
If you are a 600-EE company consolidating HR + IT + Finance and you don't have a full-time CHRO yet, Rippling is the right answer. We will say so on the first call and pass you their AE. We expect to see you again at 1,500 EE.
§ 04 · Where we deliberately don't compete

Three categories we will not pretend to be.

A surprising number of HR vendors quietly try to be everything. We won't. Here are the three categories where the right answer is a specialist, not Stratum.

01 · Out of scope

A full payroll provider.

Payroll is a regulated, jurisdictional business that demands decades of investment in country-team coverage and tax compliance. We integrate to ADP, Workday Payroll, Gusto, Rippling Payroll, and twelve country-specific systems. We do not run payroll ourselves, and we do not intend to. If you are buying a payroll provider, the right answer is one of the above. Partners · ADP · Workday Payroll · Gusto · Rippling Payroll · Deel · Remote
02 · Out of scope

A benefits administration platform.

Benefits admin — enrollment, carrier connections, COBRA, ACA reporting — is a category in which Bswift, Mercer, and Aon have invested for two decades. We surface benefits data into the people graph so a CHRO can ask about take-rate and equity by cohort. We do not manage carriers and we do not run open enrollment. Partners · Bswift · Mercer Marsh · Aon · Sequoia Consulting
03 · Out of scope

A learning management system.

LMS — course hosting, SCORM, compliance training, learning paths, manager certifications — is a category served well by Cornerstone, Workday Learning, Docebo, and LinkedIn Learning. We surface learning data (completion, certifications, skill gaps) into the people graph. We do not host courses. Partners · Cornerstone · Workday Learning · Docebo · LinkedIn Learning
§ 05 · Pick by need

A decision matrix for the CHRO.

If you are reading this with one hour before a procurement meeting, here is the shortcut. The need on the left; the vendor on the right. We have put ourselves in the table twice. The other answers are honest.

"I need to replace my whole HRIS, including payroll and benefits across multiple countries." Workday, Oracle, or SAP — depending on your existing ERP.
"I need to ask my people data a question I can defend to my board this quarter." Stratum. This is the question we exist to answer.
"I'm a 600-EE company wanting one bill for HR, IT, and finance." Rippling — and we will pass you the AE.
"I want best-in-class performance management as a standalone." Lattice — performance is genuinely their craft.
"I want continuous engagement signals across the org." Culture Amp or Lattice — both are credible.
"I want my CHRO to have leverage on a complex multi-system environment — Workday, ADP, Greenhouse, Bswift." Stratum. Sit on top, don't replace.
"I want a low-risk, board-friendly incumbent and I am not in a hurry." Workday — there is no procurement memo it cannot survive.
"I'm an Oracle Fusion ERP shop, intending to stay there." Oracle HCM — the integration math is decisive.
"I'm running SAP S/4HANA and my CIO has a standing SAP-first directive." SAP SuccessFactors — same logic.
"I want a Tuesday-afternoon answer, not a Q4-2027 roadmap commitment." Stratum or Rippling — in that order, by enterprise fit.
