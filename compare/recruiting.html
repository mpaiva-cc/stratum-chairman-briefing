§ 01 · The premise A different competitive shape than HCM.

Most ATS comparison pages on the open internet are about feature parity — do you support video screening, structured interviews, scorecards, scheduling links, offer letters, candidate portals, requisition approvals, EEO disclosures. That is the wrong frame for this page. Greenhouse and Lever do these things well; we don't need to compete on that and we don't want to. Ashby is, in many ways, the most modern entry in the category and built a real analytics layer in-product. Workday Recruiting is the enterprise default. We are happy to recommend all of them depending on the question.

The competitive shape: Stratum is an intelligence layer that sits above your ATS. The question is not "should we replace Greenhouse with Stratum?" but "should we add Stratum to whatever ATS we already run?" — and for whom is that worth the dollars. Our HCM comparison page argues that Stratum sits on top of Workday. This page argues the same thing about Greenhouse and Lever and Ashby and Workday Recruiting. The pattern is consistent. We are an intelligence surface; we are not a system of work.

If you do not have an ATS today, you need one before you need us. We are happy to recommend Greenhouse, Lever, or Ashby depending on your size — see the pick-by-need framework at the bottom of this page. We will not sell you Stratum first.

§ 03 · Head to head

Four ATSes, four honest reads.

For each of the primary ATSes, three things in monospace: where they win, where we win, and how it works together. We expect most readers will end up running both their ATS and Stratum. The vast majority of our installed base does.

Head to head · 01

Stratum + Greenhouse

Greenhouse: series F Default for: series B–D tech Connector: GA · hourly read

Greenhouse is the de facto standard for series B–D tech companies in the United States. It has the strongest scorecard culture in the category, the cleanest API surface of any ATS in this dossier, and the most mature interview-kit ecosystem. We integrate deeply with it: full connector, scorecard ingestion, offer write-back, source attribution. Greenhouse + Stratum is, by a comfortable margin, our most common combination. We are not competing with Greenhouse; we are extending it.

The pattern is straightforward. Recruiters and hiring managers continue to live in Greenhouse for the daily work — pipelines, scorecards, scheduling, candidate communication. Stratum reads from Greenhouse hourly and answers the questions Greenhouse's reporting layer cannot answer cleanly: which sources are worth the dollars, where is this req actually stuck, can we defend the agency invoice. Write-back is to the offers module only, and only when explicitly approved. We do not move candidates between stages.

"Greenhouse runs the funnel. Stratum tells me whether the funnel is working." — Director of Recruiting Operations, 1,400-EE marketplace
Where Greenhouse wins
ATS workflow itself. Interview kit templates and structured interview discipline. Scorecard standardization. Candidate experience tools (portals, status comms, automated nudges). Marketplace of third-party integrations (Gem, Modern Hire, HireRight, etc.). If you are buying your first ATS at 200–1,500 EE, Greenhouse is the safe answer.
Where we win
Cross-source attribution: Greenhouse can tell you a hire came from LinkedIn; we can tell you what that LinkedIn hire cost in dollars after blending agency fees, recruiter time, and tooling. Pipeline intelligence: "why is this req stuck" in English. EEO and AEDT compliance reporting. Cost-per-hire by source with agency math included.
How it works together
Stratum reads Greenhouse hourly via the Harvest API; entity resolution into the people graph happens nightly. Write-back is to the offers module only and only when approved by a recruiter. Customers continue to use Greenhouse for the daily work — we are the intelligence surface for the recruiting leader, not the system of work for the coordinator.
Head to head · 02

Stratum + Lever

Lever: Employ Inc. portfolio Sweet spot: 100–1,000 EE Connector: GA · hourly read

Same shape, different segment. Lever is strong in startups in the 100–1,000 EE band; the candidate-relationship surface is more modern than Greenhouse's, and the hiring manager UX is genuinely nice to look at. We integrate via Lever's public API on a hourly cadence and the connector has been GA since late 2025.

The interesting wrinkle: many of our customers run Lever in one office and Greenhouse in another. Northwind Studios — the design studio that did most of the road-testing for our recruiting analytics in late 2025 — runs Lever for the LA satellite and Greenhouse for the NYC headquarters. The recruiting leader could not get a unified view across them. We resolve both into a single graph, which is what made the $4.2M agency-spend question answerable at all (more on that below).

Where Lever wins
Candidate database design. Hiring manager UX. Native CRM features for nurture and re-engagement. Lever is the most design-led of the legacy ATSes and recruiters who have used both tend to prefer Lever's pipeline view.
Where we win
Source attribution: Lever's analytics layer is the weakest of the four primary ATSes in this dossier — the team has admitted as much on customer advisory boards. Pipeline intelligence. NYC AEDT and EU pay-transparency compliance posture. If you run Lever in one place and Greenhouse in another, we unify them in the graph.
How it works together
Stratum reads from Lever hourly via the public Lever API. Both single-ATS Lever customers and multi-ATS (Lever + Greenhouse) customers are served. The graph reconciles candidates across systems by deterministic match on email plus probabilistic match on name + employer history.
Head to head · 03

Stratum + Ashby

Ashby: series C Positioning: the modern ATS Connector: beta · Aug '26

Ashby is the most modern entry in the category. It was built with analytics as a first-class surface, not an afterthought, and its AI-assisted sourcing tools are genuinely competitive with Gem and Eightfold. The candidate experience is well-designed and the product velocity is the highest of any incumbent on this page. Ashby is the ATS we recommend most often to engineering-led series B startups that have not yet bought one.

The honest version: if you have ONLY Ashby and no other recruiting tools and you are under 1,000 EE, you may not need Stratum's recruiting features yet. Ashby's in-product analytics will cover the questions you can ask of a single system. Our value compounds when there are multiple systems to unify (Ashby + LinkedIn Recruiter, Ashby + a legacy CRM, Ashby + ten years of historical hire data from a previous ATS) and when the question crosses into HCM territory (correlation with performance ratings, retention by source, manager effectiveness).

Where Ashby wins
In-product analytics — Ashby is the only ATS in this dossier whose reporting layer we cannot fairly call weak. Candidate experience surface. AI-assisted sourcing tools. Product velocity (six-week release cadence). If you are buying your first ATS today at 300 EE and you care about analytics, buy Ashby.
Where we win
Cross-system intelligence: Ashby is excellent inside Ashby; the moment your data lives in two systems, you need something above. Correlation with performance data post-hire (the source-to-rating link that only HCM context can answer). NYC AEDT audit posture with explicit lineage. Pricing transparency on procurement-side renewals.
How it works together
Connector is in beta with three design partners; targeted GA is August 2026. Read-side covers all major Ashby objects; offer-write-back is planned for late Q3. Until then, Ashby customers run Stratum in read-only mode and use Ashby itself for the daily recruiting work — same pattern as Greenhouse.
Head to head · 04

Stratum + Workday Recruiting

WDAY: $61B mkt cap Recruiting users: ~4,000 customers Connector: GA · hourly read

The enterprise default. Workday Recruiting is rarely chosen on its own merits; it is the recruiting module of the HCM system the company already bought. Once installed, it is hard to displace because the integration with Workday HRIS, Workday Payroll, and Workday Financials creates an audit-and-procurement gravity well no challenger has been able to credibly attack on its own. We are not trying to displace Workday Recruiting and we will tell you so on the first call.

We are, however, the most common displacement-adjacent conversation in this dossier. The shape is consistent: a CHRO or VP of Talent has been frustrated by Workday Recruiting's analytics for two to four years; the BI team has built a Tableau-on-Workday workaround that nobody trusts; the CFO keeps asking questions the workaround cannot defend. Stratum reads Workday Recruiting hourly, makes the data answerable in English, and the CHRO suddenly has a surface a board will actually look at. We do not replace Workday Recruiting. We make it useful to a CHRO who has been frustrated by it for years.

"We pay Workday a seven-figure number for HCM. The least it can do is answer a question. It can't. That's why we bought you." — CHRO, financial services, 8,200 EE
Where Workday wins
Integration with Workday HRIS and Payroll — the candidate-to-employee transition is genuinely seamless and the audit trail is tight. Large-enterprise procurement (no six-figure spend approval conversation). A decade of payroll-side audit data that no recruiting-first ATS can match. SOC and compliance posture is the safe answer for federal-adjacent buyers.
Where we win
AI-native intelligence (Workday Recruiting analytics is buried behind twelve clicks; Stratum is one question). Time-to-defensible-answer on cost-per-hire across non-Workday sources — LinkedIn Recruiter, Gem, Eightfold, agencies, referrals. Pricing transparency in renewal cycles. This is our most common conversation in the enterprise.
How it works together
Stratum reads Workday Recruiting hourly via the Workday REST API; offer write-back to Workday is GA. Customers continue to run requisitions, scorecards, and approvals in Workday. The CHRO surface, the cost-per-hire roll-up, and the board-grade answers come from Stratum. Compliance reports for NYC AEDT and EU pay-transparency are generated from the unified graph.

§ 05 · The CFO question "Are we getting value from the four-point-two million?"

The CFO at Northwind Studios — one of our recruiting-side design partners since November 2025 — had a specific question for the Head of Talent during the FY26 planning cycle. The question was unusually direct, even by CFO standards. It was about agency spend, and it was a number large enough that it had survived two rounds of cost-cutting.

Are we getting value from the $4.2M we spend on recruiting agencies each year? Show me the number, and show me how you got to it. — CFO, Northwind Studios · January 2026

The Head of Talent — Lila Park, who has since become one of our most pointed product advisors — could not answer it. Northwind's ATS at the time (a Lever-Greenhouse split across two offices) could tell her how many hires came from each agency. It could not tell her cost-per-hire. It could not blend in recruiter time. It could not isolate the agency hires that turned over inside year one. It could not produce a number she could defend in front of the board.

Stratum could. The first defensible answer came back in 47 seconds after the connector finished backfilling: $4.2M of agency spend had produced $2.1M of retained-after-twelve-months value. The CFO did not get the answer he wanted. He got an answer he could defend. That is the whole product.

If your CFO has ever asked you this question and you could not defend the answer, the comparison is not Stratum vs Greenhouse. It is Stratum vs the spreadsheet you produced under pressure that you could not defend in front of the board. Greenhouse is a system of work. We are a system of evidence.

§ 06 · Pick by need

A decision matrix for recruiting leaders.

If you are reading this with one hour before a tooling-review meeting, here is the shortcut. The situation on the left; the recommendation on the right. We have put ourselves in the table twice. The other answers are honest.

"I'm a 200-EE company looking for my first ATS." Greenhouse or Lever — pick on UX preference. Come back to Stratum at 800 EE.
"I'm a 300-EE startup buying my first ATS and I care about analytics." Ashby — their in-product analytics will get you a long way alone.
"I'm a 600-EE company with strong recruiting and weak analytics." Greenhouse + Stratum — our most common combination.
"I'm a 1,500-EE company using two ATSes and can't get a unified view." Stratum. This is what the graph is for.
"I'm an enterprise on Workday Recruiting and frustrated with its analytics." Keep Workday, add Stratum — our most common displacement-adjacent conversation.
"I'm a Series A company with 50 employees and I need to start tracking candidates." Greenhouse, Lever, or Ashby — come back to Stratum when you're at 800 EE.
"I want the best sourcing/CRM layer on top of my existing ATS." Gem or Eightfold — different category than us. Both are credible.
"My CFO asked whether we're getting value from agency spend and I cannot defend the answer." Stratum. This is the literal question we exist for.
"I'm a federal-adjacent contractor with strict audit requirements." iCIMS or Workday Recruiting — buy compliance first, then add us for the surface.
"I have NYC roles and need AEDT audit posture by Q3." Stratum on top of your ATS — the audit lineage is built into the graph.
