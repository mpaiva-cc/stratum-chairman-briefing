§ 01 · The thesis

Why work here, in three arguments.

The short version of why an early career at Stratum is, we believe, an asymmetric bet on a category that has not had a serious challenger in fifteen years.

i. The category

Most enterprise software companies are building for the incumbent's playbook. Stratum is built for the next ten years of work.

Workday and SAP solved the system-of-record problem in 2012. The question that matters now — how do you make fewer, faster, more accountable people decisions with the data you already have — is not a feature on their roadmap. It is a new architecture. We get to draw it.

We sell to companies between 1,000 and 8,000 employees who have outgrown Rippling and find Workday a prison. There are 14,000 of them. None of the four incumbents will, structurally, be able to follow us.

ii. The seat

The early team will define the architecture, the eval set, and the brand. There are about thirty seats where what you do becomes how the company thinks.

We are at seat 6. By Briefing No. 13 we will be at twenty-two. By next May, forty. The connector you ship in October will still be there in 2034. The naming convention you set on the people graph will outlast the founders' opinions about it. This is not the same as a Series B company telling you the same story.

We hire for taste, range, and depth — usually two of the three. The first thirty hires are expected to have all three on their best day.

iii. The pace

We are deliberately small for as long as we can be. We would rather hire one outlier in November than four competent people in August.

Our hiring plan is paced to the product. We grow when a missing person is the binding constraint on a specific bet — not when the headcount slide demands it. Pillar (Data Infrastructure) accepted last Tuesday. Tessera (Founding Designer) accepted on Friday. Both are people we waited months for.

This pace will frustrate you if you join expecting a fully staffed team on day one. It will reward you if you have been on the other side of the wrong hire.

We are looking for taste, range, and depth — usually two of the three.
§ 02 · The operating model

Eight principles we will, on the record, defend.

Read these in good faith. They are not slogans; they are how we will resolve the next thousand small disagreements between now and the Series B.

Specific over impressive.

A connector that ships beats a connector we explained well. We are suspicious of decks. We are even more suspicious of decks that rhyme.

The eval set is the product.

If a question is not in our eval set, we have not shipped the answer. Every quarter we add to it, never remove from it, and run the whole thing in CI.

Tuesday afternoons.

Every roadmap conversation returns to: does this give a CHRO back her Tuesday afternoon? If the answer is no, the work is not on the roadmap.

Three is the right number.

Founders. Engineering pillars. Quarterly themes. Trios, not committees. We have already turned down two strong fourth co-founders. We will turn down a third.

Born compliant.

Compliance is a feature, not an afterthought. Engineering owns SOC 2, not Legal. The EU AI Act stance is in the README, not the wiki.

Write things down.

The decisions ledger is a load-bearing wall. Every reversible call is a one-pager; every irreversible call is a two-pager. We do not relitigate; we link.

Composition over consensus.

We compose conflicting opinions; we do not dilute them. The strongest version of each view wins on its terms — not by averaging away the sharp edges.

Slow hire. Faster fire than is comfortable.

The cost of the wrong tenth hire is six months of slow drift. We will feel the discomfort of acting on it in week eight, not month eight. We owe that to the people who stay.

§ 04 · How we hire

Four stages. Nine days. Real work.

We owe you a fast, honest process. No takeaway tests we will not read; no panels of seven people you will not meet again. Time-to-offer target, measured from intro call to written offer, is nine business days.

1
Day 1 · 30 minutes

Intro

Thirty minutes with one of the three founders. Mutual fit, your recent work, why Stratum specifically. We will tell you what we are unsure of about the seat — we expect you to do the same about us.

2
Day 3 · 90 minutes

Craft

Ninety-minute work session on a real problem from our current roadmap. Paid. No trick questions, no algorithm trivia. You leave with the work; we leave with a clear read on how you think.

3
Day 6 · 3 hours

Architecture, or arc

Three-hour panel deep-dive. For engineers, an architecture conversation. For everyone else, the arc of a project you ran end to end. Two of us, one peer, one external advisor for half.

4
Day 9 · 60 minutes

Compact

Sixty minutes with all three founders, Chairman occasionally. Comp, equity, expectations, your first ninety days, written down. Offer in writing at the end of the same day if both sides nod.

Time-to-offer target · 9 business days from intro to written offer. References are checked in parallel, not after. We tell you who we are calling.

§ 06 · A note on what we are not

Three things we deliberately do not optimize for.

These will, in different years, frustrate different members of the team. We are telling you up front because we would rather you self-select than discover the gap in your second year.

i. Speed-to-IPO.

We will optimize for the right shape of company, not the fastest path to a liquidity event. If your investment thesis on joining is a near-term exit, that is reasonable, and this is the wrong place for it. The Chairman has been candid: we are building something that we expect to take ten years to look obvious.

ii. Headcount as a metric.

We will report headcount because boards ask. It is not a goal. We will resist the temptation to hire because we have the cash; we will resist the temptation to fire because growth slowed. If you have run a team that was deliberately under-staffed for a year and was the better for it, you will recognize this.

iii. A perfectly happy week one.

Your first week will involve friction. Onboarding is improving but will never be polished. You will have to ask twice. You will be invited to disagree on day three. We believe friction is part of joining a serious project — we do not believe in dressing it up.
