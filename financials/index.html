§ 08 — Notes & methodology

Assumptions, on the record.

A short list of the choices baked into the numbers above. Each is a decision the three of us have signed off on; each is one we would cheerfully revisit if the data demands it. The live model holds the workings; the figures above round to it.

Provenance
All figures pre-product; bottoms-up plan signed off by all three founders on T+16d, reviewed by Sarah Cannon (Index) on T+17d. The live Google Sheets model is the canonical source; figures here round to it within ±1%.
Inference cost
Modelled at $0.07 per decision-supported — the unit our pricing tier is denominated in. Sensitive to foundation-model pricing. A 25% increase in model unit cost compresses gross margin by ~3 points at steady state; the live model has a sensitivity tab.
Compensation
Salary and equity bands pulled from 41 closed offers in H1 2026 at mid-tier enterprise SaaS companies (Series A–C, US-headquartered, Bay Area + NYC weighted). Refreshed quarterly through Pave + Carta. Founders are at 60% of band cash; pool is at 100%.
Conversion
Design partner → paid pilot conversion assumed 60% (3 of 5). Paid pilot → commercial conversion assumed 67% in Y1, 80% in Y2 as eval benchmarks mature. Win rates against incumbent are not modelled — we assume we are not in deals where Workday is incumbent.
Discount rate · NPV
12% for any NPV-style framing — consistent with Index's internal hurdle for seed-stage software portfolio companies. We do not use NPV operationally; we use it only to test asymmetric upside in the scenario grid (§ 07).
Cash discipline
Burn is measured monthly against a forward-twelve forecast. Any single month above 110% of forecast triggers a founder review; anything above 125% triggers a hire freeze in the affected function until the next monthly close. SOC2 / ISO / EU AI Act spend is ring-fenced.
Currency & tax
All figures USD. Federal & CA state income tax modelled at the effective C-corp rates for a loss-making company (i.e., NOLs accumulate, no current tax). Sales tax is collected and remitted on US ARR from Q2'27 onward via Anrok.
Reporting cadence
This document is revised on the 4th Sunday of each month. A live monthly close is delivered to the Board within ten business days of month-end. The Chairman receives a one-page delta against this v1 model on the same Sunday as the briefing.
Rounding
Numbers above are rounded for legibility: ARR in $K, cash in $M to two decimals, percentages to a single decimal. The live model is the canonical source and will, on close inspection, reveal cents.
Cross-reference
No number in this artifact contradicts the Chairman's Briefing No. 001 (founding) or No. 002 (T+18d). Where the briefings round ($8.0M seed, 26-month runway at founding), this model presents the operating refinement ($7.82M post-fees, 24-month runway at current planned ramp).
