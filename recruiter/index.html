§ 01 · Why we built this

Three reasons we built an ATS we never planned to build.

Stratum's original positioning was: we sit above your ATS. We still do. But three of our commercial customers told us — in writing, in pilots, on calls — that they wanted us to replace their ATS too. Here is why we said yes.

§ Reason 01 · customer demand

Three commercial customers asked us to build it.

Cordova, Atlas, and Vanta — all current Stratum Console customers — explicitly asked us to build a native ATS. Their existing systems (Workday Recruiting, a bespoke in-house tool, and Greenhouse, respectively) felt like deadweight next to the matching engine they were already paying us for.

"Your matching engine is better than our ATS's. Can you just be our ATS?"
— Annika Whitfield, CHRO · Cordova Manufacturing
§ Reason 02 · architectural fit

Candidates and employees are one data fabric.

Other ATSes treat candidates as a separate database, then export to HRIS as a one-way file on hire day. Our people graph already holds the employees. Adding the candidates to the same graph is not a new system — it is a node type. The data fabric runs continuously from first source contact through year-three performance review.

"We already had the harder half of the problem solved. Building the ATS on top was the smaller engineering bet."
— Stratum engineering · Q1 2026 architecture review
§ Reason 03 · the category is ready

The incumbents have left a door open.

Workday Recruiting is bloated. Greenhouse is good but its AI is bolted on after the fact. Lever is consolidating. Ashby is best-in-class for sourcing but premium-priced and narrowly scoped. There is genuine room for an AI-native challenger that starts from the graph and works outward, not from a 2014 form-based ATS that bolts AI on at the edges.

"The category is ten years overdue for a rewrite. We are not pretending otherwise."
— Stratum product memo · Recruiter v0 · Feb 2026
§ 02 · The product, at a glance

Ten things Stratum Recruiter does on day one.

Every feature below is shipping in the private beta. None of them is on a roadmap behind a paywall. We will add more — but this is the floor, not the ceiling.

AI-drafted JDs

Type a sentence; get a job description draft tuned to your company's voice and comp band. Reviewable, editable, never sent without a human.

AI candidate matching

Every applicant scored against the JD with the reasoning visible. The system refuses to auto-reject; surfacing, ranking, and the final call stays with the recruiter.

Pipeline kanban

Drag candidates between stages. Bulk operations supported. Keyboard-first for recruiters who live in the tool. Stage definitions customizable per req.

Structured scorecards

Define rubrics per role. Surface calibration drift across interviewers and across panels. Bias signals flagged for the recruiter, not hidden in a dashboard.

AI panel suggestions

Given a candidate and a req, propose the optimal 4-person interviewer panel — balanced for calibration history, availability, and diversity of perspective. Recruiter approves before invites send.

AI offer drafting

Aware of your comp bands and pay equity guardrails. Templated by level and function. One-click revision; finance and CHRO approval workflows built in.

AI acceptance predictor

Predict offer acceptance probability with the reasoning surfaced — comp gap to market, candidate's flight-risk-at-current, recency of competing offers, stage of conversation.

Source analytics

Cost-per-hire by source. First-year retention by source. ROI on agency spend, broken out by agency. The numbers other ATSes cannot calculate because they do not have the HRIS data.

NYC AEDT compliance

Annual bias audit ready out of the box. Output metrics surfaced per requisition. Bias-audit report packs auto-generated for your employment counsel — no quarterly fire drill.

18 HRIS integrations

Native write-back to Workday, BambooHR, ADP, Rippling, Personio, UKG, Paylocity, Paycom, Namely, HiBob, Justworks, Gusto, SAP SuccessFactors, Oracle HCM, Ceridian Dayforce, Sage People, Zenefits, and IRIS Cascade.

§ 03 · The architectural advantage

Built on the people graph.

Other ATSes silo candidate data and export to HRIS on hire day. Stratum Recruiter lives inside the same graph that already holds your employees — so the hand-off is a node-type change, not a file transfer.

Other ATSes are siloed.

Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby, Workday Recruiting — they all store candidate data in their own database. When a candidate becomes an employee, the data is exported (often as a one-way file) to your HRIS. Context is lost in transit. Cost-per-hire by source becomes a spreadsheet exercise; first-year retention by source becomes a quarterly project.

Stratum Recruiter is unified.

Every candidate is a node in the same people graph that holds your employees. When the candidate becomes a hire, the node converts — no export, no data loss, no system seam. Source, screen, interview, scorecard, offer, accept, hire, perform: one continuous history per person.

What this enables.

Real cost-per-hire that accounts for first-year retention. Real conversion rates that incorporate performance ratings six months in. Real source-quality analysis where the agency that delivers fast but high-attrition hires is correctly demoted. Things other ATSes cannot do, because their data is siloed.

§ 04 · Sample workflow · the requisition lifecycle

One requisition. Nine steps. Stratum at each one.

From "we need to backfill this senior engineer" to "she signed on Monday and starts a week from Wednesday." Each step shows what the recruiter does and what Stratum does for them.

Step 01 · draft

Draft the JD

Stratum drafts the JD in your company's voice from one sentence of intent; the recruiter edits before posting.

Step 02 · approve

Approve internally

Stratum routes the approval to the hiring manager and finance with comp-band context and headcount-plan reconciliation attached.

Step 03 · post

Post to job boards

Stratum syndicates to LinkedIn, Indeed, your careers site, and 14 niche boards — with per-board tracking pre-tagged.

Step 04 · source

Source actively

Stratum surfaces candidates from your existing silver-medalist pool and outbound sources, with a per-candidate match explanation.

Step 05 · screen

Screen applicants

Stratum scores every applicant against the JD with reasoning. The system refuses to auto-reject; recruiter reviews the ranked list.

Step 06 · interview

Schedule and run interviews

Stratum proposes the panel, books the calendar, sends the prep, and collects structured scorecards. Calibration drift is surfaced after the debrief.

Step 07 · offer

Draft the offer

Stratum drafts the offer letter in your template, checks pay-equity guardrails, predicts acceptance probability, and routes for e-sign.

Step 08 · accept

Candidate accepts

Stratum closes the loop on the offer, pushes signed documents to e-records, and pre-stages the HRIS write-back.

Step 09 · hire

Hire and onboard

Stratum writes to your HRIS (one of 18 supported), converts the candidate node to an employee node in the people graph, and starts the first-year retention clock.
