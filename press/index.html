§ 04 The narrative arc

A three-beat story you can crib from.

01 The architectural insight

The org is a graph, not a tree.

The four major human capital management platforms — Workday, Oracle HCM, SAP SuccessFactors, ADP — were architected between 2005 and 2012, before modern data infrastructure and well before modern language models. They treat the company as a tidy hierarchical record: one employee, one manager, one job title, one department. Real organizations stopped looking like that ten years ago. Contractors, dotted lines, project teams, cross-functional pods, and increasingly software agents make a graph, not a tree. Stratum's wager is that the next decade of work belongs to whoever builds the system of truth — a unified people graph that sits above the systems of record an enterprise already runs — rather than another system of record.

02 The buyer insight

CHROs spend Tuesday afternoons reconciling reports.

In a Stratum survey of 147 Chief People Officers at companies between 1,000 and 10,000 employees, the median respondent reported spending seventy percent of her week reconciling reports she did not ask for: pulling data from five or six systems, exporting to Excel, pivot-tabling cohorts, and answering board questions that should have been answerable in thirty seconds. The buyer's pain is not configuration. The buyer's pain is time. Stratum's product, at its core, is a copilot that answers any question of the people data with the chart, the cohort, and the regulation already cited — turning a CHRO into, in Compass's phrase, "a force of one thousand." We do not promise to make the CHRO's job easier in the abstract. We promise to sell her Tuesday afternoons back.

03 The moment

Three forces converging in one quarter.

Three forces are converging in 2026, and they will not converge again. First, language models have crossed the threshold at which a defensible, cited answer to a CHRO board question — pay equity, regretted attrition, cohort risk — is reliably producible in seconds, not days. Second, the regulatory shock of the EU AI Act, US state pay-transparency laws, and SEC human-capital disclosure rules has put a board-level reporting burden on companies whose existing HCM stack was not built to answer the questions. Third, the typical enterprise HCM contract runs four years, and a substantial cohort of 2022-vintage Workday and SuccessFactors deals comes due between Q4 2026 and Q4 2027. The buyer is asking new questions, the technology can now answer them, and the contract is up for review. We believe this is the company-creation window for HCM, and it closes in eighteen months.
