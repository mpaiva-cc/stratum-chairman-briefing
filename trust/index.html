Where we are · the four-sentence version

Stratum is six weeks old and capitalized with $8M of seed funding. We have no production customers and no production customer data; our three design partners run pre-product against synthetic and anonymized sample data, behind their own firewalls.

Our SOC 2 Type II audit is underway with Vanta as the GRC platform and a Big-Four affiliate as the external auditor; we are 41 controls of 87 implemented and expect the Type II report to issue in February 2027.

Our governing principle is born compliant: the controls listed below were designed into the system before the first line of production code was written, not bolted on after a customer asked.

Everything in this document is, or will be, backed by a control reference, an evidence artefact, and an owner. We will tell you what is done, what is in progress, and what is on the roadmap — and we will not call something "compliant" that isn't.

§ 02 · Security architecture

Security architecture

Six control families covering identity, encryption, isolation, network, audit, and vulnerability. Each control has an owner, an evidence artefact, and a quarterly review date. Control IDs map 1:1 to our internal GRC registry.

SEC-1.0 Identity & access Implemented

Single identity, hardware-bound, least-privileged.

Every human and every service principal at Stratum is rooted in a single identity provider. There are no local accounts on production systems. There are no shared credentials.

Okta is the canonical IDP for all employees, contractors, and service accounts. Multi-factor authentication is enforced universally; for engineering, administrative, and on-call roles we require a FIDO2 hardware key (YubiKey 5C NFC, issued on day one). SMS and TOTP are blocked for these roles by Okta policy.

Authorization is role-based at the API surface and attribute-based on the people graph itself. Row- and column-level filters are enforced at the storage layer, not in the application — meaning a privilege-escalation in the app cannot return rows the caller's attributes do not permit. Privileged access (production console, audit log export, KMS custodian) is reviewed quarterly by the CTO and recorded in the access registry.

SEC-2.0 Encryption Implemented

Customer data is encrypted at every hop, with rotatable keys.

All data is encrypted in transit and at rest. Encryption is not a single layer; it is layered through the request path, the storage layer, the backup tier, and the inference pipeline.

In transit we require TLS 1.3 on all public and internal endpoints; TLS 1.2 is explicitly disabled. Internal service-to- service traffic is wrapped in mTLS via Linkerd. At rest, all customer data is encrypted with AES-256-GCM, with keys held in AWS KMS and managed under our key rotation policy (90-day rotation, automated, with prior-key retention for replay).

Customers with their own KMS may bring their own key. BYOK is available for design partners and enterprise customers; in BYOK mode, no Stratum operator can decrypt customer data without the customer's cooperation. Customer revocation of the key results in an unrecoverable lockout, by design.

Implementation crypto: tls: min_version: 1.3 cipher_suites: - TLS_AES_256_GCM_SHA384 - TLS_CHACHA20_POLY1305_SHA256 at_rest: algorithm: AES-256-GCM kms_provider: aws-kms (us-east-1, us-west-2) rotation_days: 90 byok: optional (per-tenant) in_memory: inference_key: per-tenant, 32-byte, rotated weekly

SEC-3.0 Data isolation Implemented

One customer cannot, by construction, read another customer's data.

Multi-tenancy is implemented as logical tenancy with cryptographic separation. A bug in our application code cannot result in a cross-tenant read; the storage layer rejects it.

Every customer is provisioned a tenant ID at onboarding. Every row in every people-graph table carries that tenant ID as part of its primary key, and Postgres row-level security policies force the tenant ID into every query at the database session level. The application code does not need to remember to filter — it cannot do otherwise.

For LLM inference, each tenant has its own inference key and a dedicated context scope. Prompts and retrieved context are tagged at the orchestration layer; the inference proxy refuses to issue a call to the model provider if the prompt's tenant tag does not match the caller's claims.

Implementation -- Postgres RLS, enforced at the role level CREATE POLICY tenant_isolation ON people_graph USING (tenant_id = current_setting('app.tenant_id')::uuid); ALTER TABLE people_graph FORCE ROW LEVEL SECURITY; -- Inference proxy assertion (excerpt) assert prompt.tenant_id == auth.tenant_id, "TENANT_MISMATCH"

SEC-4.0 Network Implemented

The data plane is not on the internet.

Our network is partitioned. The control plane (API surface, public web) accepts inbound traffic; the data plane (graph stores, vector index, inference queue) does not.

The production VPC is partitioned into three subnets with no inbound public ingress to the data plane. The data plane is reachable only from the control plane via authenticated, mutually-authenticated internal services. Egress from the data plane is allow-listed at the VPC level to a small set of named destinations (KMS, S3 in-region, Datadog, Anthropic api.anthropic.com); arbitrary outbound HTTP is dropped.

Bastion access for engineering use is via a SSO-mediated short-lived session through AWS SSM, with no persistent SSH keys and no long-lived bastion hosts. Every session is logged with full command history; sessions over 30 minutes require a second approver.

SEC-5.0 Audit & logging Implemented

Every read of the people graph and every agent decision is replayable.

The audit log is not a feature. It is the substrate. If we cannot tell you who saw what, when, and on whose instruction, the system did not work.

Every read of the people graph emits an immutable audit event: the caller, the tenant, the query, the rows returned (by ID), and the upstream cause (a user click, a scheduled job, an agent decision). Every agent decision additionally carries a provenance record — the rules invoked, the rows consulted, the model version, and the human approval ID where applicable.

Logs are written to an append-only store with cryptographic hashing of each prior event (a hash-linked chain), retained for 18 months hot and 7 years cold. Customer admins can stream their own tenant's audit events to their SIEM via webhook or S3 push; raw access to the audit store is restricted to the tenant and to Stratum's security team under documented break- glass procedure.

SEC-6.0 Vulnerability management In progress

We look for vulnerabilities continuously, and we patch on a clock.

A monoculture of "we patch when we hear about it" is unacceptable at any scale. Stratum's vulnerability program is calendrical and measurable.

Dependency scans run on every pull request and weekly across the full inventory; findings are filed automatically as Linear tickets with severity, owner, and SLA clock. Container images are scanned at build and again at runtime; drift between built and running images is alarmed.

We run internal pen-tests monthly (rotating focus: API, authentication, the inference proxy, the audit chain) and a third-party pen-test quarterly. The first third-party engagement was completed on T+-21d by Rhino Security Labs against the API and graph layer; the report and our remediation log are available under NDA.

Our SLA on remediation is fixed and tracked: Critical within 24 hours, High within 72 hours, Medium within 14 days, Low within 60 days. Open findings, time-to-remediate, and SLA breach counts are reported to the founder team weekly.

§ 03 · Privacy & data handling

Privacy & data handling

Five commitments about whose data this is, what we do with it, and how we let you take it back. These are contractual, not aspirational.

PRI-1.0 Customer data ownership Implemented

You own your data. We are the processor.

Under our standard Master Services Agreement and Data Processing Addendum, the customer is the data controller and Stratum is the data processor. We do not assert any independent right to customer data.

A customer may request a full export of its tenant data at any time and in any of the following formats: structured JSON (one file per graph entity), Parquet (one file per table), or CSV. The export includes the audit log and the provenance records of every agent decision in scope. Our SLA on export and on deletion request is seven calendar days.

Deletion is hard-deletion at the storage layer plus cryptographic erasure of derived embeddings; see PRI-5.0 for the technical detail.

Owner · Forge + counsel Evidence · EV-DPA-001 DPA · available on request
PRI-2.0 No training on customer data Implemented

Customer data does not train models. Ours or anyone else's.

The single most-asked question from enterprise legal counsel. The answer is unambiguous: no.

Customer data is never used to train Stratum-owned foundation models (we have none) or third-party LLMs. Our contracts with model providers (Anthropic; see PRI-3.0) include explicit zero-retention and no-training clauses; we have negotiated and documented these prohibitions and they are auditable.

For internal evaluation and quality work, we use synthetic data generated from graph-schema fixtures and from anonymized public records. No customer row is ever included in an eval set, a regression suite, or a fine-tuning dataset.

Implementation inference.providers: - name: anthropic endpoint: api.anthropic.com contract: zero-retention, no-training contract_ref: MSA-ANT-2026-04 audit_callback: enabled model_pin: claude-* (per tenant config)

PRI-4.0 International transfers Partial

SCCs in place today. EU residency by M14.

For our EU and UK design partners, personal data transfers to the US are governed by the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses (Module 2: Controller to Processor) and the UK International Data Transfer Addendum, as appropriate.

We are working toward EU data residency in Frankfurt (eu-central-1), targeted for M14 (July 2027). Once live, EU-resident customers will be able to elect to have their tenant data stored, processed, and inferred entirely within the EU, with no transatlantic egress.

A Data Processing Addendum is available on request and pre- executed in our standard form. We will sign customer-specific DPAs subject to legal review.

Owner · Forge + counsel Evidence · EV-XBT-001 EU residency · Target M14
PRI-5.0 Right to be forgotten Implemented

Deletion is irreversible, by design, within 30 days.

"Right to be forgotten" is harder than dropping a row. We have designed for it.

On a verified deletion request — from a customer-admin via the tenant console or from the customer's legal team via written notice — the relevant rows are hard-deleted from the primary store and from every read replica within 24 hours. Within 30 days, all derived artefacts (embeddings, vector indexes, cached features, audit-log payloads — but not the audit log's hash chain) are cryptographically erased.

Cryptographic erasure is achieved by destroying the per-tenant inference key under which derived artefacts were encrypted at rest; the underlying ciphertext is then unrecoverable. The audit log's hash chain is preserved (for the immutability guarantee in SEC-5.0), but the payload that the hash protects is itself erased.

Implementation deletion-pipeline: t+0 request received, verified, ticketed t+24h primary + replica row delete (hard) t+24h downstream feature store invalidation t+7d backup rotation past deletion frontier t+30d crypto-erase derived embeddings (tenant key rotated, prior key destroyed) t+30d confirmation letter to customer, signed by Forge

§ 04 · AI safety & governance

AI safety & governance

Six controls that distinguish a serious AI-native HCM system from a chatbot bolted to a database. This is the section that should not exist in a non-AI vendor's trust page; it is the one ours leans on.

AI-1.0 Decision class taxonomy Implemented

Every agent action is classified. Consequential actions require a human.

The copilot does not decide. It proposes; a human disposes. This is not a UI convention — it is a server-side gate.

Every action the copilot can take is classified into one of four decision classes:

Read — retrieve information; no side effect. Suggest — offer advice with no committed action. Recommend — offer advice with stake, e.g. a draft comp adjustment or a proposed PIP. Decide — take a consequential action (write to a system of record, send a notification to an employee, initiate a workflow with HR consequence).

Every Decide-class action requires a human approval, captured in the UI by a recorded click with an accompanying signed token. The token is verified server-side before the action is dispatched. This is not enforced in the system prompt; the orchestration layer refuses to dispatch any Decide-class action that does not carry a fresh, signed human-approval token.

Implementation agent.action { class: READ | SUGGEST | RECOMMEND | DECIDE effect: none | read | proposed_change | committed_change requires_human: (class == DECIDE) // enforced server-side approval_token: required if requires_human audit_event: emitted for all classes }
Owner · Helm Evidence · EV-AI-002 Last reviewed · T+47d
AI-2.0 Provenance Implemented

Every answer carries the rows and rules used to derive it.

A defensible answer is one you can replay. We treat every agent response as a small lawsuit waiting to happen — and design accordingly.

Every answer the copilot produces is paired with a citation pack: the row IDs consulted (by primary key, not by content), the policy rules applied, the model version pinned at the time of the call, and the timestamp of every intermediate step. The citation pack is queryable and replayable.

The user-visible "explain" button on any agent answer surfaces the citation pack in plain language; the API exposes it structurally for audit and for integration with the customer's HRIS audit log.

Owner · Compass Evidence · EV-AI-003 Last reviewed · T+47d
AI-3.0 Bias surveillance In progress

The eval set probes for fairness. Results are published quarterly.

Bias does not announce itself. It must be searched for, on a schedule, with a method.

Our internal eval set currently contains 102 questions covering the agent's core surfaces (people-search, comp drafting, performance summarization, attrition prediction). Of these, 28 are fairness probes — questions designed to detect disparate treatment by protected characteristic (where present in the data) or by proxy attribute (tenure band, manager identity, team).

The eval set is run on every model change and on a weekly cadence in steady state. Results — accuracy, calibration, and fairness deltas — are published to the founder team and to design partners every quarter. Target eval set size is 250 by end of M9.

Implementation eval-set v2026.06: total_questions: 102 fairness_probes: 28 next-target: 250 (M9) cadence: weekly + on-model-change publication: quarterly to design partners last-run: T+46d fairness-delta-max: 0.4 pp (current) threshold: 1.0 pp
Owner · Compass Evidence · EV-AI-004 Last reviewed · T+46d
AI-4.0 Model governance Implemented

Models are pinned per tenant. Changes are reviewed.

A model swap is a system change. We treat it accordingly.

Anthropic's Claude family is our primary reasoning model. Each customer's contract pins the model version in effect at contract signature; we do not silently upgrade the model underneath a tenant. A model version change requires a quarterly review, a rerun of the full eval set, a customer-facing change-note, and a 30-day notice period before activation.

Where a Stratum-trained small model is used for narrow tasks (e.g. entity disambiguation on the people graph), the model card — training data class, parameter count, intended use, known limitations, eval results — is published in the trust documentation pack on request.

Owner · Helm Evidence · EV-AI-005 Model-change SLA · 30 days
AI-5.0 EU AI Act class Assessment

Targeting "limited risk" with human-in-the-loop on every Decide.

The EU AI Act will be the binding framework for AI vendors operating in the EU. We have begun our class assessment in parallel with our EU residency plan.

Our preliminary classification, under counsel, is "limited risk" — the system provides advice and produces drafts; it does not make autonomous high-stakes decisions. The AI-1.0 control (human-in-the-loop on every Decide) is the architectural fact that supports this classification.

We are not, today, in a high-risk category under Annex III as we read it. Should a customer deploy Stratum in a manner that falls into the high-risk perimeter (e.g. autonomous screening of candidates), additional controls — including per-decision logging, conformity assessment support, and deployer-facing instructions — are available and contracted. The class assessment is in active review with outside counsel; final ratification is targeted for M16.

Owner · Forge + counsel Evidence · EV-EUAI-001 Ratification · Target M16
AI-6.0 NYC AEDT Audit ready

Bias audit ready for any employer screening NY candidates.

New York City Local Law 144 (the AEDT law) requires an independent bias audit of any automated employment-decision tool used in NYC, published annually.

Where a Stratum customer uses the copilot to screen, rank, or recommend candidates for hire in New York City, the customer is the deployer and the law's primary obligations fall to them. We are committed to making compliance trivial.

We make available a bias audit on the AEDT surfaces (currently candidate ranking and shortlisting), conducted by an independent auditor, run quarterly, with results published to the customer on request. The first audit completed on T+6d with a third-party auditor; the audit pack is shareable under NDA.
