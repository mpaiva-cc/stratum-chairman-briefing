The question Marcus Tang had been asking for three years was disarmingly simple: of the $4.2 million Northwind spent on creative-recruiting agencies in 2025, how much produced a hire that stayed past their first year? Lila Park, Northwind's CHRO, had given him three different answers. She did not believe any of them.

What follows is the story of how a 1,200-person agency network — operating across four cities, two ATSs, and a cultural memory of how creative recruiting is supposed to work — finally produced a defensible number, and what it cost both Lila and us to get there. The number is in the piece. So is what it changed, and what it has not yet.

The series rules

What we publish, and what we don't.

What we publish
Real engagements, with the customer's written consent and every quotation confirmed by the speaker. Reviewed by the customer's CHRO and CIO before we hit publish. Names of internal-only individuals are lightly anonymised only where they ask us to.
What we don't
Customer names, if they don't want to be named. We have one design partner who has asked us not to write about them. We don't. We won't, and we have taken that page out of the deck we show prospects.
