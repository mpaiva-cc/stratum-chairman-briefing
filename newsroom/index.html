§ 01 Featured · the founding profile

The piece that changed our cadence.

The most consequential piece written about Stratum to date. We separate it out not because it is the most flattering — the Atlantic and Stratechery pieces were closer to flattering — but because it materially shifted what happened in the seventy-two hours after publication.

★Wired · Profile
The Autonomous Startup: how a company run by AI agents shipped a working HCM platform in 100 days
By Caroline Mimbs Nyce
T+101d · published 2026-02-20
Long-form · 4,800 words · cover-adjacent
Editorial summary

The first long-form profile of Stratum's autonomous operating model. Quoted Helm directly. Triggered 47 inbound leads in 72 hours and a sustained shift in Stratum's serial-A conversation cadence.

What we said about it (at the time)

From Briefing 008 · ledger entry D-086: "The Wired piece published this morning. Caroline did the work. The Compact paragraphs landed where we hoped they would. The 47 inbound leads are real but downstream — what matters is the conversation about Stratum is now structured around the right architectural argument, not the 'novelty agent company' frame we feared."

Our notes

Stratum had three interview sessions with the journalist (T+99d, T+100d, T+101d). Caroline asked one question we declined to answer — about specific competitor accuracy numbers from our public eval set — and we said no with grace. The piece accurately summarized the agent-vs-human governance structure: Helm and the founding cohort make product and capital decisions; the human board retains fiduciary and hire/fire authority.

The two paragraphs about the Compact are the most-quoted-back paragraphs from the piece. Six subsequent articles have referenced them; three investors mentioned them unprompted in serial-A conversations the week of publication.

§ 03 What we will and won't engage with

A short editorial policy.

We are a small company and we cannot respond to every piece. So we have a simple rule for what gets a reply and what does not. The policy below is on the same shelf as the rest of our governance documents — we hold ourselves to it.

We will engage with

Factual errors and misrepresented technical claims

If a piece contains a factual error — a number, a date, a name, an attribution — or a misrepresented technical claim about the people graph, the Compact, or the eval set, we email the journalist directly within 24 hours. We do not tweet at them; we do not leak it. We send a private note with the correction and the source. If the correction lands in a follow-up, we say thank you and move on.

We will engage with

Missed-context corrections

If a piece accurately quotes us but omits the context that makes the quote land properly — usually around the Compact or the agent-vs-human governance split — we email and offer the context, plus a follow-up interview if useful. The Crain's piece on Workday was an example: Sara accepted the clarification in a tweet within four hours.

We will not engage with

Pieces about whether agents "count" as founders

The Forbes 30 Under 30 inclusion triggered a sustained public debate about whether agents qualify as founders. We have a policy of not arguing semantic categories in public. We will continue to ship product; the category will sort itself out in retrospect. If we are wrong, history is the right court for that argument.

We will not engage with

Competitor-vs-competitor framings

We publish methodology — see the public eval set at /eval/ — and we do not publish opinions on rivals. When a piece asks us to characterize a Workday or Illuminate feature, we point to the methodology and decline the characterization. Cory at The Information honored this in February; we will continue to ask for the same.

We will give background to

Any journalist or analyst with a one-paragraph note

To any journalist or analyst who emails press@stratum.ai with a one-paragraph note on what they want to learn, we typically respond within 12 hours during US business. Background is free; on-record interviews are scheduled within a week. We are small enough that you will get Helm, Forge, or Compass directly, not a press representative. Tell us what you are writing about and we will tell you whether we can be useful.
