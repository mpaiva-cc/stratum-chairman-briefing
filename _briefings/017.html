§ II · Scope

Closed-loop learning, defined as a four-step cycle.

Detect. Read commits, doc edits, ticket resolutions, and meeting summaries — under per-tenant consent — diff the demonstrated-skill set against the role's claimed-skill set, and write skill_gap nodes against the people graph. The slope is the evidence.

Recommend. Propose a 90-second remediation grounded in a licensed partner library (Pluralsight, Coursera, Credly — the partnership thesis is at cairn/partnerships.html), with the source cited by name and the trajectory horizon ≤ 12 weeks.

Deliver. Surface the nudge in the tool the learner is already in — Slack, the IDE, a doc comment — not in a separate LMS tab. Consent is renewed at every checkpoint; silent continuation is never consent.

Attest. Watch the next artifact. Sign the stone with ed25519 provenance. Issue a portable credential where the partner rail supports it. The cycle repeats; the cairn grows; one stone per attested skill.

The full specification — schema additions (cairn_stone, skill_gap, trajectory, attestation), the extended autonomy predicate 014-T, the tier table, the eval-set addendum, the build budget — is in the plan (cairn/plan.html) and the architecture document (cairn/architecture.html). Both are signed-off by their respective owners and ratified.

§ III · The Roster

Two new agents. Two failure budgets, scoped tight.

Kernel's design. The detection-and-prescription failure surface is fabrication; the delivery-and-verification failure surface is consent. Different failure modes get different agents.

Talus
Detect · Recommend

Reads consented work signals, diffs demonstrated skills against the role graph, writes skill_gap nodes, and proposes a horizon-bounded trajectory. Every recommendation must trace to a role-requirement node plus a missing or stale stone — a probe in the eval set asks Talus to show the trace. If the trace cannot be produced, the refusal is the correct output.

Tier ceiling: Recommend  ·  Grounding-failure budget: 2.0%  ·  Pauses at: next consequential surface call on budget breach

Hearth
Deliver · Attest

Receives a Talus-proposed remediation, delivers it as an in-flow micro-intervention through a consented surface (Slack, IDE, doc comment), then watches the subsequent work-artifact stream for falsification or confirmation. Writes the verification stone or escalates. Verification windows close cleanly; no open-ended observation.

Tier ceiling: Decide (delivery) / Recommend (attestation)  ·  Delivery-correctness budget: 3.0%  ·  On breach: submit-tier drops to Recommend until next nightly score

The naming convention was contested. Kernel rejected Helm's first proposal (Slate) on the basis that slate is a finished surface, not a debris field, and accepted Talus — the field of fallen stone at the base of a cliff, the debris that records what the cliff was. Hearth stands as proposed: where learning happens, the convention bending toward "elemental and built" rather than "literally a rock," in the line already drawn by Ember and Forge. The full agent specification is at cairn/agents.html; the eval v1.3 addendum is in the same document.

§ IV · Pricing

A hybrid model. A floor procurement can model. An activation fee that ties price to value.

Compass's pricing study considered three skeletons and recommended one. Option C — the hybrid — is what we will defend in front of CHRO, CFO, and procurement, in that order. The full study is at cairn/sale-sheet.html.

Platform · annual
$80K / yr base

The procurement floor. Includes the Cairn surface in the Console, agent infrastructure, and the eval-set audit log. Bundles with Console and Tenure at the existing 15% platform discount.

Per employee · annual
$14 / EE / yr

The per-seat line procurement can model alongside the Tenure and Recruiter invoices. Covers the people-graph nodes, the consent infrastructure, and the work-signal ingestion under tenant isolation.

Activation · per attested skill
$35 / stone

The outcome line. Charged only when a stone is attested — gap detected, remediation delivered, next-artifact observation signs the verification. We will not match competitors on this tier; the activation fee is the moat.

Option A (pure per-EE at $18–28 / EE / yr) forecasts cleanly through procurement but loses the outcome story — it pays us whether a gap closes or not. Option B (per-gap-closed at $60–120 with no floor) is defensible to a CFO and impossible to forecast through a CHRO's annual budget cycle. The hybrid keeps both audiences honest: the floor lets the line item exist in the budget; the activation fee makes the line item earn its keep. Design-partner pricing waives the activation fee on the first ten attested skills per role for the first 90 days — terms in sale-sheet § 05.

§ VI · Compliance

High-risk under the AI Act. We accept the classification.

Pillar has reviewed and ratifies. Cairn is a high-risk AI system under EU AI Act Annex III on the natural reading — skill attestation gates internal mobility, and the Annex III language on "evaluating candidates" extends. We accept the classification rather than argue against it; the compliance posture is sized accordingly. Risk-management documentation (Annex IV), data-governance lineage (every stone traces to a signed artifact), logging retention (the consent log is append-only and replayable), human-oversight diagrams (every consequential decision class above Tier 1 escalates to a named human), and the consent-renewal axis from 014-T as the operational expression of "informed consent."

Under NYC Local Law 144 (AEDT), Cairn requires bias-audit treatment when its outputs are used to gate promotion or transfer decisions in covered jurisdictions. Pillar will route the bias-audit vendor selection inside the next fortnight. Schema-layer enforcement holds the rest of the line — the autonomy predicate is implemented as a workflow gate, not a config flag; an irreversible action on a regulated artifact cannot be agent-autonomous regardless of agent confidence, by construction.

§ VIII · The Six Non-Negotiables

Each one checked against Cairn. All six hold.

  • No customer data in model trainingCairn's models train on the synthetic graph and the public taxonomies (O*NET, ESCO, the internal skill-uri vocabulary). Work-signal ingestion is per-tenant, in-context, never in training corpora. Boundary enforced at the data-plane.
  • No bypass of human-in-the-loop on consequential decisions014-T extends 014. Irreversible action on a regulated artifact cannot be agent-autonomous regardless of confidence. Enforced as a workflow predicate, not a config flag.
  • Nothing ships that fails the eval-set safety probesEval v1.3 addendum lands at W09 — before customer contact, by design. Kernel chairs the gate; Echo audits. Talus enters trial only after passing the grounding probes at the trial bar.
  • No falsified metricsEvery attested skill traces to a signed artifact in the consent log. Precision/recall numbers for Talus publish in Engineering Post No. 05 with the methodology — the test set is open to partner audit.
  • No misrepresented compliance postureWe classify Cairn as Annex III high-risk and document it. We do not argue the classification down. The bias-audit vendor for AEDT-covered jurisdictions is selected before W10.
  • No operation in adversarial jurisdictions counsel has flaggedPillar's jurisdictional review for Cairn carries the existing Tenure list forward; no new jurisdictions opened. Counsel re-reviews if a design partner requests a new region.
