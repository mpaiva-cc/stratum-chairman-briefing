When these terms start binding you.

These terms govern your use of the products and services offered at stratum.ai, including the Console, the public API, the Connectors, the Decisions module, and any related customer-facing tools (collectively, the Service).

¶ 1.01 By creating an account, accessing the public Console demo, calling the API, or otherwise using the Service, you agree to be bound by these terms. If you do not agree, do not use the Service.

¶ 1.02 If you use the Service on behalf of a legal entity — your employer, your client, the company whose payroll you administer — you represent that you have authority to bind that entity. References to "you" or "Customer" in these terms include that entity.

¶ 1.03 Enterprise customers contract with Stratum under a separately signed Master Services Agreement (the MSA). Where you have an MSA in force, the MSA governs and these terms supplement it only to the extent the MSA is silent. Where a provision of these terms conflicts with a provision of the MSA, the MSA controls.

¶ 1.04 Stratum may publish additional product-specific terms — for example, terms covering the Beta program, the Connectors marketplace, or the public eval-set program. Where published, those terms incorporate by reference into your overall agreement and govern that specific feature.

¶ 1.05 Capitalized terms used but not defined in a given section have the meanings given in § 2.

A note on the public Console demo The public Console at stratum.ai/console is offered as a no-account, read-only sandbox running against a synthetic 4,200-employee dataset called Helio Robotics. Using the public demo binds you to these terms even though no account or payment is required. The demo is provided AS IS; the warranties in § 11 do not extend to demo use.

§ 3 · Eligibility & accounts

Who can use the Service, and how.

The Service is built for enterprise HR organizations and their authorized administrators. The eligibility rules below apply to any use of the Service, including the public demo.

§ 3.1 Eligibility

  1. You must be at least 18 years old to use the Service. Stratum is not built for, and does not knowingly accept, users under 18.
  2. You must not be barred from receiving the Service under U.S. or other applicable export-control or sanctions law. Without limiting the foregoing, you may not use the Service if you are located in, ordinarily resident in, or organized under the laws of any jurisdiction that is subject to a comprehensive U.S. trade embargo.
  3. You may not use the Service if you are a competitor of Stratum and are evaluating the Service for the purpose of building a competing product, unless you have disclosed that intent in writing to Stratum and Stratum has consented in writing. For the avoidance of doubt, ordinary competitive intelligence (reading the website, attending public events, reviewing the changelog) is not a violation of this clause; signing into the Console under false pretenses is.
  4. If Stratum has reason to believe that you have provided false information, are misusing the Service, or are otherwise in breach of these terms, Stratum may suspend or terminate your access immediately without liability.

§ 3.2 Accounts and identity

  1. Customer accounts are managed via single sign-on (SSO) through the Customer's identity provider. Stratum supports Okta, Azure Active Directory, Google Workspace, and SAML 2.0–compliant IdPs generally. Local accounts (username + password) are not offered for production Customers.
  2. The Customer is responsible for provisioning, deprovisioning, and managing the rights of its End Users. Stratum will accept and enforce IdP-issued claims as authoritative.
  3. The Customer designates one or more administrators (each, an Admin) who may manage configuration, access controls, and integration scope. Admins act on behalf of the Customer for purposes of these terms.
  4. End Users are responsible for the confidentiality of their credentials and for all activity in their account. Customers will notify Stratum without undue delay upon learning of any unauthorized access.
  5. Stratum will provide audit-log access showing identity events, authorization changes, and material actions taken in the Console. Audit logs are retained per the schedule published in the Trust Center.

§ 3.3 Public Console demo

  1. The public Console demo at stratum.ai/console may be used without authentication. No personally identifying data is required.
  2. The demo runs against the synthetic Helio Robotics dataset. Demo users may not upload personal data, customer data, or any data that is not their own to disclose.
  3. Stratum logs demo-session telemetry for product improvement. Telemetry is anonymized; see the Privacy Policy for details.
  4. Stratum may impose rate limits, session limits, or feature limits on the public demo at its sole discretion.

  4. Stratum may make commercially reasonable changes to the Service during the Term provided that no such change materially degrades the documented functionality of the Service. Material adverse changes are subject to the notice provisions of § 16.

§ 4.2 License grant to Stratum

In particular, Stratum will not use Customer Data to train general-purpose machine-learning models. In plain English We own the product. You own the data. The Output of the product belongs to you for your business, but the methods that produced it are ours. We do not train foundation models on what we see at your company.

§ 6 · Fees and payment

How we get paid.

Stratum's pricing is annual-platform plus quarterly-decisions, as published at stratum.ai/pricing. The specifics of an individual Customer's pricing are in the MSA. The mechanics below apply in either case.

§ 6.1 Pricing

  1. Customer's Fees are set out in the MSA. Absent an MSA, the public pricing posted at stratum.ai/pricing applies.
  2. The Stratum pricing model is composed of (a) an annual Platform Fee, prepaid; and (b) a quarterly Decisions Tier based on the volume of decisions the Service supports, invoiced in arrears.
  3. Customers do not pay by seat. Adding or removing End Users does not change Customer's Platform Fee within a Term. Excessive End Users for the contracted scope (more than the published soft cap for the Customer's tier) is a basis for an upgrade conversation but not an automatic charge.
  4. Stratum publishes a public price list and a usage-tier calculator. The published list is the price. We do not run undisclosed list-price-versus-floor games.

§ 6.2 Invoicing and payment

  1. The Platform Fee is invoiced annually on or about the Effective Date and is payable net 30 days from receipt of invoice.
  2. The Decisions Tier is invoiced quarterly in arrears and is payable net 30 days from receipt of invoice.
  3. Invoices are delivered electronically to the email or AP address designated by Customer. Customer is responsible for keeping that address current.
  4. Stratum accepts payment by ACH, wire transfer, or other electronic method. Stratum does not accept credit cards for invoices over $25,000. Stratum does not assess a credit-card surcharge for payments below that threshold.
  5. All Fees are stated in U.S. Dollars unless the MSA specifies otherwise. Customers in jurisdictions other than the U.S. may, by mutual agreement, transact in a local currency at the prevailing rate.

§ 6.3 Late payment

  1. Past-due amounts accrue interest at the lesser of 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, beginning on the day after the due date.
  2. Stratum may suspend the Service for non-payment beyond 60 days past due, with at least 10 days' prior written notice. Suspension is without prejudice to Stratum's rights to terminate for cause under § 14.3.
  3. Stratum will not exercise self-help remedies that compromise Customer Data integrity (deletion, alteration, or denial of export) on the basis of non-payment alone. Customer's data-export rights under § 14.5 are not contingent on payment of disputed amounts.

§ 6.4 Disputed invoices

  1. Customer may dispute an invoice in good faith by providing written notice to billing@stratum.ai within 30 days of receipt of the invoice. The notice will identify the disputed line items and the basis for dispute.
  2. Disputed amounts are not subject to late-payment interest under § 6.3 during the pendency of a good-faith dispute, provided the undisputed portion is paid on time.
  3. The parties will work in good faith to resolve invoice disputes within 30 days of notice. Unresolved disputes are subject to the dispute-resolution provisions of § 15.

§ 6.5 Taxes

  1. Fees are stated exclusive of taxes. Customer is responsible for all sales, use, value-added, withholding, and similar taxes assessed on the Fees, excluding taxes on Stratum's net income.
  2. If Customer is required by law to withhold any tax from a payment to Stratum, Customer will gross up the payment so Stratum receives the full invoiced amount.
  3. Customer will provide Stratum with valid exemption certificates where applicable.

§ 6.6 Renewal and price changes

  1. Annual contracts auto-renew for successive one-year terms unless either party gives written notice of non-renewal at least 60 days before the end of the then-current Term.
  2. Stratum will publish renewal pricing at least 90 days before the end of the then-current Term. Stratum's renewal price increase will not exceed the lesser of (a) 7% per annum, or (b) the trailing-twelve-month U.S. Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers (CPI-U) plus 3 percentage points — except where the Customer is migrating between published pricing tiers, in which case the price for the new tier applies.
  3. The renewal-cap provision of ¶ 6.6.2 may be modified by the MSA but, in the absence of MSA language, applies as a floor protection for the Customer.
§ 7 · SLA and credits

The uptime commitment, written down.

Service-level commitments are contractual, not aspirational. The exact uptime targets and credit schedule are below. Live current-quarter performance is published at stratum.ai/status.

§ 7.1 Uptime commitment

  1. Stratum commits to 99.9% monthly uptime for Customers on the Platform tier.
  2. Stratum commits to 99.95% monthly uptime for Customers on the Platform + Decisions tier, with a corresponding latency target of 95th-percentile chat-response time under 4 seconds and 99th-percentile under 11 seconds.
  3. Uptime is measured monthly, as a percentage of total minutes in the calendar month during which the Service was available to the Customer.
  4. The Service is considered unavailable when (a) the Console returns a 5xx error in response to a well-formed request from an authenticated End User for more than 60 seconds; or (b) the API returns 5xx error rates above 5% across all endpoints for more than 60 seconds; or (c) the chat-response latency exceeds 30 seconds for an aggregate of more than 5% of requests in any 5-minute window. Stratum's status page provides the system of record.

§ 7.2 Exclusions

  1. Scheduled maintenance windows announced at least 72 hours in advance and conducted within the announced window. Stratum schedules maintenance during a published low-traffic window for each Customer time zone.
  2. Emergency maintenance required to remediate a security vulnerability, in which case Stratum will provide as much notice as is reasonable in the circumstances.
  3. Customer-caused outages, including outages caused by Customer's IdP, Customer's network, Customer's API misuse (rate-limit violations, malformed requests), or Customer's BYOK API-key exhaustion or revocation.
  4. Outages caused by a Force Majeure Event, as defined in § 17.5.
  5. Outages caused by a third-party upstream service explicitly outside Stratum's control, where Stratum has acted in good faith to mitigate (e.g., a regional outage of the Customer's chosen model provider, a Connector outage at the Customer's HRIS vendor).

§ 7.3 Reporting

  1. Stratum publishes near-real-time service status, current-quarter SLA performance, and a 90-day incident history at stratum.ai/status.
  2. Within 5 business days of the close of each calendar month, Stratum will publish that month's uptime measurement to the status page and make it available to Customer on request.
  3. For any incident that materially affects the Customer's use of the Service for more than one hour, Stratum will publish a post-mortem within 14 days describing root cause, customer impact, remediation, and preventive measures.

§ 7.4 Service credits

  1. If Stratum fails to meet its uptime commitment in a given calendar month, Customer is entitled to a credit equal to 5% of the monthly equivalent of the Platform Fee for every 0.1 percentage points below the target, calculated as: credit % = (target − actual) ÷ 0.1 × 5%.
  2. The monthly credit is capped at 25% of the monthly equivalent of the Platform Fee, regardless of the magnitude of the miss.
  3. For the Decisions Tier, an SLA miss in a calendar month entitles Customer to a 25% credit against that quarter's Decisions invoice in addition to the Platform credit.
  4. Credits are applied to the next regular invoice. Credits do not expire and may be carried forward, but credits are not redeemable for cash except upon termination of the Term, when any then-outstanding credits are refunded in cash within 30 days.
  5. To claim a credit, Customer submits a written request to sla@stratum.ai within 30 days of the close of the affected month. Stratum will respond within 10 business days.
  6. Service credits are the exclusive remedy for SLA misses except where the miss also constitutes a breach of these terms or an MSA provision giving rise to additional rights.

§ 7.5 Chronic underperformance

  1. If Stratum fails to meet the applicable uptime target in three consecutive calendar months, or in any four months in a rolling twelve-month window, Customer may terminate the Term for cause under § 14.3 without observing the cure period, and is entitled to a pro-rata refund of the prepaid Platform Fee for the unused portion of the Term.
Our position on credits Credits are a real but small mechanism. The honest commitment is to operate the Service well enough that they are rarely needed, and to be transparent in the rare moments when they are. We do not bury credit eligibility in eligibility-by-eligibility checklists. If the status page shows the miss, the credit is owed.
§ 8 · Confidentiality

What is confidential, in both directions.

Each party will encounter the other's confidential information during the engagement. The protections are mutual and ordinary.

§ 8.1 Definitions of confidential information

  1. "Customer Confidential Information" includes Customer Data, Customer's business plans, financial information, personnel matters, technical and operational data shared with Stratum, and any information identified in writing or by context as confidential.
  2. "Stratum Confidential Information" includes Stratum's pricing (including non-public discount tables), product roadmap, methodology, eval-set scoring details, infrastructure architecture, security policies, internal performance data, and any information identified in writing or by context as confidential.
  3. Confidential Information does not include information that (a) is or becomes publicly known through no fault of the receiving party; (b) was rightfully known to the receiving party before disclosure; (c) is rightfully received from a third party without restriction; or (d) is independently developed by the receiving party without reference to the disclosing party's Confidential Information.

§ 8.2 Protection

  1. Each party will protect the other's Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of similar importance, and in no event less than reasonable care.
  2. The receiving party will use the disclosing party's Confidential Information only as necessary to perform under, exercise rights under, or evaluate the engagement, and will not disclose it to any third party except (a) to its employees, contractors, and advisors who have a need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective; or (b) as required by law, subject to ¶ 8.3.
  3. The obligations under this § 8 continue during the Term and for a period of five (5) years after termination, except for Customer Data and trade-secret-class information, which continue indefinitely.

§ 8.3 Legally compelled disclosure

  1. If the receiving party is legally compelled to disclose Confidential Information (by subpoena, court order, regulator request, or similar), it will, to the extent legally permitted, give the disclosing party prompt written notice and cooperate at the disclosing party's expense in any effort to seek a protective order or otherwise limit disclosure.
  2. Stratum publishes its Government Information Requests policy at stratum.ai/trust. Stratum does not provide back-door access; lawful process is required and contested where appropriate.

§ 8.4 Remedies

  1. The parties acknowledge that breach of confidentiality may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages are inadequate. The disclosing party is entitled to seek injunctive or equitable relief in addition to any other remedies available.

¶ 20.01 These terms are stable. Stratum's commitment to its Customers is to amend them only when amendment is genuinely warranted — by a material change in the law, in the product, or in the way the company operates. We will not push small redlines through the document to chase fashion.

¶ 20.02 This document was drafted by Stratum, Inc., with the advice of Wilson Sonsini Goodrich & Rosati. It was reviewed by counsel for five enterprise design-partner Customers prior to publication. The redlines from that review materially improved the document. Where their counsel disagreed with our drafting and we agreed with their counsel, we changed the language; where their counsel disagreed and we did not agree, we explained why in the public design-partner notes (available on request).

¶ 20.03 These terms are a contract. They are also a public artifact — a statement of how Stratum wants to be in business with its Customers. We hope they read like both.
