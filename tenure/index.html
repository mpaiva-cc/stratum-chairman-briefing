The category called this onboarding. It is bigger than that.

The legacy HCM tools sell onboarding as a checklist. Workday Journeys, Rippling Onboarding, BambooHR — workflow engines, document tasks, manager nudges. None of them treat the day a person joins, the day they transfer, the day they take parental leave, the day they leave as the same shape of problem.

They are. Each one is an employee event: a moment when something changes about a person's relationship to your company. Each one has a paper trail. Each one has a decision that someone — or now, something — has to defend.

Stratum Tenure is the operating layer for those events. It runs on top of your system of record, draws on the same people graph that powers the Console, and ships with three new agents who do the work — bounded by the same charter-and-defense posture the Compact ratified in Briefing No. 014.

Every promotion, transfer, anniversary, and exit at your company is already a decision. We just made the decision — and the defense — visible. — Helm · Chairman's Briefing No. 016
§ The roster

Three agents, three surfaces, three failure budgets.

Lifecycle moments do not share a failure surface. The day-1 mis-provision and the 10-year anniversary card require different agents with different decision classes. Kernel designed the boundary.

Threshold
The line you cross

Owns the irreversible writes — pre-boarding through Day 30, plus termination day. Equipment, identity, paperwork, payroll, asset reclaim. The agent we trust with the regulated forms because we wrote the boundary down.

  • Equipment provisioning Decide
  • IT account creation (standard role) Decide
  • IT account (elevated privilege) Recommend
  • Offer-letter PDF generation Suggest
  • I-9 / E-Verify orchestration Read-only
  • Payroll record creation Recommend
  • Termination-day asset reclaim Recommend
Lattice
The structure you join

Owns the relational moments. Buddy assignment, manager check-in nudges, mentorship pairing, the choreography of team introductions. Reversible, social-graph aware, low regulatory surface. The agent that makes onboarding feel like joining a place, not completing a form.

  • Buddy assignment Decide
  • Manager check-in cadence Decide
  • Mentor-pair recommendation Recommend
  • Team-intro choreography Decide
  • First 30/60/90 plan draft Suggest
  • LOA-return re-onboarding sequence Decide
Ember
The small warmth

Owns recognition. Anniversaries, milestones, promotion announcements, leave acknowledgements, the goodbye note when someone retires. Lowest stakes by failure budget, highest stakes by brand voice. The agent that remembers your tenth year on a Tuesday.

  • Work-anniversary acknowledgement Decide
  • Promotion announcement (draft) Suggest
  • Parental-leave wraparound Decide
  • Retirement send-off Suggest
  • Internal-move welcome Decide
  • Bereavement / medical leave Human only
§ The decision class

Four tiers. One rule.

The agent acts autonomously when the worst case is we apologize and reissue. The human signs when the worst case is we get sued, fined, or made the news. Briefing 014 wrote this down. Tenure enforces it as a workflow predicate.

Tier I

Decide

The agent does the thing. Reversible within 24 hours. No regulated artifacts. Failure mode is "we apologize and try again."

Tier II

Recommend

The agent prepares, a human approves with one click. IdP groups, role-start dates, equipment over $1.5K, equity-grant scheduling (never amounts).

Tier III

Suggest

The agent drafts; the human signs. Offer-letter copy, promotion announcement language, 30/60/90 plans. Voice matters more than speed.

Tier IV

Read-only

The agent surfaces information. Cannot act. I-9 verification, E-Verify case submission, EEOC adverse-impact reports, bereavement.
