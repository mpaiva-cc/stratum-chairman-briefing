Stratum / Tenure · The Tour · Six scenes · One employee

Meet Maria. She joins on June 2.

What follows is the storyboard for Stratum Tenure. Six lifecycle moments — pre-board, Day 0, the first week, an internal transfer at Day 90, a three-year anniversary, and a voluntary departure — each handled by one of three agents. Every action is bounded by an explicit decision class, and every human step is named. When Tenure goes live with your real worker data, this is the workflow that runs.

Scene 01 / 06 T −14 to T −1 · pre-board Threshold Decide Recommend Read-only

Fourteen days before Day 0, Threshold opens the runway.

Maria's offer is signed at 09:42 on May 12. Within thirty seconds, Threshold opens her record in the people graph, claims the joiner workflow, and starts the gates. The standard MacBook Pro is requested from Apple Business Manager. The background check is ordered through Checkr. The Section 1 of her I-9 is pre-populated from the offer-letter data and sent for her to sign. Priya — her manager — gets a brief: here is what your new hire's first two weeks look like; here are the moments where you'll be needed.

Threshold acts. It does not decide. Equipment standard tier is reversible (the agent can cancel the order until shipment). I-9 Section 2 — the part DHS forbids delegation on — sits in the Recommend column for Priya to handle in person on Day 0.

Boundary Threshold prepares the I-9. A human verifies the documents and signs Section 2. DHS guidance is explicit: the employer attestation cannot be made by an autonomous agent.

Scene 02 / 06 Day 0 · June 2, 2026 · 08:30 PT Threshold Lattice Decide

Day 0. The crossing.

At 08:30, the BGC has cleared. Threshold opens Maria's Okta account, grants her Slack and Notion seats with read-only permissions on Engineering channels, prepares her GitHub access scoped to the public-engineering repos only. Her badge is ready at the front desk; the receptionist gets a one-line note ("Maria Chen, Eng, Priya's hire, please collect ID for I-9 Section 2"). Lattice picks up Maria's buddy assignment — paired with David Park (joined March 2026, same team, same career-level + 1) — and schedules the first three meetings.

Elevated privileges (admin scopes, billing access, production secrets) are not granted by Threshold. They're held in the Recommend column for Priya to approve role-by-role over Maria's first 30 days.

Boundary Standard-role IdP provisioning is autonomous (Decide). Admin / finance / PHI scopes always escalate (Recommend). Threshold never grants prod access on Day 0.

Scene 03 / 06 Day 7 · June 9, 2026 Lattice Decide Suggest

Day 7. The footing pulse.

A week in. Lattice runs a quiet check: paperwork audit (all forms returned), access audit (no privilege creep), buddy check-in confirmed (David and Maria had coffee twice, both rated the meetings 4/5), 30/60/90 plan drafted by Lattice from Priya's template and Maria's role brief — waiting for Priya's review before it goes to Maria. Lattice posts a one-line note to Priya: everything's on track; here's the plan to review by Friday.

No action required from anyone unless something's wrong. That's the design. Onboarding fizzles at week 3 in legacy tools because nobody owns the cadence. Lattice owns it.

Scene 04 / 06 Day 90 · August 31, 2026 · Internal transfer Threshold Lattice Decide Recommend

Day 90. Maria moves teams.

Maria's been an obvious Infrastructure-team fit for weeks. Priya and the Infra lead, Aisha, agree the move. Maria says yes. In legacy HCM, this is a six-week paperwork exercise: requisition reapproved, comp-band re-evaluated, IdP scopes manually re-issued, emails forwarded. With Tenure, it's an event.

Threshold revokes Maria's Eng-team access on the day of the move, grants Infra access according to the standard Infra role template, swaps her manager edge from Priya to Aisha, archives her Eng-team Notion docs into her personal vault, and updates her payroll cost-center. Lattice schedules the team-intro choreography on the Infra side: new buddy (Sam Liu), new 1:1 cadence, new 30/60/90 starter draft. Comp doesn't change (lateral move, same band). The transfer happens in a single afternoon. The only human signs are Priya, Aisha, and Maria.

Boundary Internal moves that change pay band or org go to Recommend. Lateral moves at the same comp band are Decide. The decision rule is published; the audit log is permanent.

Scene 05 / 06 3-year anniversary · June 2, 2029 Ember Suggest Decide

Three years in. Ember remembers on a Tuesday.

Maria's three-year anniversary is June 2, 2029. Forty-eight hours before, Ember drafts a personalized acknowledgement and quietly puts it on Aisha's Monday morning checklist: "Wednesday is Maria's 3-year mark. Here's a draft of what you might say — it references the RFC she authored last year and the on-call rotation she designed. Edit or send." Aisha can send it, edit it, or skip it. Ember doesn't post anything publicly without a human's hand on the keyboard.

The work-anniversary acknowledgement itself — the kind that just notes the milestone with no manager involvement — is Decide. Ember writes those and ships them. The promotion-adjacent ones, the role-change-adjacent ones, the ones where voice matters more than speed: Suggest. A human signs.

Scene 06 / 06 5 years in · departure Threshold Recommend Read-only

Five years in, Maria takes a Director role elsewhere.

Maria gives two weeks' notice. Threshold doesn't act. Threshold prepares. It composes the exit packet — final paycheck schedule, COBRA letter, equity-vest-schedule snapshot, last-day calendar removal — and surfaces the proposed timing to Aisha. Threshold checks the legal-status field: is HR aware? Yes (Maria told them yesterday). Is there a counter? No. Is there an active investigation? No. The gate clears.

Then Aisha approves the timing. Then Threshold acts: schedules the IdP revocation for 17:00 on Maria's last day, the device reclaim for the next morning, the Slack handle deactivation for two weeks after. Asset reclaim is reversible in theory; timing is not. The agent does not press send on a termination workflow until a human has named the date.

Boundary Departure timing is Recommend. The action is reversible, the timing is not — early reclaim before legal sign-off is a wrongful-termination amplifier. The agent prepares; a human approves the moment.
