§ 02 The thesis

Why this company, now.

The full thesis lives in the deck. Three short paragraphs here for an investor reading this page cold.

01
HCM is a $34B category dominated by four incumbents with a median customer NPS of 11. The AI-native displacement window is open for the next 24–36 months. After that, the incumbents will have learned the new vocabulary and an entrant will need ten times the capital to win the same conversation.
02
Stratum is the system of truth, not a new system of record. We don't compete with Workday's full breadth; we sit above it. This is a defensible position because the architecture compounds: every customer connector improves the people graph, every answer improves the eval set, every audit improves the trust posture. Workday's flank is the integration boundary, and that boundary widens with AI.
03
The wedge is the CHRO's copilot. A single user (the CHRO), a clear pain (Tuesday afternoons), a defensible answer (the eval set). The product, the marketing, and the GTM all reinforce. We are not trying to be everywhere; we are trying to be somewhere, and then to be impossible to remove.

§ 05 For prospective Series A investors

We are not in a process. Yet.

Stratum is currently not in active Series A fundraising. We are in conversation cadence — eight firms have introduced themselves so far. We are deliberately not running a process until Q1 2027 because the most valuable thing we can do right now is build. The strongest pitch we will have at Series A is the customer reference call we will be able to offer; we want to earn that call before we run a deck past anyone.

Eight months of additional traction will compound the metrics that matter — ARR, retention, eval-set scores, named references — far more than eight months of investor meetings would. We will be more honest, and you will get a cleaner read on the company, if we wait.
