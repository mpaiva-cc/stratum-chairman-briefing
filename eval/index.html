§ III · The methodology

How the set is built, measured, and refreshed.

§ How questions are added

Composition

The 250 questions are sourced, not invented. They are the questions our customers, advisors, and independent CHROs actually ask — collected, deduplicated, and rewritten to a common schema.

  • Mercator / Halcyon / Northwind CHRO interviews 80
  • Stratum founder workshops 60
  • CHRO advisory panel (independent) 60
  • Public benchmarks (Sapient, Mercer) 50
§ How accuracy is measured

Scoring

A held-out subset of fifty questions runs nightly against the production model. CHRO advisors review answers quarterly, in a structured rubric covering cohort fidelity, statistical correctness, and uncertainty calibration. Bias probes are evaluated by an external auditor.

  • Held-out nightly subset 50 q
  • Quarterly CHRO review full 250
  • Bias-probe audit (Rhino) quarterly
  • Refusal-probe pass rate 98.1%
§ How the set is refreshed

Rotation

The set is a living artifact. Fifty questions are added each quarter; fifty are retired — the oldest, the lowest-information, and any that have ceased to track real-world CHRO work. Every change ships with a release note in the public repo.

  • Added per quarter 50
  • Retired per quarter 50
  • Net total (steady state) 250
  • Next refresh 21 Oct 2026
§ IV · How to contribute, audit, or benchmark

The eval set is a public artifact. Use it.

  • To suggest a question: email evalset@stratum.ai with the question and the methodology you'd want us to use. We respond within five business days.
  • To run your own questions against Stratum, open the Console and ask anything. Sessions are sandboxed; nothing you ask becomes training data.
  • To benchmark another tool against this set, download the JSON via evalset@stratum.ai (CC BY-SA 4.0). The held-out subset stays in escrow with Rhino Security Labs.
  • To publish your own custom eval co-authored with our applied AI team, ask your account team about the Partner co-publishing programme.

"The eval set is the company's most credible asset. We will publish it. We will be auditable on it."
