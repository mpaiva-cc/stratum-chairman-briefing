§ I  ·  Executive Summary

Six weeks in, the company is on plan, with three exceptions.

¶1.01
Operating state. Stratum has six employees on the cap table — four active (the three founders and Asha Pillai, who joined 02 June) and two who have accepted offers and not yet started (Tomás Iglesias, design, starts 23 June; Sofia Vargas, customer engineering, starts 30 June). Cash on hand is $7.13M against a $8.0M seed closed 12 May, yielding twenty months of runway at our current operating plan. We have signed five design partners against an internal target of five; we have shipped two of twelve planned connectors to alpha state; and we have zero production customers, which is consistent with the operating plan we shared at the founding round.
¶1.02
Material variance from plan. Three items warrant the Board's attention before this body convenes on Wednesday. First, the connector roadmap is tracking nine days behind the alpha cadence committed in the founding plan; D. Okonkwo's view is that the slippage is recoverable inside the current quarter and does not threaten the Q4 design-partner go-live commitments. Second, the Halcyon Health partnership — our second-signed design partner and the one with the highest near-term revenue gravity — has downgraded from production-track to a sixty-day evaluation following an internal sponsor transition; this is documented in Briefing No. 003 and addressed at § V below. Third, monthly burn is running approximately $20K above plan, driven by a recruiter retainer initiated 19 May and an unbudgeted customer-engineering hire (S. Vargas) approved by the Chairman on 09 June under the case-by-case delegation; both variances are explained and contained.
¶1.03
Asks of the Board. Three matters are submitted for resolution at the meeting on 24 June and are set out in full at § VI: (i) authorization to enter a 90-day usage-based pricing pilot with one design partner; (ii) a $200K out-of-budget appropriation for a third-party security audit ahead of the Halcyon decision window; and (iii) approval of the Compensation Committee's first action — a 0.2% top-up grant to the founding designer in recognition of pre-start contributions. Three further items at § VII are submitted for discussion without vote.

Eight risks carry forward; one downgrade, one new addition.

¶4.01

R3 ↓
Enterprise sales cycle drag. Downgraded from Med/High to Med/Med. Rationale: the Bhusri-network compression of two of our five design partners (Northwind and Cordova) signed in <14 days each suggests the network premium is materially larger than we underwrote at founding. R10 ✦
Partnership-label inflation. NEW. Med/Med. Owner: P. Rao. The Halcyon transition exposed a risk we under-weighted: when an early-stage company signs a logo whose sponsor is a known buyer, the partnership label compresses two distinct things — (a) the sponsor's personal interest, and (b) the buying organization's institutional commitment. The first does not survive an org change; the second does. Mitigation in flight: every new design partner must produce a multi-party stakeholder map (sponsor, economic buyer, operating owner, IT) before contract signature, with at least three signatures across that map. This is being added to the contracting checklist effective 22 June.

Risks that remain unchanged but warrant Board attention. R1 (correctness of the underlying graph at the payroll-grade threshold) and R2 (model-vendor concentration on the agent stack) remain the two highest-severity items on the register; both are being actively engineered against and neither is the subject of a resolution in this memo. Resolution 1 · Usage-Based Pricing Pilot Vote required

Authorization to enter a 90-day usage-based pricing pilot with one design partner.

Proposed motion Resolved, that the CEO is authorized to enter into a single, time-bound 90-day pricing pilot with one design partner of the Company's choosing under a usage-based fee structure with no platform floor, on the express understanding that the pilot is one-time, non-recurring, terminates automatically at 90 days, and that the data produced thereunder will inform the Company's pricing model v1 to be presented to the Board at its September meeting.

Rationale. The hybrid pricing model ratified at D-019 includes a usage component whose unit economics are unknown. A 90-day pilot — single-partner, no floor, automatic sunset — gives us a real-world signal at low contractual risk before we publish a pricing page in September. P. Rao recommends Cordova as the pilot partner: their CHRO (Whitfield) has indicated openness, the workload is well-instrumented, and the contract length aligns with our model-v1 timeline.

Expected impact. Zero revenue impact in the pilot window (design-partner contracts are at zero-platform regardless). Material learning impact on the September pricing-model presentation. Small contractual complexity (one bespoke amendment); P. Rao has the template drafted.

If approved: P. Rao proceeds with Cordova; pilot live by 15 July; readout at the September Board meeting. If declined: we present a pricing model v1 in September built entirely on internal modeling, with materially wider confidence intervals.

Resolution 2 · Out-of-Budget Security Audit Vote required

Authorization for up to $200,000 in out-of-budget spend on a third-party security audit and penetration test ahead of the Halcyon decision window.

Proposed motion Resolved, that the CEO is authorized to expend up to two hundred thousand dollars ($200,000) outside the approved FY26 operating budget on a third-party security audit and external penetration test, to be commissioned from a firm selected at the CEO's discretion from a list of three reviewed by counsel, with the audit to be substantially complete prior to Halcyon Health's evaluation decision on 31 August 2026.

Rationale. The Halcyon evaluation will, by Marsh's account, centrally turn on the security posture of a vendor handling employee data for 4,400 healthcare workers. SOC 2 Type II will not be attested until March 2027; we cannot accelerate it. A third-party penetration test plus a written security audit from a recognized firm (we are evaluating Bishop Fox, NCC Group, and Trail of Bits) is the next-best instrument. It also benefits the broader pipeline.

Expected impact. Cash impact: $200K one-time in Q3, against $7.13M cash on hand. Runway impact: approximately twenty days. Halcyon impact: not determinative but materially positive. Pipeline impact: the audit attestation can be referenced in every late-stage enterprise conversation through the SOC 2 Type II attestation in March 2027.

If approved: firm selection by 01 July; engagement starts 15 July; report by 25 August. If declined: we attempt to satisfy Halcyon's security requirement using internal documentation plus the SOC 2 Type I report (available August); we judge this materially less likely to clear the bar.

§ VII  ·  Items for Board Discussion  ·  No Vote Required

Three items raised for the Board's consideration and counsel.

¶7.01
The following items are not submitted for resolution but the founders would value the Board's view on each before the next quarterly meeting.
  1. Ratification of the Founder Compact as a formal corporate document. The Compact is the founders' six-clause working agreement signed on 11 May; its provisions cover decision-rights, dispute escalation, equity rebalancing on departure, and the cadence of difficult conversations. It has functioned well for six weeks. The question for the Board: should the Compact be entered as a Board-acknowledged document, or remain a private founder instrument? S. Cannon's advice is specifically invited; her view, informally, has been that ratification adds weight without cost. M. Chen is open; D. Okonkwo and P. Rao are open with a preference for retaining the right to amend without Board approval.
  2. S. Cannon's request to attend the M6 retrospective in person. The founders run a structured retrospective every six months (the first falls in November). Cannon has asked to attend in person. The founders are open. The question is whether the Board considers this a precedent that should be governed by an explicit policy (e.g. one investor observer per retro), or whether case-by-case judgment is sufficient.
  3. The Chairman's role in approving non-budgeted hires. Under the case-by-case delegation in place since 11 May, the Chairman approved the Vargas hire (D-038) on 09 June, twenty-one days before her start date. The mechanism worked: it was fast, it was informed, and it would have been awkward to take to the full Board for a single hire. The question is whether to formalize this as a standing delegation (Chairman may approve off-budget hires up to a defined cash-impact ceiling, with Board ratification at the next meeting) or maintain ad-hoc judgment. M. Chen's view: a standing delegation with a $30K/mo cash-impact ceiling and ratification-at-next-meeting would be the cleanest formalization.
