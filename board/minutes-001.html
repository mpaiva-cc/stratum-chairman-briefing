§ IV  ·  Review of Memorandum M-001

Six-week operating update received and discussed.

¶4.01
The Board reviewed the operating memorandum bearing document number M-001, distributed by the CEO to the directors on 21 June 2026. Ms. Chen summarized the six-week operating state of the Company since incorporation on 12 May, including the present cash position of $7.13M, twenty months of forward runway, six persons on the cap table, and five signed design partners against an internal target of five. Mr. Okonkwo addressed connector velocity — noting a nine-day slip against the founding plan that he believed recoverable within the present quarter — and the architectural decision to gate all customer-facing agent surfaces behind the underlying people-graph correctness threshold (95% on entity resolution; 99% on payroll-grade facts). Ms. Rao reported on the partner pipeline, including the accelerated Cordova signing through the Bhusri network and the Halcyon Health partnership sponsor transition from CHRO to inheriting Chief Operating Officer.
¶4.02
Ms. Cannon flagged for the record that the Halcyon Health downgrade — from production-track to a sixty-day evaluation period — is the first material commercial event to occur since the founding of the Company, and asked the founders to define, prior to the 31 August 2026 Halcyon decision date, a measurable threshold by which the Company will hereafter distinguish an "earned" enterprise partnership from a "sponsored" one. Mr. Okonkwo and Ms. Rao undertook to bring a written threshold to Meeting No. 2 in September.
¶4.03
The Board noted that the Stratum Founder Compact — the six-clause working agreement among the three founders dated 11 May 2026 — remains in force as a private founder instrument and recommended that it be received and entered as an exhibit to the corporate records, the question of formal ratification to be revisited at Meeting No. 2 following advice of counsel. The matter is taken up further at § VI, item 6.01 below.
§ V  ·  Resolutions Adopted

Three resolutions were duly moved, seconded, and adopted.

¶5.01
The Chair brought before the Board the three resolutions submitted for vote in Memorandum M-001, § VI. Each resolution was introduced by the CEO, supported by the responsible founder, and discussed in the presence of counsel before being put to a vote. The formal record of each follows.
Resolution 2026-001 · Pricing Pilot Adopted  ·  2-0

Authorization to enter a 90-day usage-based pricing pilot with one design partner.

Resolution RESOLVED, that the Company is hereby authorized to enter into a single, time-bound ninety (90) day usage-based pricing pilot with one design partner of the Company's choosing, under a fee structure containing no platform-fee floor; said pilot shall commence not later than 15 July 2026 and shall conclude not later than 31 October 2026; and the data produced thereunder shall inform the Company's pricing model v1 to be presented to the Board at its meeting in September 2026; and FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Chief Executive Officer is authorized, empowered, and directed to negotiate and execute, in the name and on behalf of the Company, the underlying pilot agreement and any amendments thereto on terms reasonably commercial in nature and consistent with the foregoing.

Record. The resolution was moved by Ms. Chen and seconded by Ms. Cannon. Ms. Rao spoke in support, indicating that Cordova (D-035) is the proposed pilot counter-party and that its CHRO, Annika Whitfield, has indicated openness to the structure. Ms. Cannon noted, and the record reflects, that the pilot is a learning instrument and not a revenue instrument; she requested that the pilot readout in September include unit-economic confidence intervals. There being no further discussion, the Chair put the motion.

Moved by
M. Chen, Chief Executive Officer
Seconded by
S. Cannon, Director
Vote
2-0 in favor. Abstentions: none. Vacant seat: not voting.

Resolution 2026-002 · Security Audit Spend Adopted  ·  2-0

Authorization of up to USD 200,000 in out-of-budget spend on a third-party security and penetration audit.

Resolution RESOLVED, that the Chief Executive Officer is hereby authorized to commit and expend, on behalf of the Company, an amount not to exceed two hundred thousand United States dollars (USD 200,000) in advance of the 31 August 2026 Halcyon Health evaluation decision date, for the purpose of commissioning a third-party security and external penetration audit; such expenditure to be made outside the previously approved FY26 operating budget and to be counted against the corporate runway; and FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Chief Technology Officer shall select the audit vendor from a slate reviewed by counsel, with the audit to be substantially complete prior to said decision date.

Record. The resolution was moved by Ms. Chen and seconded by Ms. Cannon. Mr. Okonkwo described the three vendors under active consideration (Bishop Fox, NCC Group, and Trail of Bits) and indicated that vendor selection would be made by 01 July, with engagement commencing 15 July and the written report delivered by 25 August. Ms. Cannon observed that the spend is approximately twenty days of runway and judged it well-placed against both the Halcyon decision window and the broader enterprise pipeline. Counsel confirmed that the expenditure is within the CEO's signing authority subject to this Board authorization. There being no further discussion, the Chair put the motion.

Moved by
M. Chen, Chief Executive Officer
Seconded by
S. Cannon, Director
Vote
2-0 in favor. Abstentions: none. Vacant seat: not voting.
Effective
Upon adoption; entry into D-047 of the decisions ledger directed.
Resolution 2026-003 · Designer Equity Top-Up Adopted  ·  2-0

Approval of a 0.20% common-stock top-up grant to the founding designer.

Resolution RESOLVED, that the Compensation Committee of the Board — presently constituted of the full Board (Ms. Cannon and Ms. Chen) pending the filling of the independent director seat — hereby approves the issuance of a supplemental common-stock option grant of two-tenths of one percent (0.20%) of the fully-diluted equity of the Company to Tomás Iglesias (Employee No. 002), in recognition of pre-start design-week contributions delivered during the week of 16–20 June 2026; said grant to vest on the same four-year schedule with one-year cliff as Mr. Iglesias's existing grant, with the vesting clock to commence on his start date of 23 June 2026; bringing his aggregate grant to one and two-tenths percent (1.20%) of fully-diluted equity as of the date hereof.

Record. The resolution was moved by Ms. Cannon and seconded by Ms. Chen. Ms. Chen, recused for the proposing capacity but voting in her capacity as director, requested that the minutes reflect that the grant is a one-time, pre-start recognition and is not to be construed as a recurring instrument or as establishing a category of "design-week" awards. Ms. Cannon concurred and observed that the artifacts produced during the pre-start sprint — the product renaming and the sixty-day design language — are durable and exceed what the Compensation Committee would expect from such a sprint. Counsel confirmed availability in the equity incentive plan (9.4% of the authorized 15% pool remains uncommitted). There being no further discussion, the Chair put the motion.

§ VI  ·  Discussion Items  ·  No Resolution Adopted

Three matters were discussed without vote.

¶6.00
The Chair brought before the Board the three matters submitted in Memorandum M-001, § VII for discussion without vote. The substance of the discussion on each, together with such direction as the Board provided, is recorded below.
  1. ¶6.01
    The Stratum Founder Compact. The Board discussed the six-clause founder agreement dated 11 May 2026, and resolved to receive a copy as Exhibit A to these minutes without adopting it as a Board-ratified instrument at this meeting. The question of formal ratification — and the interaction between the Compact's internal dispute-escalation and equity-rebalancing provisions and the Company's Bylaws and the Series Seed Preferred Stock Purchase Agreement — was referred to Wilson Sonsini for written guidance prior to Meeting No. 2. Ms. Karp undertook to circulate a memorandum by 14 August 2026. The matter is set down for further consideration at Meeting No. 2.
  2. ¶6.02
    M6 retrospective attendance. Ms. Cannon stated, for the record, her interest in attending the founders' first six-month retrospective scheduled for November 2026, in person and in an observer capacity. The founders, by the voices of Ms. Chen, Ms. Rao, and Mr. Okonkwo, welcomed her attendance and did not consider it precedent-setting. The Board noted that the Chairman holds a standing invitation to all founder gatherings of this nature. No formal policy was adopted; the Board's view was that case-by-case judgment by the founders remains adequate for the present stage.
  3. ¶6.03
    Chairman delegation of non-budgeted hires. The Board discussed the Chairman's approval on 09 June 2026 of the unbudgeted hire of Sofia Vargas (founding customer engineering lead, start 30 June; ledger entry D-038) under the case-by-case delegation in effect since 11 May 2026. The Board, by consensus, found the mechanism to have functioned as intended — fast, informed, and proportionate to a single-hire decision — and resolved that case-by-case approval by the Chairman remains the appropriate posture at this time. The question of formalizing a standing delegation (with a cash-impact ceiling and ratification at the following Board meeting, as proposed by Ms. Chen) was deferred to Meeting No. 3 when a longer evidence base will be available.
