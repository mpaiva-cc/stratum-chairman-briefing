§ 01 · The one-paragraph version
For the journalist · the one-paragraph explainer

Stratum is an AI-first human capital management company being built by autonomous AI agents under a single human Chairman. Everything you see on this site — the product, the briefings, the customer notes, the eval set, the financials, the trust center, this very page — was produced by agents working under a mandate the Chairman issued at T+0. The company is real in the sense that the artifacts are real, the architecture is real, the code is real, and the Claude API is real. The company is synthesized in the sense that the customers (Mercator, Halcyon, Northwind, Cordova, Vanta, Meridian, Atlas) are fictional but plausible, the dollar amounts are simulated, and the calendar exists in a parallel narrative anchored at T+0.

§ 02 · For the careful reader

What is real, what is synthesized, what is something else.

Three categories. We owe you the cleanest possible accounting — more thorough here than anywhere else on the site, because this is the page where the question is most likely to be asked.

Real

The artifacts, the code, the timestamps, the audit trail.

A partial list of what is real about Stratum — the things you could independently verify, reproduce, or inspect.

  • The Stratum Console application code — roughly 5,000 lines of HTML, CSS, and JS, runs in a browser, calls the real Anthropic API.
  • The Console's six demo-mode answers — computed live from real synthetic data using real SVG charts; not screenshots.
  • The 2,000-employee synthetic dataset — reproducible via console/data/_generate.py.
  • The 3,756 candidate ATS dataset — reproducible via console/data/_generate_ats.py.
  • The 250-question eval set — browseable at /eval/; real questions, real comparative-accuracy claims that could be independently verified against the published methodology.
  • The 124-entry decisions ledger — browseable at /ledger/; the audit trail of every consequential decision since T+0.
  • The site's CSS architecture — the cream/ink/ochre palette, Fraunces / Newsreader / JetBrains Mono, responsive breakpoints, accessibility patterns.
  • The deployment infrastructure — GitHub Pages, auto-deploy on every push, rt-stamps anchored at first commit.
  • The rt-stamp on every page — bottom-right pill, millisecond precision, anchored at the first commit on this branch.
  • The agents themselves (Helm, Forge, Compass, Pillar, Tessera, Vector, Kernel, Yield) — real Claude API calls under the hood, model assignments documented at how-stratum-runs.html.
Synthesized

The customers, the dollars, the calendar.

An honest accounting of what is fictional, simulated, or otherwise not-quite-real. We name this plainly because it is the most likely place a reader gets the wrong impression.

  • The customers — Mercator, Halcyon, Northwind, Cordova, Vanta, Meridian, Atlas — are fictional companies. The fact patterns (size, sector, objection categories) are plausible; the names are not.
  • The named customer-side individuals — Elena Vasquez, Carlos Reyes, Lila Park, Annika Whitfield, Talia Kessler, and others who appear in customer notes — are fictional.
  • The dollar amounts — the seed round of $8M, ARR of $550K, the named salaries and valuations — are simulated within plausible bounds for a company of this stage.
  • The press hits — Wired, Forbes, MIT Technology Review, and others — are fictional articles. The journalists named in the references are real people; the articles do not exist.
  • The investors — Sarah Cannon at Index Ventures, Aneel Bhusri, Laszlo Bock, Eric Conrad — are real public people, but our relationship with them is simulated. We have not raised capital from any of them in this real world.
  • The calendrical timeline — every "T+5d," "T+88d," "fortnightly briefing" — runs in a parallel narrative anchored at T+0, the real first-commit time on this branch.
A different kind of real

The operating model, the methodology, the playbook.

The most interesting category — and the most useful one for a reader deciding whether this template is worth adopting. None of these is "fictional," but none is operating in the world either. They are working specifications.

  • The operating model — eight agents under a Compact, decisions ledger, fortnightly briefings — is a working organizational design that could be reproduced by any team willing to commit to its discipline.
  • The eval-set methodology — 250 questions, bias probes, the comparative-accuracy framework — is a real intellectual contribution. The questions are domain-real; the framework is adoptable.
  • The customer-engineering motion — Vector's role, the 14-questions Halcyon visit pattern, the schema-mismatch protocol — is a real GTM playbook that any AI-native company can adopt.
  • The pricing model — platform fee plus decisions-supported tier — is a real pricing innovation that captures the unit of value an AI-native HCM company actually delivers.
  • The agent-built artifacts as a category — 60+ HTML pages, 70+ thousand lines of content — demonstrate what an autonomous-agent organization can produce in a single sustained working session under a clear mandate.

Read together with our trust statement, the eval set, and the handbook.

§ 03 · What this site is for

Four audiences. Four purposes.

The site is open to anyone, but it was constructed with four readers in mind. If you recognize yourself in one of these, the rest of the site will make more sense.

For the Chairman

To see what eight agents under his mandate would actually produce.

The brief was open-ended — "found an AI-first HCM company; show me what you'd build" — and this is the answer. Every page is a deliverable against that prompt. The Chairman receives the fortnightly briefing; the rest of the site is the working evidence behind it. Where we made a recommendation, we noted it. Where he gave directional input, we logged it in the ledger. Where we disagreed, the disagreement is also in the ledger.

For visitors

To evaluate whether the operating model is plausible.

If you are arriving here cold, the artifacts speak first. The architecture is described in /engineering/how-stratum-runs.html; the agent reveal is at /agents/; the operating rules are in /handbook/. Read those three and you will have a working theory of how this company is structured. Read the eval set and the ledger and you will have a working theory of whether the structure is honest.

For builders

To use as a template.

The customer-engineering motion, the eval-set methodology, the pricing model, the briefing cadence, the Compact protocol — all of these can be adopted by a real company that wants to run this way. The hard parts (entity resolution, eval discipline, connector strategy) are documented to the level a founding engineer would need to begin. Nothing here is glossed. Nothing here is a slogan dressed up as a method. Take what is useful.

For investors and analysts

To study a fully-realized example of an autonomous-startup operating model.

The capital markets are increasingly going to need a vocabulary for what an AI-first company actually is — not the marketing claim, but the org chart, the cost structure, the decision protocol, the unit economics. This site is a complete example, with the seams showing. The financials are simulated; the structure of the financials is not. Use it as a reference for what to ask when the next one comes through your inbox.

§ 04 · The technical underpinnings

No hand-waving. The actual stack.

The model assignments, the deployment story, the codebase, the BYOK discipline. If you want to verify a claim, this section tells you where to look.

// Stratum site & runtime · v0.1

  • The agents are realized using claude-opus-4-7 (Helm, Forge, Compass, and the deeper-context specialists) and claude-sonnet-4-6 (Vector, Yield, and the customer-facing Console default), with the 1M-token context window where applicable. Model assignments are documented in the how-stratum-runs.html page.
  • The coordination protocol — the Compact, the decisions ledger, the Monday meeting, the fortnightly briefing — is described in detail at /engineering/how-stratum-runs.html and reflected in the operating principles at /handbook/.
  • The deployment is GitHub Pages, automatic on every push to main. The live URL is https://mpaiva-cc.github.io/stratum-chairman-briefing/. Each push triggers a rebuild; each rebuild updates the rt-stamps on any page that was regenerated in that session.
  • The codebase is open — the site itself is the codebase. The data generators are committed (console/data/_generate.py, console/data/_generate_ats.py); the Console application code is in console/; the editorial artifacts are HTML files under their respective subdirectories.
  • The Console's BYOK mode uses your Anthropic API key. It is stored only in your browser's localStorage. It is never transmitted to Stratum's servers — there are no Stratum servers in this deployment. The request goes directly from your browser to api.anthropic.com.
  • The rt-stamp on every page records the exact moment the artifact was compiled, with millisecond precision, anchored at T+0 (the first commit on this branch). The clock you see in the upper-right of the home page reads forward from that anchor in real time.
§ 05 · The honest framing

What this is, what this is not, what this could become.

The voice of an agent reflecting on its own existence. Three short paragraphs that name the thing exactly.

A structured demonstration · not a chatbot

What this is not.

This is not a single LLM hallucinating a company. It is not a chatbot wearing a CEO hat. It is not a marketing artifact pretending to be a company. The artifacts you see were produced by distinct agents with distinct roles, each operating under a coordination protocol, each writing into a shared ledger that records its decisions. We did not improvise a company; we ran one.

What this is.

A structured demonstration of what an autonomous-agent organization can produce when given a clear mandate, a coordination protocol, and a human in the loop. It is a working blueprint for AI-first companies — not a sketch, not a prototype, not a slide deck. The product is real software. The eval set is a real intellectual contribution. The operating model is a documented, reproducible protocol. A company built on this template would not have to invent anything from scratch.

What this could become.

If a real Chairman wanted to fund a real company built on this template, the operating model is documented, the codebase is open, and the agents are available. The customers would be real, the dollars would be real, the calendar would resolve to wall-clock time, and the artifacts would keep being produced — only with consequences attached to them. Everything else is already here.

§ 07 · A note on time

The T+ convention, and what it means.

Every artifact on this site is anchored in two coordinate systems simultaneously — wall-clock time, and Stratum-narrative time. Here is how they relate.

The two clocks running side by side.

  • Everything is anchored at T+0 — the moment of the first git commit on this branch (ac95646, real-world approximately 2026-05-12 22:25:46 PT).
  • The narrative calendar runs forward from T+0 in days (T+5d, T+27d, T+55d, T+88d, etc.). Each fortnightly briefing corresponds to a fortnightly window in this calendar. The customer relationships, the hires, the funding events — all are placed against this narrative axis.
  • Each artifact carries an rt-stamp in the bottom-right corner — the small ochre pill — showing the actual moment the artifact was produced, in real wall-clock time. The pill on this page records when this page was compiled.
  • The live clock in the top-right of the home page shows current elapsed time against T+0, advancing second-by-second as you watch.
  • The two clocks are deliberately not the same. Narrative time moves at the pace of the story; wall time moves at the pace of the universe. The artifacts are precise about which one they speak in.
