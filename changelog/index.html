Stratum/Changelog the honest log of what we shipped.

The honest log of what we shipped, by version, by week. Eleven internal builds across three codename groups — Foundation, Loam, Marble — covering the people-graph schema, three connectors, and the design language. We write these for design partners, for our chairman, and for ourselves.

v0.4.2
Marble
Sun, T+53d
build · 4f2a91c · main
Shipped by Forge (eng)
A. Pillai (eng)
T. Iglesias (design)

A small release on paper, a large one in practice. ADP-Workforce Now alpha was originally scheduled for today; Pillar's audit of the contractor identity model caught three classes of error that would have produced span-of-control numbers wrong by twelve percent at our largest partner. We slipped ADP-WFN to T+67d and shipped schema v2 instead. Design system v0.2 also landed, with the console rename ratified from internal vocabulary to component library.

Changed
  • schema people-graph schema v2 ratified — contractor, temp-to-perm, multi-employer, and FTE-with-second-1099 cases now have first-class node types instead of overloaded employment.kind enums
  • schema span_of_control() recomputes against new edges; backfill complete on Mercator (3,412 people, 22 minutes) and Northshore sandbox (4,400 people, 38 minutes)
  • console internal rename from "copilot" to "console" applied across component library, route names, and telemetry events; customer-facing string remains "copilot" through public beta
  • eval raised entity-resolution acceptance threshold from τ=0.91 to τ=0.93 after schema-v2 regression caught two false-positive merges that the old threshold would have committed
Added
  • design console.v0.2 ships — Ask · Inspect · Cite · Confirm panes, three font sizes, two density modes; Figma library mirrored to @stratum/ui at parity
  • design color token set frozen at 24 tokens; ochre is reserved for system, not status — status uses moss/plum/ink-3 to avoid the "everything is urgent" failure mode
  • graph contractor-to-employee transition handling; converting a contractor node to an employee node now preserves edges and tenure rather than creating a duplicate entity
  • cite every agent answer now carries an inline cite[] block with source system, row count, and confidence; rendered as a footnote rail in the console
Fixed
  • graph resolved edge case where dual-citizenship + multi-payroll caused identity duplication on the Mercator sandbox; was producing 4 of 22 false conflicts
  • infra ClickHouse materialised view mv_org_rollup was stale by up to 9 minutes under burst writes; refresh interval now event-driven from Temporal, p95 lag down to 22 s
  • workday rate-limiter mis-counted retries against Workday's bucket and tripped soft-bans every 14 hours; corrected and added jitter
Known
  • adp-wfn connector alpha slipped from T+53d to T+67d; tracked under STR-114
  • adp-wfn currently misses regional sub-tenancies for partners with payroll in >1 country; design work blocked on Mercator's Singapore filing decision
  • eval eval set still at 40/250 questions; expansion planned in v0.4.4 once Tessera's grading rubric is finalised
Contributors Pillar (eng · schema v2 lead) · Forge (eng · review) · Tessera (design · console v0.2)

v0.3.2
Loam
Fri, T+16d
build · c81f4d2 · main
Shipped by Forge (eng)

The first ingestion of real customer data. Mercator Health's sandbox extract — 3,412 employees, 8 source systems, 14 months of history — moved into our people graph in 42 minutes. Twenty-two identity conflicts surfaced for human review. None of them were surprises; all of them were diagnosable.

Added
  • ingest first end-to-end ingestion from a design partner's sandbox — Mercator, 3,412 EE, 22 identity conflicts surfaced
  • graph human-review queue UI scaffold (CLI-only for now) — operator can accept, reject, or split a proposed merge with one keystroke
  • audit every resolution decision is written to an append-only resolution_ledger with the rule, the score, the human reviewer (if any), and the timestamp
Changed
  • graph resolution conflicts are now first-class objects, not log lines; queryable from the CLI
Known
  • graph Mercator has 31 employees with dual citizenship and a regional payroll; 4 of the 22 conflicts trace to this — patched in v0.3.3
  • infra the 42-minute runtime is single-threaded; we will not parallelise until we have the schema right

v0.3.1
Loam
Tue, T+13d
build · 3d9a5e1 · main
Shipped by Forge (eng)

Entity resolution v0.3 — probabilistic blocking + a graph-confirmation pass at τ=0.93. The model is doing in two passes what the first cut did in five, and producing fewer false merges. We have a thing that resembles a moat.

Added
  • graph entity resolution v0.3 — probabilistic blocking (24 features) + graph-confirmation pass; accepts at τ=0.93, rejects below τ=0.55, queues the middle for human review
  • graph 24 features include name n-grams, email locality, payroll number prefix, manager-chain proximity, badge ID, and tenure-band overlap
  • graph graph-confirmation pass: if A and B both share ≥2 high-confidence neighbours, the merge proposal score is multiplied by 1.18
Changed
  • graph resolver no longer attempts to merge cross-tenancy; tenancy is now an outer boundary, not a feature
  • graph default block size raised from 64 to 256 candidates after measuring p95 recall on the test fixtures
Fixed
  • graph Unicode normalisation: O'Brien and O'Brien (curly vs straight apostrophe) now match; recovers ~0.8% of merges on Mercator fixture
Contributors Forge (eng · sole)
v0.3.0
Loam
Thu, T+8d
build · 1f0c2d6 · main
Shipped by Forge (eng)
Helm (product)

The people-graph schema v1 is ratified. Nine node types, fourteen edge types, one decision rule: anything that can be inferred is an edge, not a column. Codename group rotates from Foundation to Loam — the schema-and-resolution layer.

Added
  • schema people-graph.v1 — 9 node types: Person, Employment, Position, Org, Skill, Compensation, Performance, Event, Tenancy
  • schema 14 edge types: holds, reports_to, member_of, has_skill, compensated_by, graded, resulted_in, and seven more
  • schema design rule documented in /docs/schema/v1.md: if a fact can be derived from edges and a function, it does not get a column
  • infra initial Postgres + pgvector + Apache AGE setup; ClickHouse for rollups, Temporal for everything that crosses a boundary
Changed
  • arch codename group changes from Foundation to Loam — the layer we will scrape, turn, and plant in
Known
  • schema v1 assumes contractors are a flavour of Employment; this proves wrong in v0.4.2 and is refactored to a first-class node type
  • schema no event sourcing yet — events are recorded but not replayable; planned for Quill
Contributors Forge (eng · schema) · Helm (product · review)

Codename glossary · six groups, three shipped

We ship codename groups, not individual point releases. A codename travels with a layer of the system until that layer is judged stable enough to build on. Foundation and Loam are closed; Marble is in flight.

Codename Versions Scope Status
Foundation v0.1.x – v0.2.x Repository, infrastructure, first connector scaffolding. Postgres / ClickHouse / Temporal stack. Compliance posture engaged with Vanta. The place the product lives. Closed · May 2026
Loam v0.3.x The schema and entity-resolution layer. People-graph schema v1, probabilistic + graph-confirmation resolution at τ=0.93, first Workday alpha, first design-partner ingestion. The layer we scrape, turn, and plant in. Closed · T+39d
Marble v0.4.x First design language and the second wave of connectors. Console renamed, design system v0.2, Workday beta, Greenhouse alpha, ADP-WFN slipping to July. The surface the user touches. In flight · since T+41d
Quill v0.5.x The agent surface, fully wired. Console ships to design partners on real data; eval set grows to 250 questions; cited-answer coverage becomes a SLA. Planned next. Q3 2026 · planned
Compass v0.6.x First write-back workflows. Comp Review module against Workday and ADP. Countersign and revert primitives. Where intelligence earns the right to change a row. Q4 2026 · planned
Beacon v1.0 Public general availability. SOC 2 Type II clean, EU AI Act class assessment filed, ten production customers, NPS measured. The first time we stop calling it private alpha. Q1 2027 · planned
