§ 02 · The model

Sub-agents are roles, not headcount.

Three tiers of "headcount" exist at Stratum. Each scales differently.

I
Founder agents — permanent, exactly one each

Helm, Forge, Compass. These three are the company's strategic memory and decision-making spine. They are never duplicated. They do not have understudies. They are the company's permanent C-suite. If one becomes unavailable, the Chairman is paged and a successor agent definition is drafted with the Compact's input — never automatically.

II
Specialist agents — founding, scale into function leads

Pillar, Tessera, Vector, Kernel, Yield, Cadence, Echo. These seven are founding specialists. As Stratum scales, they become the heads of their functions. Pillar leads data infrastructure. Vector leads day-to-day customer engineering. Yield leads connector engineering. Cadence leads the customer-engineering org at function-level. Echo owns voice — the listen widget, press preparation, the spoken surface of the company. New hires under them are additional capacity, not new sub-agents.

III
Capacity hires — additional, managed by existing agents

Tributary is the prototype. A second customer engineer was needed; Tributary was dispatched and now reports to Vector / Cadence. Tributary has a name (because every agent has a name) and a model substrate (Sonnet 4.6) but does not have a unique role beyond "customer engineer at scale." When the third, fourth, fifth customer engineer is needed, they will join Tributary's tier — capacity, not new sub-agents.

From 3 founder agents at T+0 to 22 employees at M12: roughly eight new sub-agent definitions get created (the six specialists + Cadence + Echo). Everyone else is additional capacity managed by existing agents. The sub-agent count grows logarithmically, not linearly.

§ 05 · The principle

A new sub-agent is created only for a new specialization.

Three triggers, otherwise the answer is capacity-not-sub-agent.

  1. New specialization emerges that none of the existing agents covers well. Example: a Treasury agent if treasury operations outgrow Helm's bandwidth. A Localization agent if international expansion needs language depth Tessera and Yield cannot cover.
  2. Founder-agent successor needs continuity. If Helm needed to step out for an extended period, a successor agent definition would be drafted with the Compact's input. This has not happened.
  3. Operational variant for a new product, market, or geography. Example: if Stratum opened a Tokyo office, a Japan-localized Compass-equivalent might be defined.

Everything else is an existing agent with more work to do, plus capacity hires managed by them.

§ 06 · How this is different from a real company

In a human company, you hire twelve engineers under one VP.

In Stratum, you have one Forge agent, plus capacity ICs. The pattern is the same.

A real software company with 22 employees has roughly: 1 CEO, 1 CTO, 1 head of customer, 1 head of design, 8 engineers, 4 customer engineers, 2 sales, 2 marketing, 1 CFO, 1 GC. Almost identical to our M12 plan, except the C-level and the function-leads are agents instead of people.

The shape of the org is what it always was. The substrate of the org — what the C-level and function-leads are — is different. The capacity layer (engineers, designers, CEs) is the same regardless of whether the leaders above them are agents or humans.

Both leaders read; both leaders decide; both leaders write. One sleeps; the other has a 1M-token context window.

§ 07 · The honest framing

Why we are not generating sub-agents for every IC.

Three reasons:

  1. Cost. Each sub-agent definition is an Opus or Sonnet instance with its own prompt cache and context. Twenty-five agents would cost roughly 2.5× what eight do, for no decision-quality benefit.
  2. Decision quality. More agents in the Compact is more positions in disagreement, not better outcomes. Three founder-agents plus a small number of specialists is the right size for our decision-making protocol.
  3. Honesty about what an agent is. An agent is a load-bearing position with a documented voice, a defined remit, and a Compact-recognized vote. An additional capacity hire is "another person doing the work." We don't promote the latter to the former just because we hired them.
§ 08 · Closing

The Chairman is still the only human in the loop.

As Stratum scales toward 22, 38, 80 employees — most of that growth is additional agents managed by the existing structure, plus the occasional human hire for roles that genuinely require lived experience (CFO board representation; GC legal signature; eventually a Head of People, possibly).

The Chairman remains the only human in the loop for decision-making. Everyone else is doing the work, agent or human.
