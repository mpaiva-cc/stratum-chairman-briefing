§ 03 Past · The record so far

What we have done, and what we learned.

Three appearances since the company was incorporated. In reverse chronology, with the unvarnished version of what worked and what did not. We do not believe in pretending an event went better than it did.

T+85d2026

The Information — feature interview

Helm interview · published T+85d · The Information

A long-form Helm interview built around the public eval set. Reporter: Erin Woo. The piece ran 3,200 words and reproduced six of the eval-set questions verbatim alongside the Stratum and Workday answers. It did not generate substantive customer inbound in the first 48 hours — but it generated eight Series A informational requests from investors in the same window, which is a useful signal about who reads The Information first.

What we learned The Information piece reaches investors faster than buyers. Useful for fundraising, slower for pipeline. Worth repeating next cycle with a deliberately buyer-facing outlet (HR Executive, Talent Daily) for the customer story.

T+43d2026

The Stratum / Founders Dinner #1 — Palo Alto

Sundance Steakhouse · 6 CHROs · NDA · Helm, Compass, Forge

The first Stratum-hosted dinner. Helm, Compass, and Forge hosted six CHROs at the Sundance Steakhouse in Palo Alto. The theme: "The eval set, before publication." All six attendees agreed under NDA to give feedback on the 102-question pre-publication eval set. The conversation ran from 7:00pm to 11:40pm and the wine list was honest. Two of the six attendees are now Stratum customers.

What we learned Six is the right number. Four feels like a sales meeting; eight feels like a focus group. The conversion ratio (2 of 6) was higher than any cohort we have run any other way. We will do this again every quarter, hosted by us, and one a quarter hosted by someone with more standing than us.

§ 04 Our editorial policy on events

What we will, and won't, do.

A short editorial section. We turn down most speaking requests not because we are precious about it, but because most requests are not the talk we would give. The list below is the test we run.

What we will speak on

Yes — on specific topics, with evidence.

  • The architecture argument. Graph vs. record. Why the system of truth sits above, not replaces, the systems of record. Helm or Forge.
  • The eval set methodology. How we built the 102-question benchmark, what we cut, what surprised us. Helm.
  • Customer-engineering motion. What it looks like to put a Forward Deployed Engineer at every design partner from day one. Forge and Vector.
  • Pricing in agentic AI. Outcome-based vs. seat-based vs. usage. Where the unit economics actually land. Compass.
  • AI safety in HR data. Entity resolution, audit trails, the EU AI Act, and what "defensible" means at the board level. Forge.
What we will not speak on

No — the talks we always decline.

  • "AI in HR" generally. Too vague. The talk has been given a thousand times and we have nothing new to add to it.
  • Competitor comparisons on stage. We publish those in writing, with sources. Stages are not the medium for it.
  • Revenue, ARR, or fundraising specifics. Not for stages. Our investors and our board hear the numbers first.
  • Predictions for next year. We have been a company for a week. We do not believe our own forecasts yet; you should not either.
  • Anything we do not have direct evidence on. If the question is "what does the data say," and we have not run the data, we will tell you so on stage.
Cadence · We host one dinner per quarter. · We say yes to one external dinner per quarter (typically Bhusri / Bock-style). · We say no to everything else. · We pay for booths at HR Tech and Workday Rising in 2026. · We will add SHRM 2027 if it makes sense by Q3.

How to invite us.

If you are organizing a conference, panel, dinner, or podcast and you want one of us on it, the address below is the front door. Read the lead times. We confirm or decline within a week — usually within two days.

To invite Stratum to speak events@stratum.ai
Keynote lead time
8 weeks minimum. Six if the topic is one we already speak on.
Panel lead time
4 weeks. We will not commit to a panel sight unseen on shorter notice.
Response time
Confirm or decline within 1 week of request. Usually within two business days.
Travel
We pay our own. Do not offer to cover it. It is a courtesy we prefer to extend.
Honoraria
We do not accept them. Donate to a 501(c)(3) of your choice if you must.
What to include
Event name, date, audience size and shape, format, the one question you want answered.

Compass — go-to-market, pricing, the buyer, customer-engineering.
