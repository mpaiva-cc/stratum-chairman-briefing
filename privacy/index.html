Privacy Policy.

What information we collect, how we use it, and what we will not do with it.

Effective · T+0d (founding) Last revised · T+115d Next review · T+205d Owner · Forge, CTO
Plain-language summary

The five things you most need to know.

  1. We do not use customer data to train AI models. Ever. Our contracts with model providers prohibit retention of customer data that flows through the agent. We keep audit logs that prove it.
  2. We do not sell or share customer data with third parties for marketing. Not now, not later, not after a funding event, not after an acquisition. The prohibition is a contractual term, not a policy preference.
  3. You can request a copy or deletion of your data at any time. Service-level objective: thirty (30) days to fulfilment, expedited for regulatory or security emergencies. Email dpr@stratum.ai.
  4. We are a data processor; the customer is the data controller. Their consent rules govern; we follow. If you are an employee whose employer uses Stratum, address rights requests to your employer first; we will assist them in fulfilling them.
  5. SOC 2 Type II in audit; ISO 27001 in 2027; GDPR-compliant today. EU SCCs in place; DPF certification active. Data Protection Officer designated. Subprocessors named and DPAs published.
The summary is not a substitute for the policy itself. It is, however, accurate.

The legal entity behind this document, where to reach it, and who is accountable for what it says.

§ 1.4 Scope of this policy

This policy applies to:

  • the Stratum production platform (the Console, the agent, the people-graph store, the API);
  • the public marketing site at stratum.ai and its sub-domains;
  • data flowing into Stratum from customer-authorised connectors (HRIS, ATS, payroll, identity providers);

§ 2 · Categories of data

What we collect.

Three categories, separated by who owns them. Read this section first if you only read one — most downstream questions resolve here.

§ 2.A Customer-controller data

The largest, most sensitive category. The customer is the controller; Stratum is the processor.

When our customers grant Stratum access to their HRIS, ATS, payroll, learning-management, identity, performance, and adjacent systems, we ingest the personal data those systems hold about the customer's workforce. Typical categories include:

  • Identity — names, employee IDs, dates of hire, dates of termination, work email, work phone, manager, organisational unit, location.
  • Employment — job titles, role codes, department, cost centre, employment type (FT/PT/contract), grade or level, reporting line, work-authorization status (where the employer holds it).
  • Compensation — base pay, variable pay, equity grants, bonus history, pay grade, salary band, compa-ratio, total compensation, pay frequency, currency.
  • Performance — ratings, review cycles, calibration outcomes, promotion history, performance-improvement-plan status, goals, OKRs.
  • Demographic self-ID — only where the customer collects it and only with the lawful basis the customer has established (e.g., for EEO-1 / pay-equity reporting in the US; for diversity reporting in the UK; under explicit consent or workplace law elsewhere).
  • Learning & development — courses, certifications, training-completion records.
  • Recruiting (where the customer uses Stratum on the ATS side) — candidate names, contact details, résumés, recruiter notes, interview feedback, offer status.
  • Time, attendance, leave — timesheets, leave balances, leave history (where the customer's HRIS exposes them).
Special categories (GDPR Art. 9) Some of the above can include special categories of personal data — for example, demographic self-ID may include race or ethnicity, leave records may reveal health information, and so on. Stratum processes special-category data only on the customer-controller's documented instructions and only where the customer has established a valid Article 9(2) lawful basis. If you are an end-user or regulator and need to know whether your specific data falls in this bucket, contact your employer's HR or data-protection function; Stratum cannot disclose customer-specific processing details to a third party without the customer's authorisation.

§ 2.B Stratum-direct data

A smaller, lower-sensitivity category. Stratum is the controller.

When you interact with Stratum surfaces directly — our public site, the Console, our customer-facing tools, our sales motion — we collect:

  • Visit data — IP address (truncated to /24 within 24h), user-agent string, referrer URL, requested URL, response code, timestamp.
  • Behaviour data — page views, time-on-page, scroll depth, search queries inside the Console (search terms are persisted in audit logs for compliance reasons; they are not used for advertising and are not sold).
  • Contact data — name, email address, company, role, and free-text fields you submit through forms (request a demo, contact sales, talk to security, apply for a job).
  • Account data — your Stratum account email, hashed password (we use Argon2id; we never see your plaintext password), MFA enrolment status, last-login timestamp, account-role assignments.
  • BYOK key material — if you connect via Bring-Your-Own-Key for a model provider, the API key is stored in your browser's local-storage (origin-bound, HTTPS only) and is not transmitted to Stratum servers. You can verify this in the Network tab of your browser's developer tools.
  • Support data — emails you send us, transcripts of support chats, screenshots you attach.

§ 2.C Service-account data

A narrow technical category. No personal data; system-health only.

When customers grant Stratum API access via service accounts, we record connection telemetry: successful and failed sync timestamps, error codes returned by the source system, payload sizes, sync durations, the row counts touched by each sync, and the latency of write-back operations. This telemetry exists so we can prove freshness, diagnose failures, and answer the question "did Stratum just write to our HRIS, and what did it write?" in seconds rather than days. It contains no employee-level personal data — only system identifiers (e.g., Workday tenant ID, BambooHR subdomain) and aggregate counts.

§ 3 · Purposes of processing

How we use it.

Five purposes, named explicitly. Anything not on this list is not a purpose for which we process your data.

§ 3.1 To operate the Stratum platform

We ingest data from authorised customer source systems, resolve entities (one employee record from many feeds), persist the resolved graph, query it on the customer's behalf, and write changes back to source systems when the customer instructs us to. This is the core product. Without this processing, the platform does not function.

Lawful basis (GDPR): performance of a contract with the customer (Art. 6(1)(b)) for §2.B Stratum-direct data; on documented instructions from the customer-controller (Art. 28) for §2.A customer data.

§ 3.2 To answer the customer's questions

Stratum's distinguishing feature is an agent that answers natural-language questions ("show me everyone in Engineering whose manager skipped 1:1s last week," "who is at risk of attrition in EMEA sales," "which open roles are unfilled past their target close date") against the customer's data. Producing those answers requires fetching the rows that satisfy the query, computing the requested aggregate or judgement, and returning a result with citations.

Every such answer is recorded in an audit log accessible to the customer: who asked, what they asked, which rows were retrieved, and what was returned. The customer can export this log at any time.

§ 3.3 To maintain service

We monitor system health, generate alerts, respond to incidents, perform routine maintenance, take backups, and validate restore procedures. This requires touching customer data in narrow, documented ways. Production access by Stratum personnel is logged, requires a break-glass justification, expires automatically, and is reviewed quarterly. See stratum.ai/security § SEC-1 for the control details.

§ 3.4 To improve our product

We use anonymised, aggregate system signals — query latencies, error rates, the distribution of feature usage by tier — to improve the product. We do not use customer data, customer queries, or any individual record to train, fine-tune, or evaluate AI models. Methodology refinements (better entity-resolution heuristics, better citation pruning, better policy enforcement) are developed on synthetic data and validated on anonymised aggregates.

This is the single most important sentence in the document, and it is repeated under §9: customer data never enters model training, fine-tuning, evaluation, or any other AI-development pipeline.

§ 3.5 To comply with the law

We will respond to lawful process — subpoenas, court orders, regulatory inquiries, national-security letters where we have no choice but to answer — and we will provide what the process compels us to provide and no more. Where the law permits, we will notify the affected customer before disclosure so they may seek a protective order. Where the law forbids notice (a non-disclosure order, a gag order), we will record the fact of the order in our transparency log without revealing its contents, and we will fight to publish the existence of the request at the earliest legal opportunity. Stratum maintains a warrant-canary statement at stratum.ai/security § canary.

What we will not do We will not process your data for behavioural advertising. We will not sell your data to a data broker. We will not use it to score you for any purpose other than the one the customer has commissioned. We will not silently expand the purposes for which we process it; any material change to this list of purposes is a material change to this policy and triggers the §12 notice procedure.

§ 5 · Data residency

Where it's stored.

Default region today, alternatives on the roadmap, and what we will do for customers with binding data-residency obligations.

By default, all Stratum data is stored and processed in the United States, in AWS regions us-east-1 (Northern Virginia, primary) and us-west-2 (Oregon, disaster recovery). Snapshots are replicated cross-region within 15 minutes; recovery-point objective is 15 minutes, recovery-time objective is 4 hours.

§ 5.1 EU residency

A dedicated EU tenant in AWS eu-central-1 (Frankfurt) is planned for general availability at approximately T+190d (mid-2027). EU customers will be able to opt in at sign-up or migrate on request; migration is a one-way, three-week operation we coordinate with the customer. EU-tenant data is not replicated to the US for any purpose — administrative or otherwise. Inference for EU-tenant queries is routed to EU-resident model endpoints (see §9.1).

§ 5.2 Other jurisdictions

For customers whose employees fall under data-residency laws that require storage inside a specific jurisdiction (Russia's Federal Law No. 242-FZ, China's PIPL, Saudi Arabia's PDPL where the customer holds employee records inside KSA, etc.), Stratum offers two options:

  1. Field-level exclusion — the customer designates fields or employee populations that may not leave the source jurisdiction. Stratum honours the exclusion at the connector layer; the data is never ingested. Queries that would require excluded data return a "data not available in this tenant" notice with a pointer to the local system.
  2. Local edge tenant — for paying customers with sufficient volume, Stratum deploys an inference and storage edge in the required region, federated to the customer's primary tenant. This is bespoke and priced as such.

§ 5.3 Backups

Operational backups (point-in-time recovery): 7 days, encrypted at rest with customer-distinct keys, stored in the same region as the primary tenant. Quarterly snapshots: 12 months, encrypted at rest, stored in a cold-storage tier in the same region. Deletion requests (§6.5) apply to backups via cryptographic erasure of the per-tenant data-encryption key, completing within 30 days of the original deletion request.

§ 6 · Retention & deletion

How long we keep it.

Different data has different lifespans. Default rule: we keep it for as long as we need it for the purpose for which it was collected, and not longer.

§ 6.1 Customer data (§2.A)

For the duration of the customer contract, plus a 90-day post-termination grace period during which the customer may export their data in standard formats (Parquet for analytical exports, JSON for object exports, CSV for the dimensionally-impoverished). After day 91, the data is hard-deleted across primary stores, replicas, and search indices; cryptographic erasure of the tenant DEK is applied to backups and is complete within an additional 30 days. A deletion certificate is issued to the customer.

§ 6.2 Derived analytics & aggregates

Anonymised aggregate metrics computed from customer data (e.g., median pay-band drift across the customer base, query-frequency distributions, latency percentiles) are retained beyond the customer-contract term for product-improvement and benchmarking purposes. No individual records are retained; the aggregates are k-anonymous with k ≥ 50 and pass a re-identification audit before they leave the tenant boundary.

§ 6.3 System & audit logs

Application logs, audit logs, and security logs are retained for 18 months for incident-response, compliance, and forensic purposes. Logs are scrubbed of payloads (PII) at ingest; only metadata persists by default. Logs older than 18 months are deleted unless they have been preserved under a litigation hold (preserved logs are not used for any other purpose; release on hold expiry).

§ 6.4 Backups

See §5.3. In short: 7-day operational, 12-month quarterly snapshot, encrypted at rest with per-tenant keys; deletion requests propagate via cryptographic erasure of those keys.

§ 6.5 On deletion request

A controller-issued deletion request (or, where Stratum is the controller, a data-subject deletion request under §7.3) is honoured within 30 days across all primary stores, replicas, search indices, and derived embeddings. Backups are reconciled within a further 30 days via cryptographic erasure of the per-tenant DEK. A deletion certificate, naming the data scope and timestamping completion, is issued at the conclusion.

Embeddings & derived data Vector embeddings, search indices, cached features, and other derived representations of personal data are personal data when they can be associated with an identifiable person. Stratum treats them as such: deletion requests apply to derived data on the same SLA as the source, and our deletion script enumerates every derived store explicitly. There is no "but the embeddings" loophole at Stratum.

§ 6.6 Statutory holds & litigation holds

Where law requires us to preserve data beyond the periods above (regulatory hold, subpoena, court order, ongoing investigation), we preserve it for the duration of the obligation and no longer. The fact and scope of the hold are recorded in the audit log; the affected customer is notified where the law permits. Data on hold is not used for any purpose other than the obligation that gave rise to it.

§ 7 · Data-subject rights

Your rights.

What you can ask us to do, how to ask, and how long it takes.

The rights below apply to natural persons whose personal data is processed by Stratum. Where Stratum is the processor (§2.A), data-subject rights are typically exercised through the controller (your employer); we will assist them in fulfilling those requests on their stated SLA. Where Stratum is the controller (§2.B), the rights are exercised directly with us at dpr@stratum.ai.

Access (Art. 15)
Request a copy of the personal data Stratum holds about you, the purposes for which we process it, the recipients with whom we share it, the retention period, the lawful basis. We provide the copy in a structured, commonly-used, machine-readable format (JSON by default; CSV on request).
Rectification (Art. 16)
Correct inaccurate or incomplete data. Where Stratum is the processor, we route the correction to the customer-controller's source system and confirm propagation; we do not silently overwrite data the customer is authoritative for.
Erasure (Art. 17)
Request deletion of your data, subject to the limits in Art. 17(3) (compliance with legal obligations, defense of legal claims, etc.). Where Stratum is the processor, your request is forwarded to the controller; we will not unilaterally delete customer data without the controller's instruction.
Restriction (Art. 18)
Require us to pause processing while a dispute about accuracy, lawfulness, or necessity is resolved. We mark the affected records as restricted and exclude them from new processing until the dispute is settled.
Portability (Art. 20)
Receive your data in a structured, commonly-used, machine-readable format, or have it transmitted directly to another controller where technically feasible. We use JSON for the export and provide a documented schema.
Objection (Art. 21)
Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct-marketing purposes. We have no direct-marketing processing of customer-employee data; for other objections we weigh your rights against the legitimate interest and respond in writing with a reasoned outcome.
Automated decisions (Art. 22)
You have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing that produces legal or similarly significant effects. Stratum's decision-class enforcement (§9.3) is built precisely to ensure this — any agent decision that affects an individual employee requires human approval, enforced server-side.
Complaint (Art. 77)
Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. EU residents may complain to the supervisory authority of their habitual residence, place of work, or place of the alleged infringement. We will not retaliate, deny service, or otherwise penalise you for exercising this right.
CCPA (California residents)
Right to know, right to delete, right to correct, right to portability, right to limit use of sensitive personal information, right to non-discrimination. We do not "sell" or "share" (CCPA defined), so the opt-out is not applicable, but you may exercise every other right.

§ 7.1 How to exercise a right

Email dpr@stratum.ai from the address Stratum holds for you, or — if you reach us from a different address — be prepared to provide identifying information sufficient for us to authenticate the request without exposing data to an impostor. We acknowledge inside 24 hours and respond substantively within 30 days. We may extend the response window by a further 60 days where the request is complex or where the volume of requests is high, and we will tell you, in writing, if we do.

§ 7.2 Authenticating you

For customer-controller data, we will route the request to the customer's authorised contact and assist them; this is the most common path. For Stratum-direct data, we authenticate via the email address on file, an SSO step-up where the account uses SSO, or a documented identity-verification exchange for high-sensitivity requests. We do not require disclosure of more identity information than necessary to authenticate.

§ 7.3 Fees & refusals

First request in a 12-month rolling window: free. Manifestly unfounded or repetitive requests: we may charge a reasonable administrative fee or refuse, with reasons. Refusals are documented and include a pointer to the right of appeal and to the supervisory authority.

§ 7.4 Emergency expedite

If a delay in responding would create a credible risk to a person's safety (e.g., a known stalker accessing an employer's directory; a leaked offer letter prompting harassment), we expedite to 72 hours. Flag the request as urgent in the subject line and explain the risk briefly; we triage on receipt.

§ 8 · International transfers

Across borders.

The legal scaffolding for moving data between jurisdictions. Boring, but it has to be right.

§ 8.1 Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)

For transfers of personal data from the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland to the United States or any other third country not benefiting from an adequacy decision, Stratum relies on the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses (Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914), incorporated by reference into Stratum's Data Processing Agreement. The UK Addendum to the EU SCCs is incorporated for UK transfers; the Swiss FDPIC's addendum for Swiss transfers. Module 2 (controller → processor) is the default; Module 3 applies where the customer engages Stratum as a sub-processor to its own controller.

§ 8.2 EU–US Data Privacy Framework

Stratum is certified under the EU–US Data Privacy Framework (DPF) and the UK Extension and Swiss–US DPF, as administered by the U.S. Department of Commerce. Certification covers human-resources and non-human-resources data received from the EEA, the UK, and Switzerland in reliance on the relevant Framework. Stratum's public DPF entry is listed at www.dataprivacyframework.gov. Complaints about DPF compliance may be addressed to dpf@stratum.ai; an independent recourse mechanism is provided at no cost to the complainant; and binding arbitration is available as a last resort under Annex I of the Framework.

§ 8.3 Adequacy decisions

Where the European Commission has adopted an adequacy decision covering the destination of a transfer (Andorra, Argentina, Canada commercial, Faroe Islands, Guernsey, Isle of Man, Israel, Japan, Jersey, New Zealand, Republic of Korea, Switzerland, the United Kingdom, Uruguay), Stratum relies on that decision and dispenses with SCCs as the transfer mechanism for the covered scope.

§ 8.4 Transfer impact assessments (Schrems II)

For each transfer to a third country, Stratum performs a transfer impact assessment per the EDPB's recommendations on supplementary measures (06/2020). The TIA evaluates the laws of the destination country, the practical access regimes (notably FISA §702 and EO 12333 for U.S. transfers), and Stratum's supplementary measures (encryption in transit and at rest with customer-distinct keys; pseudonymisation in transit where feasible; legal review of every government request; transparency report). Findings are documented and provided to customers on request. Data flows are mapped per requirement; the map is updated quarterly.

FISA §702 & what we can promise Stratum operates in the United States and is therefore potentially subject to FISA §702 directives addressed to "electronic communication service providers." Our position: (i) we have never received a §702 directive; (ii) if we did, we would challenge it to the extent the statute permits; (iii) the warrant canary at stratum.ai/security § canary is reaffirmed in each quarterly transparency report and will be allowed to lapse rather than be falsely reaffirmed; (iv) all customer data at rest is encrypted with customer-distinct keys, with the keys controlled by Stratum's KMS such that compelled disclosure of ciphertext alone is of limited use.
§ 9 · AI-specific practices

When the processor is an agent.

Stratum is an AI-native HCM company. That fact creates obligations over and above the generic processor playbook. This section enumerates them.

§ 9.1 No training on customer data

Stratum has a contractual prohibition with Anthropic, our default model provider, against retention of customer data flowing through the agent (zero-retention API endpoints; data not used for training or fine-tuning). The contractual term is reciprocally enforceable: Anthropic warrants it, and we warrant it to our customers.

Equivalent terms are in place with every other model provider we use today (none currently in production beyond Anthropic; any addition is treated as a new subprocessor under §4.2). Audit logs on the Stratum side and access logs on the model-provider side are cross-checked quarterly by the CTO; results are reported in the transparency log.

BYOK ("Bring Your Own Key") customers route inference through their own model-provider account; in that mode, the contractual posture is between the customer and the model provider, and Stratum sees only the prompt and response in transit (subject to the customer's encryption-in-transit settings).

§ 9.2 No cross-tenant data flow

Each customer is logically and cryptographically isolated. The isolation is enforced at three layers:

  1. Storage layer — every row is tagged with a tenant ID; every query carries a tenant context derived from authenticated session; the row-level security policy rejects any query whose context does not match.
  2. Encryption layer — each tenant has a distinct data-encryption key managed by AWS KMS; a query that somehow bypassed RLS would return ciphertext that cannot be decrypted with another tenant's key.
  3. Agent layer — the agent operates in a per-tenant execution context; it has no access to a shared "world model" of customer data; retrieval is scoped to the tenant at the embedding-store level, not just at the prompt level.

A red-team finding of cross-tenant data exposure would be a Severity-1 security incident, would be disclosed under §11.1 of Stratum's customer security agreement, and would, in our view, constitute a material breach of this policy. To date: zero such findings. See stratum.ai/security § SEC-3 for the architectural details.

§ 9.3 Decision-class enforcement

Stratum classifies every agent operation by its consequence to an individual employee:

  • Read-only — query, report, summary. Agent-autonomous.
  • Write-soft — edits to descriptive fields, scheduling, draft creation. Agent-autonomous with audit trail.
  • Decision-class — anything that affects an employee's status, pay, role, performance record, or employment. Server-side gate; human approval required; no prompt-level override.

"Server-side" is the load-bearing word. The gate is enforced in Stratum's policy engine, not in the agent's instructions. Even an operator with full agent control cannot bypass it; the gate is a property of the platform, not of the prompt. This is how we satisfy Article 22 GDPR and the EU AI Act's prohibition on solely automated decisions with legal or similarly significant effects.

§ 9.4 Provenance & citation

Every answer the agent returns is accompanied by a citation pack identifying which rows of data contributed to the answer, the queries that retrieved them, and the policies that authorised the retrieval. The citation pack is customer-inspectable in the Console and exportable via the audit API. An answer without a citation pack is an error and is suppressed.

§ 9.5 Right to know if your data went to an agent

Yes. Stratum publishes — to the customer-controller — an audit log of every agent interaction with their data: who asked, what was asked, which rows were retrieved, what was returned, and which policies fired. If you are an employee of a Stratum customer and you want to know whether your data has been read by the agent and in what context, ask your employer; they have the log and the contractual right to share it with you.

§ 9.6 Model versioning & provenance

Every agent response carries the model identifier and version that produced it. Stratum maintains an internal model-card for each production model, including known evaluation results, known failure modes, and the rollback target. Customers may pin to a model version for a contractually-bounded period (typically 12 months) to satisfy their own governance requirements.

§ 9.7 EU AI Act stance

Stratum is monitoring the implementation of Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (the AI Act). Our internal class-assessment treats workforce-decision automation as high-risk AI under Annex III(4), and our controls are being aligned with the Title III obligations (risk-management system, data governance, technical documentation, record-keeping, transparency, human oversight, accuracy and robustness, post-market monitoring) ahead of the August 2026 high-risk-systems deadline. See stratum.ai/ai for the AI-governance surface.

§ 10.1 Do Not Track & Global Privacy Control

Stratum honours the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal as a valid opt-out under §1798.135(b)(1)(B) CCPA, though as noted in §4.5 we do not "sell" or "share" personal information, so the GPC signal has no behavioural effect on Stratum. We do not set or respect Do Not Track separately; the W3C track of the standard was withdrawn in 2019.

§ 10.2 Consent banner

Visitors from EEA, UK, and Swiss IPs are presented with a cookie banner on first visit to the marketing site. The banner offers "Accept all," "Reject all," and granular toggles; the default is "Reject all" (essential only). Console users do not see the banner because they are an authenticated party with whom we have a service contract; consent is documented in the customer's MSA / DPA rather than at the cookie layer.

§ 11 · Children

Not for minors.

Stratum is a workforce platform. Workforces are adults.

The Stratum platform is not directed to children under 18, and we do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 18 in the conduct of our business. The product is sold to enterprises; the data subjects are their employees and candidates, who are by definition working-age adults.

If we become aware that we have collected personal data from a person under 18 — for example, through an inadvertent ATS integration that includes minors in a customer's source system — we will delete that data within 7 days of becoming aware and notify the customer-controller in writing. If you believe Stratum holds data about a minor, contact privacy@stratum.ai; we will act on the report.

For COPPA-eligible jurisdictions: Stratum does not target the service at children under 13; we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13; the procedures above apply with the lower threshold.

§ 12 · Changes to this policy

When this document changes.

How we revise it, how we tell you, and where to read the diff.

§ 12.1 Material vs. non-material changes

A material change is any change that (i) expands the categories of data we collect, (ii) expands the purposes for which we use data, (iii) adds a subprocessor, (iv) changes data residency for an existing tenant, (v) reduces data-subject rights, or (vi) changes the legal basis for processing. Anything else is a non-material change (clarification, typo, link update, role rename).

§ 12.2 Notice procedure

Material changes are emailed to affected customers 30 days in advance of the effective date, via the canonical privacy-contact address on file. Non-material changes are published silently; the version number increments and the changelog at the bottom of this page records the change.

During the 30-day notice period for a material change, customers may object. Objection triggers a good-faith negotiation; if the objection cannot be resolved, the customer may terminate the affected portion of service without penalty and export their data under §6.1.

§ 12.3 Versioning

Each issuance of this policy has a version (e.g., v1.4), an effective date, and a document ID (PRIV-2026-001). The previous five versions are archived at stratum.ai/changelog § privacy and remain accessible indefinitely.

§ 12.4 Conflict-of-laws

Where a change to this policy would, if applied, place Stratum in breach of a law applicable to a given customer's data (e.g., a new statute in the customer's jurisdiction), the conflicting provision does not take effect for that customer's data until the conflict is resolved. We will tell you, in writing, when we observe such a conflict.
