§ 01 · The numbers, individually

Every metric, fully shown — six panels, raw rows.

Each panel below holds one operating story: the headline, a brief plain-English explanation, a chart drawn from the same six fortnightly snapshots that drive the Chairman's Briefing, and a mono table of the raw rows. If you see a number you can cite anywhere in our communications, it is in one of these six panels.

A Cash & runway

Cash on hand is the balance in our operating accounts at the end of each fortnight. Burn is the trailing-30-day net cash outflow. Runway is cash divided by burn, rounded down. We do not net out projected revenue when we report runway — runway here is the conservative answer to how many months can we survive if no customer ever pays us again.

§ 02 · Publication ethics

Why we publish, and what we will not.

Open metrics are only credible if you can also see the boundaries of the openness. This is the editorial policy that governs this page.

Editorial policy · v1.0 · 2026-05-13

We publish these numbers because the alternative is asking our customers, candidates, and the press to trust us on the basis of nothing. Every company says it is doing well. Most can't prove it. We would rather show our work — including the parts that make us look weaker than the version on the website — than ask anyone to take our word for it.

We do not adjust numbers retroactively. If we miscount, we publish the correction alongside the original. Past snapshots on this page are exactly the numbers we published on the day of the corresponding Chairman's Briefing — even where we have since refined our methodology. Methodology changes are noted in the source note of the affected metric.

Numbers we do not publish here: individual employee salaries, customer names without consent, prospective customer pipelines by name, anything subject to NDA, headcount demographics at n < 25 (small-sample privacy), individual founder confidence scores, vendor contracts, and per-customer ACV. We name the categories we withhold; we do not simply leave a hole.

Frequency. Every fortnight, at the moment of the Chairman's Briefing. Some metrics update faster: uptime, incidents, and pen-test status are pulled live from /status; eval accuracy refreshes on each Stratum release. The "Last refresh" timestamp at the top of this page is the most recent fortnight cutover — individual line items may be more recent.

Audit trail. An archive of every fortnight's snapshot is mirrored in the public Ledger and signed with a git-style hash, so external observers can verify that what we publish today matches what we published two months ago. We expect a corrected snapshot, on average, every other quarter — and when one happens we will say so on this page, in this color, with this prominence.
