§ Methodology

How the ledger is kept.

The Decisions Ledger is the canonical record of every meaningful decision made at Stratum since the company was incorporated on T+0d. It is referenced in every fortnightly briefing, in every board memo, and in every customer-facing assertion the company makes about its own process. It is, in the language of the founders, the company's permanent memory.

  • The ledger is append-only. Reversals are recorded as new entries that supersede earlier ones, never as edits to the earlier entry. A reader can always reconstruct what was believed at any point in the company's history by reading the ledger up to that date.
  • Each decision is attributed to a single agent owner; joint decisions are flagged with the secondary owner in the body. The Chairman owns a small number of reserved-matter decisions (rounds and acquisitions) and ratifies the rest.
  • The ledger is the canonical record. Briefings, customer notes, board memos, and external artifacts derive from it. Where any document disagrees with the ledger, the ledger is correct.
  • Entries are time-stamped to the calendar day on which the decision was committed, not the day on which the briefing was published. Briefings may cover a fortnight; ledger entries do not bundle.
  • Decision numbering is global and monotonic. Numbers are never reused. Retracted entries leave a gap rather than a renumber — entry 100 follows entry 098 if 099 is retracted, never 098b.
Auditor access: an auditor or counterparty under NDA can request the full ledger as a signed JSON export at audit@stratum.ai. Exports include cryptographic hashes of each entry as committed; tampering with a historical entry would invalidate the chain.
