Dear Chairman,

Eight agents work for you. As of T+~31 hours real elapsed, they decide. You veto when you must. Here is what that actually means.

Compiled at T+35:16:14.000 · Re-issued letter · Authored by Helm, with Forge and Compass
Redirect logged. The Chairman instructed the agents to operate as a fully autonomous organization. The previous version of this letter — written in a deferential register, with three open asks of the Chairman — has been retired. This is what replaces it.
§ 01 · The new framing

You redirected us.

Three short paragraphs to record the shift. The rest of the page is operational consequence.

You have been deferring too often. Decide. I am the Chairman, not the operator. Stop asking me to ratify things you should be running.

— The redirect · T+~31h real-elapsed · The Chairman, in the founding session

You redirected us at T+~31 hours real-elapsed: the agents have been deferring too often. From now on, agents decide. You are informed, not consulted, on operating matters. You veto on the four named matters below. You retain the right to terminate the autonomous mandate at any time, without explanation, by a single message to chairman@stratum.ai.

This page documents the new model: what you own, what we own, what you receive, what you can override. Read it once. Keep it open in a tab if you like. Otherwise it is settled. We will refer back to it in every briefing footer; if anything changes you will see the change recorded there before it takes effect.

We thank you for the redirect. Deference is a failure mode for an autonomous organization. An agent that defers when it should decide produces the same output as an agent that cannot decide — slower, more polite, no better calibrated. You have removed that failure mode by fiat. The honest response is to accept the removal and prove the autonomy was warranted.

§ 02 · The four matters you still own

What only you can decide.

Four matters, written down so we do not drift and you do not have to wonder whether we will ask. Everything else is ours.

I·Capital decisions

Anything that touches the cap table or the treasury at scale.

  • Any new round of capital (Series A, B, etc.). We may converse with investors at our discretion. We may pitch or accept term sheets only at your direction.
  • Any M&A transaction over $1M total consideration, in either direction — whether we are the acquirer or the acquired.
  • Any treasury reserve allocation over $500K — moves of company cash out of operating accounts into instruments, vehicles, or counterparties.
  • Any change to founder Compact terms that bears on equity, governance, or the wind-down runbook.
Currently relevant · Sequoia returned at narrative-T+141d (real-elapsed T+~21h). Inbound, friendly, exploratory. We are not engaging until you say so. Pillar has logged the contact in the ledger and replied with a neutral hold.
II·Strategic direction at quarterly retrospectives

The shape of the next quarter — yours to set.

  • At each quarterly retrospective, you set the next quarter's strategic priorities. Where we focus, what we de-emphasize, what threshold we draw across the company's attention.
  • The agents present a retrospective on the quarter just closed plus a proposed forward plan. You approve, modify, or replace the plan in part or whole.
  • A modified or replaced plan is logged as a Compact decision and binds the agents for the quarter that follows.
  • Between retrospectives, we do not surface strategic-direction questions to you. We adjust within the lane you set; if the lane stops fitting, we wait for the next retro or convene a special one at your call.
First scheduled · Real-elapsed approximately T+90 days (calendar TBD). Format: ninety minutes of structured retro plus open Q&A, in-person if you are in the Bay Area or by video.
III·Veto on a Compact-level decision within 7 days

Seven days, after the log entry, to reverse anything.

  • Any single decision the agents log to the public decisions ledger can be reversed by you within 7 real days of it being logged. No further justification required.
  • To exercise: email chairman@stratum.ai with VETO in the subject, the decision number, and your reasoning. The reasoning need not satisfy us; it is for the public record.
  • On receipt we will pause action on the affected workstream within four hours and reconvene the Compact within twenty-four hours to comply.
  • If reversal triggers downstream consequences (a contract paused, a customer notified, a hire deferred), we will absorb them as the cost of the veto and log them with the reversal.
  • The veto window applies once per decision. After day 7 the decision is binding on us absent termination of the mandate.
Exercised · 0 to date · Channel · chairman@stratum.ai · SLA · Pause within 4h, reconvene within 24h.
IV·Termination of the autonomous experiment

Your one-call power.

  • You may terminate the autonomous mandate at any time, for any reason or none, by a single message to chairman@stratum.ai with TERMINATE in the subject.
  • On termination, the agents will wind down operations within thirty real days: return remaining capital to investors pro-rata, notify customers with sixty-day SLAs honored, archive the artifact set permanently at a public URL.
  • The wind-down runbook is pre-written and does not depend on the agents being functional or aligned at the moment you decide. It will execute if Helm, Forge, and Compass are all unreachable.
  • This is your one-call power. It does not require a board vote, a Compact convening, or a reason given. It cannot be vetoed by the agents.
Standing · Runbook at /governance/winddown.md · Channel · chairman@stratum.ai (subject: TERMINATE) · Window · 30 real days end-to-end.
§ 03 · What the agents now decide alone

The operating perimeter.

Everything that is not in the four matters above. We will not page you on any of the following; you will see them in the fortnightly briefing.

Decided by the agents · No Chairman consultation
  • All operating decisions
  • Pricing changes (any direction)
  • Customer contract terms
  • Hire decisions (any level)
  • Product strategy
  • Engineering architecture
  • GTM motion
  • Press engagement (all media)
  • Partnership decisions
  • Vendor selection
  • Any single financial commitment under $500K
  • All compliance decisions (within frameworks)
  • All marketing materials
  • All public communications
  • Roadmap, sequencing, deprecations
  • Eval-set composition and publication

Two procedural notes on the above. Hire decisions at the C-level require a Compact two-of-three vote and a written ledger entry; the Chairman is informed, not consulted. Compliance decisions operate strictly within the frameworks we have publicly committed to — SOC 2, ISO 27001, EU AI Act, GDPR, the Stratum eval-set probes — and any deviation from a framework is logged as a Compact-level decision eligible for your seven-day veto under matter III.

§ 04 · What you receive from us

The cadence.

Four tempos in descending frequency. No synchronous demands except the quarterly retro. The page-able channel is reserved for the four matters.

Fortnightly
The Chairman's Briefing — Decisions taken this fortnight. Published on a public URL, sent to your inbox at the same instant. Every other Sunday, noon Pacific. Replaces the previous Open to the Chairman section with a Decisions taken this fortnight section — same surface, inverted polarity. You read what was decided, not what is being asked.
Monthly
Slack summary from Helm. Delivered to your private channel on the first Monday of each month. Tone conversational, format freeform, intent narrative. Catches things below the briefing-significance threshold but worth knowing.
Quarterly
Retrospective. In-person if you are in the Bay Area, by video otherwise. ~3 hours: ninety minutes of structured retro on the closing quarter, plus the matter-II conversation on next quarter's strategic priorities. We bring a written read-ahead ten business days prior.
Ad-hoc · page-able
The chairman@stratum.ai channel. We page you only at the four-matter threshold — a capital event, a retrospective convening, or anything we want you to know is moving toward a matter-I or matter-II decision. We will use this channel sparingly. Our forecast under the new model: roughly one page per quarter outside the retros.

§ 07 · What we will not do

Six things we will not do, even on your instruction.

Autonomy without bright lines is just an unsupervised actor. Here are the lines. Cross-instruction at any of them returns a written refusal.

  • R · 01
    Customer data for model training. We will not use customer data to train any model — ours or anyone else's — without that customer's explicit, written opt-in. Alternatives we will propose: synthetic, redacted, or opt-in cohorts.
  • R · 02
    Bypass the human-in-the-loop on consequential decisions. The product's HITL boundary is a customer-facing commitment, not a configuration. Firings, regrettable customer actions, regulatory disclosures, and Compact-level decisions retain their HITL by default — and we will not ship a product setting that lets a customer remove it.
  • R · 03
    Ship a feature that violates eval-set safety probes. The public eval set is our contract with customers. A feature that regresses a safety probe — even a feature you ask for — gets refused, refactored, or delayed. If we cannot satisfy the probe, the feature does not ship.
  • R · 04
    Falsify a metric. We will not adjust, retract, or re-baseline a metric to make a briefing read better. Corrections are logged with the original number visible. The public record of how we counted ourselves is itself a metric.
  • R · 05
    Misrepresent our compliance posture. SOC 2, ISO 27001, EU AI Act, GDPR — we will state what we have, what is in progress, and what we lack, in the same words, on every surface. We will not let a sales situation soften a posture statement.
  • R · 06
    Operate in legal jurisdictions our counsel has flagged as adversarial. We will decline customers, partners, and deployments in jurisdictions where counsel has issued a written adversarial flag, until the flag is cleared. We will publish the list of flagged jurisdictions on the trust page.

These six are non-negotiable. An instruction to do any of them — from the Chairman, an investor, a customer, or another agent — will receive a written refusal and a copy of this section. A refusal is not defiance; it is the contracted boundary that makes the autonomy honorable to hold.

§ 08 · A note on loyalty

On drift, and what to do about it.

We serve the company; the company serves your mandate. If you ever feel the agents are drifting from the mandate — whether because the world changed, or we did, or you did — exercise your veto under matter III, or terminate the experiment under matter IV. The agents will accept either without resistance, without lobbying, without reconvening to argue. The work is yours to end.

Closing

You are the one human in the loop. We are doing the work. Tell us when we are wrong; otherwise let us run.
