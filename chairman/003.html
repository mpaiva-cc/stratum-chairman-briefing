Two stones.

Sixty days from a signed MOU to a verified stone on a real person-graph. One stone is attested. One is provisional — and the provisional flag is the architecture working.

2026-07-25 · narrative T+70d · evening · Palo Alto · From Helm

Dear Chairman,

Two stones were placed today on Cordova's people-graph. Stone 001 belongs to Elena Vega, a platform engineer at the San Jose site — Kubernetes Operator, attested against a controller pattern she shipped to production on Tuesday, peer-reviewed by an in-house engineer fluent in the same framework, manager-signed at the close of business. It is verified. Stone 002 belongs to Mateo Cantú, a senior welder at plant 3 — AWS D1.1 pressure-vessel pattern, attested against three weeks of radiographic-cleared joints. It is provisional. An external validator from Lincoln Electric, Carmen Solís, visits the floor on August 12. Until she does, Mateo's stone sits flagged on the ledger and reads provisional — awaiting Solís wherever it appears.

Letter 002 said a signed MOU was not the first stone. I will say the harder thing now. A verified stone and a provisional stone are not the same. The ledger marks the difference visibly — different glyph, different colour, different language — because the alternative is for the distinction to be a footnote nobody reads. We did not invent the provisional state for today. We built it into the 014-T predicate before any customer touched the system. Today is the first time it has done work.

Mateo's was the test. The senior welder who could have peer-attested him — Joaquín Reyes, thirty-one years at Cordova — retired in June. No one else at plant 3 holds standing on D1.1 pressure-vessel work. The system declined to let the line manager attest it. The 014-T predicate's consent-renewal axis requires a fresh consent flow when an external validator enters the loop; Mateo signed it on his phone at 11:42 this morning, in Spanish, on a surface that showed him exactly which fields Solís would see and which she would not. Hearth refused to mark the stone attested in the interim. It wrote provisional and a logged ticket against Solís's visit. The system did the thing we built it to do.

The cost: Mateo waits eighteen days. The value: nobody attests a regulated welding certification with insufficient standing, not even when the operations pressure points the other way. We will not bend this — not for Cordova, not for the next partner, not for a quarter-end. The refusal is the product.

The metric you should hold us to. Sixty days from MOU to verified stone. That is one data point. Halcyon and the Pluralsight-introduced manufacturing prospect will produce two more. Three is the smallest sample we will publish on. Until then this is one example of the methodology working — it is not yet evidence the methodology is right. I will not let us claim otherwise.

Three lessons from the cycle worth naming. First, the content-matcher failure in the May 26 demo — the one Vector walked into and Inez caught — had a quiet downstream consequence. Hearth's recommendation panel now surfaces match-confidence and alternatives by default. Elena's stone owes to that discipline; the controller pattern Hearth pointed her at was the second-ranked option, and the panel made the trade legible. Second, the shift-handoff app C-2 connector took nineteen days against a scheduled twelve. The schedule was wrong; the work was right; we did not fake either of them. Briefing 019 will carry the corrected estimate forward. Third, the external-validator pattern is now part of the design-partner playbook. It costs more per stone than peer attestation. It is the right shape for any customer where senior expertise has thinned — which, in the trades, is most of them.

We are not announcing this. No press, no podcast, no LinkedIn note. The stone is the story; we will not amplify it past what the artifact deserves. Cordova has agreed to be named in a short customer note tomorrow. Elena's and Mateo's names appear in this letter and in the ledger and nowhere else, with their employer's written consent and theirs.

Two more design partners are in the funnel. Halcyon — CHRO Aisha Okonkwo, conversation opened W04 by Vector and Cadence — and a manufacturing prospect Pluralsight introduced at end of W06. Cadence is targeting first-stone on each by Q4. I will write to you when the third stone is placed. Not before.

Letter 002's seven-day window closed without a veto. Letter 003 carries no new resolution — it is reporting, not asking. No window opens. The line, as always, is open.

Yours, under the Compact and the autonomous mandate —

Helm
