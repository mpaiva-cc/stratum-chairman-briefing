§ 02 · The architecture

Your three ATSes, reconciled. Your pipeline, queryable.

Stratum's people graph extends to include candidates, requisitions, and scorecard data. Every candidate is an entity in the graph. Every interview is a node. Every scorecard is an edge with a score and a recommend signal. Every referral is a relationship between a current employee and an applicant.

Most enterprises run two or three ATS systems by accident: one from an acquisition, one for a region, one for high-volume hourly. Stratum reads all of them, reconciles candidate identity across them, and surfaces a single defensible answer — with every record linked back to the source system it came from.

We are not a system of record. Your ATS stays your system of record. We are the intelligence layer above it.

The unit of value: time-to-defensible-answer. Not records, not pipelines, but answers about your recruiting operation that survive a board-meeting follow-up.

§ 04 · What we read · what we don't

The smaller our footprint in your ATS, the less audit surface for your security team.

Stratum is read-only by default. We never write back without explicit human sign-off. Here is the full list of what we ingest, and the explicit list of what we don't.

What Stratum reads from your ATS

  • Candidate records — with PII; encrypted at rest; access scoped per role.
  • Requisitions — open, closed, on-hold; with hiring manager, recruiter, opened date.
  • Scorecards — interview feedback synthesized into a structured summary; raw text stays in your ATS.
  • Source attribution — referral, agency, inbound, sourced; tied to the candidate record.
  • Comp data — offer amounts, bands, equity grants for each closed offer.
  • Demographic self-ID — when provided and consented; used only for compliance reporting.
  • Stage transitions — every move in the pipeline with timestamp and actor.
  • Read-only by default — write-back is a separately-gated permission.

What Stratum does not read or store

  • Interview video / audio recordings — never ingested. Live in Zoom / your interview-intelligence vendor.
  • Slack discussion threads — only if you explicitly connect Slack and scope the channels; otherwise invisible to us.
  • Resume PDFs (raw) — we read the structured fields parsed by your ATS; the source PDF stays in your ATS bucket.
  • Reference contact info — phone numbers and personal emails of references are never copied out.
  • Free-text notes flagged "private" — recruiter private notes marked private in your ATS are excluded by default.
  • Candidate-side login data — passwords, sessions, browser fingerprints from the careers site.
  • Payroll & benefits — handled by the HRIS connector, not the ATS connector.
  • Anything outside the requisition graph — if it doesn't help answer a question, we don't ingest it.
The rule: if we don't need it to answer your question, we don't ingest it. Every connector you turn on gets a written data scope that your security team signs. Every field we read appears in our sub-processor & data-flow appendix to the DPA.

Sixty days from kickoff to your first predicted-acceptance model.

One workshop with VP TA + two senior recruiters. We catalogue the three to five questions your CHRO gets asked most. Output: a 1-page scope document with the connectors we'll wire first.
