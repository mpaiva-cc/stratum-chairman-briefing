How to read this page

We split the roadmap into four tracks plus a link out. The further from today, the less specific we get — because the further out we are, the less honestly we can promise a date. A roadmap that pretends to know Q3 2027 in May 2026 is a roadmap that has decided to be wrong on purpose. We would rather be vague and right than precise and lying.

  • Shipping nowItems currently being engineered. Expected within ~6 weeks. Dates are firm to within a sprint.
  • Up nextItems in design. Expected Q3–Q4 2026. Quarter-level confidence; specific weeks subject to change.
  • On the horizonItems in concept. Expected 2027. Half-year buckets only; we will narrow as we get closer.
  • Not now (and why)Items we are deliberately not building. With reasoning, because that is the part most roadmaps hide.
  • DoneAlready shipped. We keep that history in the Stratum changelog — eleven internal builds across Foundation, Loam, and Marble.

§ 04 · Not now (and why)

Not now — and why

The most important section on this page. The work we are deliberately not building yet, with one-sentence reasoning. A roadmap that only says "yes" is a roadmap with no spine.

Not now

A full HRIS replacement

We are the system of truth above your HRIS, not a competitor to it. If you want to replace Workday or BambooHR, you don't want Stratum.

Not now

Payroll processing

ADP, Gusto, Workday Payroll, and fourteen country-specific systems do this well. We integrate; we don't compete.

Not now

An applicant-side careers portal

Greenhouse, Ashby, and Workday Recruiting offer good candidate-facing flows. We are the intelligence layer above them; we don't displace them.

Not now

An L&D or learning management system

Cornerstone, LinkedIn Learning, and Docebo serve this. We surface learning data; we don't host courses.

Not now

A general-purpose AI assistant

We are deliberately narrow: CHRO and recruiting questions answered with citations. A general assistant is not a positioning we can defend.

Not now

A version of Stratum for sub-500 EE companies

Our pricing and complexity don't fit until you are running three or more HR systems. Come back to us when you grow into the 1,000+ EE band.
